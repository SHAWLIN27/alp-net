# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This sub-topic explores the buoyant force (upthrust) experienced by objects submerged in fluids. It is centered on [[Archimedes' Principle]], which states that the upthrust on an object is equal to the weight of the fluid it displaces. Understanding this principle is crucial for explaining why objects float, sink, or appear lighter in water. It connects directly to [[Viscous Drag and Stokes' Law]] (both are forces in fluids) and is a prerequisite for understanding [[Terminal Velocity in Fluids]]. This concept builds on the general idea of [[Types of Force]], specifically contact forces from fluid pressure.

**中文:**
本子知识点探讨物体在流体中受到的浮力（上推力）。其核心是[[阿基米德原理]]，该原理指出物体所受的浮力等于其排开的流体的重量。理解这一原理对于解释物体为何会漂浮、下沉或在水中显得更轻至关重要。它与[[粘性阻力和斯托克斯定律]]（两者都是流体中的力）直接相关，也是理解[[流体中的终端速度]]的前提。这一概念建立在[[力的类型]]中关于流体压力产生的接触力的一般概念之上。

# 2. Core Definition / 核心定义

| Term (EN/CN) | Definition (EN) | Definition (CN) |
| ------------ | --------------- | --------------- |
| **Upthrust** / 上推力 | The upward force exerted by a fluid on a body immersed in it, caused by the difference in pressure between the top and bottom of the body. | 流体对浸入其中的物体施加的向上的力，由物体顶部和底部之间的压力差引起。 |
| **Archimedes' Principle** / 阿基米德原理 | A body wholly or partially immersed in a fluid experiences an upthrust equal to the weight of the fluid it displaces. | 完全或部分浸入流体中的物体，会受到一个等于其排开流体重量的上推力。 |
| **Displaced Fluid** / 排开的流体 | The volume of fluid that is pushed aside by an object when it is immersed. | 物体浸入时被排挤开的流体的体积。 |
| **Apparent Weight** / 表观重量 | The weight of an object as measured when it is submerged in a fluid, equal to its true weight minus the upthrust. | 物体浸没在流体中时测量到的重量，等于其真实重量减去上推力。 |
| **Floatation** / 漂浮 | A condition where an object rests on the surface of a fluid, with only part of its volume submerged, such that the upthrust equals its weight. | 物体静止在流体表面，只有部分体积浸没，此时上推力等于其重力的状态。 |

# 3. Key Concepts / 关键概念

**English:**
The origin of upthrust lies in the variation of fluid pressure with depth. As established in [[Forces in Fluids (Upthrust and Viscous Force)]], pressure $P = \rho g h$. The bottom of a submerged object is deeper than its top, so the pressure pushing up on the bottom is greater than the pressure pushing down on the top. This pressure difference results in a net upward force: the upthrust.

Archimedes' Principle provides a simple way to calculate this force. Consider a cylinder of cross-sectional area $A$ and height $h$ fully submerged in a fluid of density $\rho_f$. The pressure difference is $\Delta P = \rho_f g h$. The upthrust is $F_u = \Delta P \times A = \rho_f g h A = \rho_f g V$, where $V$ is the volume of the cylinder. This is exactly the weight of the fluid that would occupy that volume.

**Common Pitfall:** Students often confuse the volume of the object with the volume of fluid displaced. For a fully submerged object, they are equal. For a floating object, the displaced volume is only the submerged part of the object's volume.

**中文:**
上推力的根源在于流体压力随深度的变化。根据[[流体中的力（上推力和粘性力）]]中的知识，压力 $P = \rho g h$。浸没物体的底部比顶部更深，因此底部向上的压力大于顶部向下的压力。这种压力差产生了一个净向上的力：上推力。

阿基米德原理提供了一种计算这个力的简单方法。考虑一个完全浸没在密度为 $\rho_f$ 的流体中、横截面积为 $A$、高度为 $h$ 的圆柱体。压力差为 $\Delta P = \rho_f g h$。上推力为 $F_u = \Delta P \times A = \rho_f g h A = \rho_f g V$，其中 $V$ 是圆柱体的体积。这正好是占据该体积的流体的重量。

**常见错误：** 学生经常混淆物体的体积和排开流体的体积。对于完全浸没的物体，两者相等。对于漂浮的物体，排开的体积只是物体浸没部分的体积。

# 4. Formulas / 公式

The central formula for this sub-topic is:

$$ F_u = \rho_f V_d g $$

Where:
- $F_u$ = Upthrust (N)
- $\rho_f$ = Density of the fluid (kg m⁻³)
- $V_d$ = Volume of fluid displaced (m³)
- $g$ = Acceleration due to gravity (m s⁻²)

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
| ------------- | ------------ | ------------ | ----------- |
| $F_u$ | Upthrust | 上推力 | N (牛顿) |
| $\rho_f$ | Density of fluid | 流体密度 | kg m⁻³ |
| $V_d$ | Displaced volume | 排开体积 | m³ |
| $g$ | Gravitational field strength | 重力场强度 | m s⁻² |

**Derivation / 推导:**
For a fully submerged object of volume $V$:
1. Pressure at top of object: $P_{top} = \rho_f g h_{top}$
2. Pressure at bottom of object: $P_{bottom} = \rho_f g h_{bottom}$
3. Pressure difference: $\Delta P = P_{bottom} - P_{top} = \rho_f g (h_{bottom} - h_{top}) = \rho_f g h$
4. Upthrust = Pressure difference × Area: $F_u = \Delta P \times A = \rho_f g h A = \rho_f g V$
5. Since $V = V_d$ for a fully submerged object, $F_u = \rho_f V_d g$.

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- The fluid must be incompressible (density $\rho_f$ is constant).
- The object must be fully or partially submerged.
- The formula gives the magnitude of the upthrust.

# 6. Worked Example / 典型例题

### Question / 题目
**English:**
A solid metal cube of side 5.0 cm is suspended from a spring balance. The balance reads 10.0 N in air. The cube is then fully immersed in a liquid of density 800 kg m⁻³. Calculate the new reading on the spring balance. (Take $g = 9.81 \text{ m s}^{-2}$)

**中文:**
一个边长为 5.0 cm 的实心金属立方体悬挂在弹簧秤上。在空气中，弹簧秤读数为 10.0 N。然后将该立方体完全浸没在密度为 800 kg m⁻³ 的液体中。计算弹簧秤上的新读数。（取 $g = 9.81 \text{ m s}^{-2}$）

### Solution / 解答

**Step 1: Calculate the volume of the cube.**
$$ V = (0.05 \text{ m})^3 = 1.25 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}^3 $$
Since the cube is fully immersed, the displaced volume $V_d = V = 1.25 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}^3$.

**Step 2: Calculate the upthrust.**
$$ F_u = \rho_f V_d g = (800 \text{ kg m}^{-3})(1.25 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}^3)(9.81 \text{ m s}^{-2}) $$
$$ F_u = 0.981 \text{ N} $$

**Step 3: Calculate the apparent weight.**
The apparent weight is the true weight minus the upthrust.
$$ \text{Apparent Weight} = \text{True Weight} - F_u $$
$$ \text{Apparent Weight} = 10.0 \text{ N} - 0.981 \text{ N} = 9.019 \text{ N} $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** 9.02 N **答案:** 9.02 N

### Quick Tip / 提示
Always convert all lengths to meters before calculating volume. The spring balance reading in a fluid is the apparent weight, not the true weight.
