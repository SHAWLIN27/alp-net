# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
Normal Contact Force (often called the Normal Reaction or just "Normal Force") is the force exerted by a surface on an object resting on it. It acts perpendicular (normal) to the surface. This is one of the most fundamental contact forces in physics, essential for understanding why objects don't fall through tables, how scales measure weight, and how friction arises. Within the broader [[Types of Force]] topic, Normal Contact Force is a core member alongside [[Weight and Gravitational Force]], [[Tension]], and [[Friction]]. Understanding it requires a solid grasp of [[Scalars and Vectors]] because the normal force is a vector quantity with both magnitude and direction.

**中文:**
法向接触力（通常称为法向反作用力或简称“法向力”）是表面对放置在其上的物体施加的力。它垂直于表面。这是物理学中最基本的接触力之一，对于理解物体为何不会从桌子上掉下来、秤如何测量重量以及摩擦力如何产生至关重要。在更广泛的[[Types of Force]]主题中，法向接触力与[[Weight and Gravitational Force]]、[[Tension]]和[[Friction]]一起是核心成员。理解它需要扎实掌握[[Scalars and Vectors]]，因为法向力是一个具有大小和方向的矢量。

# 3. Key Concepts / 关键概念

**English:**
The Normal Contact Force is a **reaction force** — it only exists when an object is in contact with a surface. Its magnitude is not fixed; it adjusts to balance the perpendicular component of all other forces acting on the object.

**Key Principles:**
1. **Direction:** Always perpendicular to the surface. For a horizontal surface, it points vertically upward. For an inclined plane, it points perpendicular to the slope.
2. **Magnitude:** Determined by the perpendicular component of the object's weight and any other applied forces. On a horizontal surface with no other vertical forces, $N = mg$. On an inclined plane, $N = mg\cos\theta$.
3. **Newton's Third Law:** The normal force is the surface's reaction to the object pushing down on it. The object exerts an equal and opposite force on the surface.
4. **Relationship with Friction:** The normal force directly determines the maximum possible [[Friction]] force: $F_{max} = \mu N$.

**Common Pitfalls:**
- ❌ Thinking the normal force always equals weight. It only does on a horizontal surface with no other vertical forces.
- ❌ Confusing normal force with the reaction to weight. The normal force is the reaction to the object pushing on the surface, not to gravity.
- ❌ Forgetting that on an inclined plane, the normal force is less than the weight.

**Physical Interpretation:**
Imagine placing a book on a table. The book's weight pushes down on the table. The table's atoms compress slightly, creating an upward restoring force — this is the normal contact force. If you push down harder on the book, the table compresses more, increasing the normal force.

**中文:**
法向接触力是一种**反作用力**——它只在物体与表面接触时存在。它的大小不是固定的；它会调整以平衡作用在物体上的所有其他力的垂直分量。

**关键原则：**
1. **方向：** 始终垂直于表面。对于水平表面，它垂直向上。对于斜面，它垂直于斜面。
2. **大小：** 由物体重量的垂直分量和任何其他施加的力决定。在没有其他垂直力的水平表面上，$N = mg$。在斜面上，$N = mg\cos\theta$。
3. **牛顿第三定律：** 法向力是表面对物体向下推它的反作用。物体对表面施加大小相等、方向相反的力。
4. **与摩擦力的关系：** 法向力直接决定了最大可能的[[Friction]]力：$F_{max} = \mu N$。

**常见误区：**
- ❌ 认为法向力总是等于重量。它只在没有其他垂直力的水平表面上成立。
- ❌ 将法向力与重力的反作用力混淆。法向力是物体推表面的反作用，而不是重力的反作用。
- ❌ 忘记在斜面上，法向力小于重量。

**物理解释：**
想象把一本书放在桌子上。书的重量向下推桌子。桌子的原子稍微压缩，产生向上的恢复力——这就是法向接触力。如果你更用力地向下推书，桌子压缩更多，法向力增加。

# 4. Formulas / 公式

## Formula 1: Normal Force on a Horizontal Surface

$$ N = mg $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
| ------------- | ------------ | ------------ | ----------- |
| $N$ | Normal contact force | 法向接触力 | N (Newtons) |
| $m$ | Mass of object | 物体质量 | kg |
| $g$ | Acceleration due to gravity (≈ 9.81 m/s²) | 重力加速度 | m/s² |

## Formula 2: Normal Force on an Inclined Plane

$$ N = mg\cos\theta $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
| ------------- | ------------ | ------------ | ----------- |
| $\theta$ | Angle of incline from horizontal | 斜面与水平面的夹角 | ° (degrees) |

## Formula 3: Normal Force with Additional Vertical Forces

$$ N = mg + F_{applied,\perp} $$

Where $F_{applied,\perp}$ is the perpendicular component of any additional applied force (positive if pushing down, negative if pulling up).

**Derivation / 推导:**
For an object at rest on a surface, the net force perpendicular to the surface must be zero (equilibrium). Therefore:
$$ \sum F_{\perp} = 0 $$
$$ N - mg\cos\theta = 0 $$
$$ N = mg\cos\theta $$

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- Object is in contact with the surface
- Surface is rigid (non-deformable)
- For Formula 1: Horizontal surface, no other vertical forces
- For Formula 2: Object on a frictionless or rough inclined plane (the normal force is the same regardless of friction)

# 6. Worked Example / 典型例题

### Example 1: Normal Force on a Horizontal Surface

### Question / 题目
**English:** A 5.0 kg box rests on a horizontal floor. A person pushes down on the box with a force of 20 N. Calculate the normal contact force exerted by the floor on the box. (Take $g = 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$)

**中文:** 一个5.0公斤的箱子放在水平地板上。一个人用20 N的力向下推箱子。计算地板对箱子的法向接触力。（取 $g = 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$）

### Solution / 解答

**Step 1: Identify all vertical forces**
- Weight: $W = mg = 5.0 \times 9.81 = 49.05 \text{ N}$ (downward)
- Applied push: $F_{push} = 20 \text{ N}$ (downward)
- Normal force: $N$ (upward, unknown)

**Step 2: Apply equilibrium condition (box is at rest)**
$$ \sum F_{\perp} = 0 $$
$$ N - W - F_{push} = 0 $$

**Step 3: Solve for N**
$$ N = W + F_{push} $$
$$ N = 49.05 + 20 $$
$$ N = 69.05 \text{ N} $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** 69 N (to 2 significant figures) **答案:** 69 N（保留两位有效数字）

### Quick Tip / 提示
When additional vertical forces are present, the normal force adjusts to maintain equilibrium. Pushing down increases N; pulling up decreases N. If you pull up with a force equal to the weight, N becomes zero and the object lifts off!

---

### Example 2: Normal Force on an Inclined Plane

### Question / 题目
**English:** A 3.0 kg block slides down a frictionless slope inclined at 25° to the horizontal. Calculate the normal contact force acting on the block. ($g = 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$)

**中文:** 一个3.0公斤的块沿与水平面成25°角的无摩擦斜面滑下。计算作用在块上的法向接触力。（$g = 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$）

### Solution / 解答

**Step 1: Identify the perpendicular component of weight**
$$ W_{\perp} = mg\cos\theta = 3.0 \times 9.81 \times \cos 25° $$

**Step 2: Calculate**
$$ W_{\perp} = 29.43 \times 0.9063 $$
$$ W_{\perp} = 26.67 \text{ N} $$

**Step 3: Apply equilibrium perpendicular to slope**
$$ N = W_{\perp} = 26.67 \text{ N} $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** 27 N (to 2 significant figures) **答案:** 27 N（保留两位有效数字）

### Quick Tip / 提示
On an inclined plane, the normal force is always less than the weight. The steeper the slope, the smaller the normal force. At 90° (vertical), N = 0 — the object is in free fall!

**Flashcard 1:**
Q (EN): What is the direction of the normal contact force relative to the surface?
Q (CN): 法向接触力相对于表面的方向是什么？
A (EN): Always perpendicular (at 90°) to the surface.
A (CN): 始终垂直于表面（成90°角）。

**Flashcard 2:**
Q (EN): Does the normal force always equal the weight of an object?
Q (CN): 法向力总是等于物体的重量吗？
A (EN): No. It only equals weight on a horizontal surface with no other vertical forces. On an inclined plane, N = mg cos θ.
A (CN): 不。它只在没有其他垂直力的水平表面上等于重量。在斜面上，N = mg cos θ。

**Flashcard 3:**
Q (EN): What happens to the normal force if you push down on an object resting on a table?
Q (CN): 如果你向下推放在桌子上的物体，法向力会发生什么变化？
A (EN): The normal force increases to balance the additional downward force.
A (CN): 法向力增加以平衡额外的向下力。

**Flashcard 4:**
Q (EN): What is the normal force on an object on a frictionless inclined plane at angle θ?
Q (CN): 在角度为θ的无摩擦斜面上，物体的法向力是多少？
A (EN): N = mg cos θ
A (CN): N = mg cos θ

**Flashcard 5:**
Q (EN): How is the normal force related to friction?
Q (CN): 法向力与摩擦力有什么关系？
A (EN): The maximum frictional force is proportional to the normal force: F_max = μN.
A (CN): 最大摩擦力与法向力成正比：F_max = μN。
