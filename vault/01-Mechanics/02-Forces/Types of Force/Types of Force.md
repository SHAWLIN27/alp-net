# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This topic introduces the fundamental types of forces encountered in A-Level Mechanics. Forces are vector quantities that cause changes in motion (acceleration) or deformation of objects. Understanding the different types of forces—including weight, normal contact force, tension, and friction—is essential for constructing accurate [[Free-body Diagrams]] and solving mechanics problems. This topic serves as the foundation for [[Newton's Laws of Motion]], [[Equilibrium]], and [[Dynamics]].

In the real world, forces govern everything from a book resting on a table ([[Normal Contact Force]]) to a car accelerating on a road ([[Friction]]). Engineers, architects, and physicists use force analysis to design safe structures, vehicles, and machines. In Cambridge 9702 and Edexcel IAL examinations, this topic is assessed through definitions, calculations, and the ability to identify and label forces on diagrams. It is a prerequisite for more advanced topics such as [[Forces in Fluids (Upthrust and Viscous Force)]] and [[Circular Motion]].

**中文：**
本主题介绍A-Level力学中遇到的基本力类型。力是矢量，能引起物体运动状态的变化（加速度）或形变。理解不同类型的力——包括重力、法向接触力、张力和摩擦力——对于构建准确的[[Free-body Diagrams]]和解决力学问题至关重要。本主题是[[Newton's Laws of Motion]]、[[Equilibrium]]和[[Dynamics]]的基础。

在现实世界中，力支配着从书桌上的书（[[Normal Contact Force]]）到道路上加速的汽车（[[Friction]]）的一切。工程师、建筑师和物理学家使用力分析来设计安全的结构、车辆和机器。在剑桥9702和爱德思IAL考试中，本主题通过定义、计算以及识别和标注力的能力来评估。它是[[Forces in Fluids (Upthrust and Viscous Force)]]和[[Circular Motion]]等高级主题的先决条件。

# 4. Key Concepts Explained / 关键概念详解

## 4.1 Force as a Vector / 力作为矢量

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** A force is a [[Vector]] quantity, meaning it has both magnitude and direction. Forces are measured in newtons (N). When multiple forces act on an object, they can be combined using [[Vector Addition]] to find the resultant force. The direction of a force is crucial—changing the direction changes the effect on the object. For example, a force applied upward lifts an object, while the same force applied sideways moves it horizontally.

**中文：** 力是一个[[Vector]]量，意味着它既有大小又有方向。力的单位是牛顿（N）。当多个力作用在一个物体上时，可以通过[[Vector Addition]]将它们合成为合力。力的方向至关重要——改变方向会改变对物体的影响。例如，向上的力提起物体，而相同大小的侧向力则水平移动物体。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** In everyday life, pushing a door open requires a force in the direction of the push. If you push at an angle, only the component of the force perpendicular to the door causes it to open. This illustrates why force direction matters.

**中文：** 在日常生活中，推开门需要沿推的方向施加力。如果以角度推，只有垂直于门的力分量才能使门打开。这说明了为什么力的方向很重要。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:** Students often think force is a scalar. It is not—direction is essential.
- **中文：** 学生常认为力是标量。不是——方向是必不可少的。
- **English:** Students confuse force with pressure. Force is a push/pull; pressure is force per unit area.
- **中文：** 学生混淆力和压强。力是推/拉；压强是单位面积上的力。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:** Cambridge and Edexcel often ask students to resolve forces into components. Practice using [[Trigonometry]] to find horizontal and vertical components. Always draw a diagram first.

**中文：** 剑桥和爱德思常要求学生将力分解为分量。练习使用[[Trigonometry]]求水平和垂直分量。始终先画图。

---

## 4.2 Weight and Gravitational Force / 重量与万有引力

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** Weight is the [[Gravitational Force]] exerted on an object by a planet. It is given by $W = mg$, where $m$ is mass (kg) and $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity (approximately $9.81 \, \text{m s}^{-2}$ on Earth). Weight always acts vertically downward toward the center of the planet. Unlike mass, weight depends on location—an object weighs less on the Moon because $g$ is smaller. See [[Weight and Gravitational Force]] for more details.

**中文：** 重量是行星对物体施加的[[Gravitational Force]]。由$W = mg$给出，其中$m$是质量（kg），$g$是重力加速度（地球上约为$9.81 \, \text{m s}^{-2}$）。重量始终垂直向下指向行星中心。与质量不同，重量取决于位置——物体在月球上重量更轻，因为$g$更小。详见[[Weight and Gravitational Force]]。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** When you stand on a bathroom scale, it measures your weight (the gravitational force pulling you down). The scale reading changes if you go to a different planet.

**中文：** 当你站在浴室秤上时，它测量你的重量（将你向下拉的重力）。如果你去不同的星球，秤的读数会改变。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:** "Weight = mass." No—mass is the amount of matter; weight is the force of gravity on that matter.
- **中文：** "重量等于质量。" 不——质量是物质的量；重量是重力对该物质的作用力。
- **English:** "Weight is always $9.81 \, \text{N}$." No—weight depends on mass. A 10 kg object weighs $98.1 \, \text{N}$.
- **中文：** "重量总是$9.81 \, \text{N}$。" 不——重量取决于质量。10 kg的物体重量为$98.1 \, \text{N}$。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:** Always use $g = 9.81 \, \text{m s}^{-2}$ unless told otherwise. In exam questions, weight is often the starting point for calculating other forces.

**中文：** 除非另有说明，始终使用$g = 9.81 \, \text{m s}^{-2}$。在考试题中，重量通常是计算其他力的起点。

## 4.3 Normal Contact Force / 法向接触力

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** The normal contact force (often called the normal reaction) is the force exerted by a surface on an object in contact with it. It acts perpendicular to the surface. For an object resting on a horizontal surface, the normal contact force equals the weight if no other vertical forces act. On an inclined plane, the normal contact force is less than the weight. See [[Normal Contact Force]] for more details.

**中文：** 法向接触力（常称为法向反作用力）是表面对与其接触的物体施加的力。它垂直于表面作用。对于静止在水平面上的物体，如果没有其他垂直力作用，法向接触力等于重量。在斜面上，法向接触力小于重量。详见[[Normal Contact Force]]。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** When you place a book on a table, the table pushes up on the book with a normal contact force. This prevents the book from falling through the table.

**中文：** 当你把书放在桌子上时，桌子向上推书，产生法向接触力。这防止书掉穿桌子。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:** "Normal contact force always equals weight." No—it only equals weight on a horizontal surface with no other vertical forces.
- **中文：** "法向接触力总是等于重量。" 不——只有在水平面上且没有其他垂直力时才等于重量。
- **English:** "Normal contact force is always vertical." No—it is always perpendicular to the surface, which may be inclined.
- **中文：** "法向接触力总是垂直的。" 不——它总是垂直于表面，表面可能是倾斜的。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:** In [[Free-body Diagrams]], always draw the normal contact force perpendicular to the surface. On inclined planes, resolve weight into components parallel and perpendicular to the plane.

**中文：** 在[[Free-body Diagrams]]中，始终将法向接触力画成垂直于表面。在斜面上，将重量分解为平行和垂直于平面的分量。

---

## 4.4 Tension / 张力

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** Tension is the pulling force transmitted through a string, rope, cable, or chain when it is stretched. Tension acts along the length of the string, away from the object it is attached to. For a light, inextensible string, tension is the same throughout the string. See [[Tension]] for more details.

**中文：** 张力是当绳子、绳索、缆绳或链条被拉伸时传递的拉力。张力沿绳子的长度作用，远离它所连接的物体。对于轻质、不可伸长的绳子，张力在整根绳子上是相同的。详见[[Tension]]。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** When you pull a toy car with a string, the tension in the string transmits the force from your hand to the car. If the string is slack, there is no tension.

**中文：** 当你用绳子拉玩具车时，绳子中的张力将力从你的手传递到车。如果绳子松弛，就没有张力。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:** "Tension is a pushing force." No—tension is always a pulling force. Strings cannot push.
- **中文：** "张力是推力。" 不——张力总是拉力。绳子不能推。
- **English:** "Tension equals weight of the object." No—tension depends on the forces applied to the string.
- **中文：** "张力等于物体的重量。" 不——张力取决于施加在绳子上的力。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:** In pulley systems, tension is often the same on both sides of a light, frictionless pulley. Draw arrows away from the object to show tension.

**中文：** 在滑轮系统中，对于轻质、无摩擦的滑轮，张力在两侧通常相同。画箭头远离物体以表示张力。

## 4.5 Friction / 摩擦力

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** Friction is the force that opposes relative motion between two surfaces in contact. It acts parallel to the surfaces. There are two types: static friction (prevents motion from starting) and kinetic friction (opposes motion when surfaces are sliding). Friction depends on the nature of the surfaces and the normal contact force. See [[Friction]] for more details.

**中文：** 摩擦力是阻碍两个接触表面之间相对运动的力。它平行于表面作用。有两种类型：静摩擦力（阻止运动开始）和动摩擦力（当表面滑动时阻碍运动）。摩擦力取决于表面的性质和法向接触力。详见[[Friction]]。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** Without friction, you could not walk—your feet would slip. Car tires rely on friction to grip the road. However, friction also causes wear and energy loss.

**中文：** 没有摩擦力，你就无法行走——你的脚会打滑。汽车轮胎依靠摩擦力抓地。然而，摩擦力也会导致磨损和能量损失。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:** "Friction always opposes motion." Yes, but it can also be the force that causes motion (e.g., car accelerating).
- **中文：** "摩擦力总是阻碍运动。" 是的，但它也可以是引起运动的力（例如，汽车加速）。
- **English:** "Friction is always equal to the applied force." No—static friction can vary up to a maximum value.
- **中文：** "摩擦力总是等于施加的力。" 不——静摩擦力可以变化到最大值。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:** In exam questions, identify whether the surface is rough or smooth. If smooth, friction is zero. If rough, friction opposes motion. Use $F_f = \mu R$ for kinetic friction.

**中文：** 在考试题中，确定表面是粗糙还是光滑。如果光滑，摩擦力为零。如果粗糙，摩擦力阻碍运动。使用$F_f = \mu R$计算动摩擦力。

## 4.6 Upthrust / 浮力

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** Upthrust (or buoyant force) is the upward force exerted by a fluid on a submerged or partially submerged object. It arises because pressure in a fluid increases with depth, so the pressure on the bottom of the object is greater than on the top. Upthrust equals the weight of the fluid displaced (Archimedes' principle). See [[Forces in Fluids (Upthrust and Viscous Force)]] for more details.

**中文：** 浮力是流体对浸没或部分浸没的物体施加的向上力。它产生的原因是流体中的压力随深度增加，因此物体底部的压力大于顶部。浮力等于排开流体的重量（阿基米德原理）。详见[[Forces in Fluids (Upthrust and Viscous Force)]]。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** A boat floats because the upthrust from the water equals its weight. A helium balloon rises because the upthrust from the air is greater than its weight.

**中文：** 船漂浮是因为水提供的浮力等于其重量。氦气球上升是因为空气提供的浮力大于其重量。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:** "Upthrust depends on the weight of the object." No—it depends on the volume of fluid displaced.
- **中文：** "浮力取决于物体的重量。" 不——它取决于排开流体的体积。
- **English:** "Upthrust only acts in water." No—it acts in any fluid, including air.
- **中文：** "浮力只在水中有。" 不——它在任何流体中都有，包括空气。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:** Cambridge 9702 explicitly includes upthrust in this section. Edexcel also covers it. Use Archimedes' principle: $U = \rho V g$.

**中文：** 剑桥9702在本节明确包括浮力。爱德思也涵盖它。使用阿基米德原理：$U = \rho V g$。

# 5. Essential Equations / 核心公式

## 5.1 Weight Equation / 重量公式

**Equation / 公式:**
$$ W = mg $$

**Variables / 变量:**
| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $W$ | Weight | 重量 | N (newton) |
| $m$ | Mass | 质量 | kg |
| $g$ | Acceleration due to gravity | 重力加速度 | $\text{m s}^{-2}$ |

**Derivation / 推导:**
**English:** Weight is derived from Newton's second law, $F = ma$. When the only force acting is gravity, $F = W$ and $a = g$, so $W = mg$.

**中文：** 重量由牛顿第二定律$F = ma$推导得出。当唯一作用的力是重力时，$F = W$且$a = g$，所以$W = mg$。

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
**English:** Valid for any object near the surface of a planet where $g$ is constant. For large distances, use Newton's law of gravitation.

**中文：** 适用于行星表面附近$g$为常数的任何物体。对于大距离，使用牛顿万有引力定律。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
**English:** Does not account for relativistic effects or variations in $g$ with altitude.

**中文：** 不考虑相对论效应或$g$随高度的变化。

**Rearrangements / 变形:**
$$ m = \frac{W}{g} $$
$$ g = \frac{W}{m} $$

---

## 5.2 Friction Equation (Kinetic) / 摩擦力公式（动摩擦）

**Equation / 公式:**
$$ F_f = \mu R $$

**Variables / 变量:**
| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $F_f$ | Frictional force | 摩擦力 | N |
| $\mu$ | Coefficient of friction | 摩擦系数 | dimensionless |
| $R$ | Normal contact force | 法向接触力 | N |

**Derivation / 推导:**
**English:** This is an empirical relationship, not derived from first principles. It states that kinetic friction is proportional to the normal contact force.

**中文：** 这是一个经验关系，不是从基本原理推导出来的。它表明动摩擦力与法向接触力成正比。

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
**English:** Valid only for kinetic (sliding) friction. For static friction, use $F_f \leq \mu_s R$.

**中文：** 仅适用于动（滑动）摩擦。对于静摩擦，使用$F_f \leq \mu_s R$。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
**English:** Does not apply to rolling friction or fluid friction. Assumes surfaces are dry and not lubricated.

**中文：** 不适用于滚动摩擦或流体摩擦。假设表面干燥且无润滑。

**Rearrangements / 变形:**
$$ \mu = \frac{F_f}{R} $$
$$ R = \frac{F_f}{\mu} $$

---

## 5.3 Upthrust Equation / 浮力公式

**Equation / 公式:**
$$ U = \rho V g $$

**Variables / 变量:**
| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $U$ | Upthrust | 浮力 | N |
| $\rho$ | Density of fluid | 流体密度 | $\text{kg m}^{-3}$ |
| $V$ | Volume of fluid displaced | 排开流体的体积 | $\text{m}^3$ |
| $g$ | Acceleration due to gravity | 重力加速度 | $\text{m s}^{-2}$ |

**Derivation / 推导:**
**English:** From Archimedes' principle: upthrust = weight of fluid displaced. Weight of fluid = mass of fluid × $g$ = density × volume × $g$.

**中文：** 由阿基米德原理：浮力 = 排开流体的重量。流体的重量 = 流体的质量 × $g$ = 密度 × 体积 × $g$。

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
**English:** Valid for any object fully or partially submerged in a fluid at rest.

**中文：** 适用于任何完全或部分浸没在静止流体中的物体。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
**English:** Does not account for compressibility of fluids or objects. Assumes uniform density of fluid.

**中文：** 不考虑流体或物体的可压缩性。假设流体密度均匀。

**Rearrangements / 变形:**
$$ \rho = \frac{U}{Vg} $$
$$ V = \frac{U}{\rho g} $$

### Shape / 形状
**English:** Initially, friction increases linearly with applied force (static region) until a maximum, then drops slightly and remains constant (kinetic region).
**中文：** 最初，摩擦力随施加力线性增加（静摩擦区域），直到最大值，然后略微下降并保持恒定（动摩擦区域）。

### Gradient Meaning / 斜率含义
**English:** In the static region, gradient = 1 (friction equals applied force). In kinetic region, gradient = 0.
**中文：** 在静摩擦区域，斜率 = 1（摩擦力等于施加力）。在动摩擦区域，斜率 = 0。

### Area Meaning / 面积含义
**English:** No physical meaning.
**中文：** 没有物理意义。

### Exam Interpretation / 考试解读
**English:** The peak represents the maximum static friction. The constant region represents kinetic friction.
**中文：** 峰值代表最大静摩擦力。恒定区域代表动摩擦力。

### Common Questions / 常见问题
**English:** "Determine the coefficient of static friction." "Explain why friction drops after motion starts."
**中文：** "确定静摩擦系数。" "解释为什么运动开始后摩擦力下降。"

# 8. Worked Examples / 典型例题

## Example 1: Calculating Weight and Normal Contact Force / 计算重量和法向接触力

### Question / 题目
**English:** A box of mass 5.0 kg rests on a horizontal floor. (a) Calculate the weight of the box. (b) State the magnitude and direction of the normal contact force acting on the box. (c) If a person pushes down on the box with a force of 20 N, what is the new normal contact force?

**中文：** 一个质量为5.0 kg的箱子静止在水平地板上。(a) 计算箱子的重量。(b) 说明作用在箱子上的法向接触力的大小和方向。(c) 如果一个人用20 N的力向下压箱子，新的法向接触力是多少？

### Image Prompt / 图片提示
> 📷 **IMAGE PROMPT — DIAGRAM: Box on Floor with Push**
>
> A 2D diagram showing a rectangular box on a horizontal floor. Three arrows: W downward from center, R upward from surface, and an additional downward arrow labeled "20 N" from the top of the box. Labels: "5.0 kg", "W", "R", "20 N". Educational style.

### Solution / 解答

**Step 1: Calculate weight**
$$ W = mg = 5.0 \times 9.81 = 49.05 \, \text{N} \approx 49 \, \text{N} $$

**Step 2: Normal contact force without push**
For an object at rest on a horizontal surface with no other vertical forces:
$$ R = W = 49 \, \text{N} $$
Direction: vertically upward.

**Step 3: Normal contact force with push**
When a downward force of 20 N is applied, the normal contact force must balance both the weight and the push:
$$ R = W + F_{\text{push}} = 49 + 20 = 69 \, \text{N} $$
Direction: vertically upward.

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** (a) 49 N (b) 49 N upward (c) 69 N upward
**答案：** (a) 49 N (b) 49 N 向上 (c) 69 N 向上

### Examiner Notes / 考官点评
**English:** Common mistake: students forget to include the push force when calculating the new normal contact force. Always consider all vertical forces. Use $g = 9.81 \, \text{m s}^{-2}$ unless told otherwise.

**中文：** 常见错误：学生在计算新的法向接触力时忘记包括推力。始终考虑所有垂直力。除非另有说明，使用$g = 9.81 \, \text{m s}^{-2}$。

### Alternative Method / 替代方法
**English:** Use Newton's first law: for equilibrium, the sum of upward forces equals the sum of downward forces.
**中文：** 使用牛顿第一定律：对于平衡，向上的力之和等于向下的力之和。

## Example 2: Friction on an Inclined Plane / 斜面上的摩擦力

### Question / 题目
**English:** A block of mass 2.0 kg is placed on a rough inclined plane at 30° to the horizontal. The block is on the point of sliding down the plane. (a) Draw a free-body diagram showing all forces acting on the block. (b) Calculate the normal contact force. (c) Calculate the maximum static frictional force. (d) Determine the coefficient of static friction.

**中文：** 一个质量为2.0 kg的木块放在与水平面成30°角的粗糙斜面上。木块即将沿斜面下滑。(a) 画出受力分析图，显示作用在木块上的所有力。(b) 计算法向接触力。(c) 计算最大静摩擦力。(d) 确定静摩擦系数。

### Image Prompt / 图片提示
> 📷 **IMAGE PROMPT — DIAGRAM: Block on Inclined Plane with Friction**
>
> A 2D diagram showing a rectangular block on an inclined plane at 30°. Three arrows: W pointing straight down, R perpendicular to the plane, F parallel to the plane pointing upward. Dashed lines show components of W: W sin30° parallel to plane, W cos30° perpendicular to plane. Labels: "2.0 kg", "30°", "W", "R", "F", "W sin30°", "W cos30°". Educational style.

### Solution / 解答

**Step 1: Free-body diagram**
Forces: Weight (W) vertically downward, Normal contact force (R) perpendicular to plane, Friction (F) parallel to plane upward.

**Step 2: Calculate weight**
$$ W = mg = 2.0 \times 9.81 = 19.62 \, \text{N} $$

**Step 3: Resolve weight into components**
Perpendicular to plane: $W_{\perp} = W \cos 30° = 19.62 \times \cos 30° = 19.62 \times 0.866 = 17.0 \, \text{N}$
Parallel to plane: $W_{\parallel} = W \sin 30° = 19.62 \times 0.5 = 9.81 \, \text{N}$

**Step 4: Calculate normal contact force**
For equilibrium perpendicular to plane:
$$ R = W_{\perp} = 17.0 \, \text{N} $$

**Step 5: Calculate maximum static frictional force**
At the point of sliding, friction equals the component of weight down the plane:
$$ F_{\text{max}} = W_{\parallel} = 9.81 \, \text{N} $$

**Step 6: Determine coefficient of static friction**
$$ \mu_s = \frac{F_{\text{max}}}{R} = \frac{9.81}{17.0} = 0.577 $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** (a) See diagram (b) 17.0 N (c) 9.81 N (d) 0.577
**答案：** (a) 见图 (b) 17.0 N (c) 9.81 N (d) 0.577

### Examiner Notes / 考官点评
**English:** Common mistake: students forget to resolve weight into components. Always draw the components as dashed lines. The coefficient of friction has no units.

**中文：** 常见错误：学生忘记将重量分解为分量。始终将分量画成虚线。摩擦系数没有单位。

### Alternative Method / 替代方法
**English:** Use the relationship $\mu = \tan \theta$ when the block is on the point of sliding. $\mu = \tan 30° = 0.577$.
**中文：** 当木块即将滑动时，使用关系$\mu = \tan \theta$。$\mu = \tan 30° = 0.577$。

# 10. Practical Skills Connections / 实验技能链接

**English:**
This topic connects to practical skills assessed in CAIE Paper 3 (AS) and Paper 5 (A2), as well as Edexcel Unit 3 (AS) and Unit 6 (A2). Key practical activities include:

1. **Measuring Weight Using a Spring Balance / 使用弹簧秤测量重量**
   - **English:** Students calibrate a spring balance and measure the weight of various objects. They must account for uncertainties in the scale reading and the effect of gravity variations.
   - **中文：** 学生校准弹簧秤并测量各种物体的重量。他们必须考虑刻度读数的不确定度和重力变化的影响。

2. **Investigating Friction / 研究摩擦力**
   - **English:** Students use a force sensor or spring balance to measure the force required to pull a block across a surface. They vary the normal contact force by adding masses and plot a graph of friction vs normal force to determine the coefficient of friction.
   - **中文：** 学生使用力传感器或弹簧秤测量拉动木块通过表面所需的力。他们通过增加质量来改变法向接触力，并绘制摩擦力与法向力的关系图以确定摩擦系数。

3. **Investigating Upthrust / 研究浮力**
   - **English:** Students submerge objects in water and measure the apparent weight loss using a spring balance. They relate this to the volume of water displaced.
   - **中文：** 学生将物体浸入水中，并使用弹簧秤测量表观重量损失。他们将其与排开水的体积联系起来。

**Measurements and Uncertainties / 测量与不确定度:**
- **English:** All force measurements have uncertainties due to scale reading errors. Students should estimate and record uncertainties, and use error bars on graphs.
- **中文：** 所有力的测量都有由于刻度读数误差引起的不确定度。学生应估计并记录不确定度，并在图表上使用误差棒。

**Graph Plotting / 图表绘制:**
- **English:** Plotting friction vs normal force requires a line of best fit. The gradient gives the coefficient of friction. Students must include error bars and calculate the gradient with its uncertainty.
- **中文：** 绘制摩擦力与法向力的关系图需要最佳拟合线。斜率给出摩擦系数。学生必须包括误差棒并计算斜率及其不确定度。

**Experimental Design / 实验设计:**
- **English:** Students may be asked to design an experiment to determine the coefficient of friction between two surfaces. Key considerations include: controlling variables (e.g., surface area, speed), repeating measurements, and reducing systematic errors.
- **中文：** 学生可能被要求设计一个实验来确定两个表面之间的摩擦系数。关键考虑因素包括：控制变量（例如，表面积、速度）、重复测量和减少系统误差。

> 📋 **CIE Only:** CAIE Paper 3 often includes questions on measuring forces using a spring balance and investigating friction. Students must be familiar with the format of the practical paper.
>
> 📋 **Edexcel Only:** Edexcel Unit 3 includes a practical investigation on forces. Students may be asked to evaluate experimental methods and suggest improvements.
