# Weight and Gravitational Force / 重量与引力

---

# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
Weight is the gravitational force exerted on an object by a planet or other massive body. This sub-topic explores the relationship between mass and weight, the concept of gravitational field strength $g$, and how weight varies with location. Understanding weight is fundamental to [[Types of Force]] and is essential for constructing [[Free-body Diagrams]] and analyzing equilibrium situations. Weight is distinct from mass — mass is a scalar property of matter, while weight is a vector force that depends on the local gravitational field.

**中文:**
重量是行星或其他大质量物体对物体施加的引力。本子知识点探讨质量与重量的关系、引力场强度 $g$ 的概念，以及重量如何随位置变化。理解重量是 [[Types of Force]] 的基础，对于构建 [[Free-body Diagrams]] 和分析平衡情况至关重要。重量与质量不同——质量是物质的标量属性，而重量是取决于局部引力场的矢量力。

# 2. Core Definition / 核心定义

| Term (EN/CN) | Definition (EN) | Definition (CN) |
| ------------ | --------------- | --------------- |
| **Weight** / 重量 | The gravitational force exerted on an object, equal to the product of its mass and the local gravitational field strength. | 物体所受的引力，等于其质量与当地引力场强度的乘积。 |
| **Gravitational Field Strength** / 引力场强度 | The force per unit mass experienced by a small test mass placed in a gravitational field. | 放置在引力场中的小测试质量所受到的每单位质量的力。 |

| **Free Fall** / 自由落体 | Motion under the influence of gravity alone, with no other forces acting. | 仅受重力作用、无其他力作用的运动。 |

# 3. Key Concepts / 关键概念

**English:**

### Weight vs. Mass — A Critical Distinction

The most common mistake students make is confusing weight and mass. **Mass** ($m$) is a [[Scalars and Vectors|scalar]] quantity — it measures how much matter an object contains and is constant everywhere. **Weight** ($W$) is a [[Scalars and Vectors|vector]] force — it depends on both the mass and the local gravitational field strength $g$.

$$ W = mg $$

On Earth, $g \approx 9.81 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$, so a 1 kg mass has a weight of approximately 9.81 N. On the Moon, where $g \approx 1.62 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$, the same 1 kg mass has a weight of only 1.62 N — but its mass remains 1 kg.

### Gravitational Field Strength $g$

Gravitational field strength is defined as:

$$ g = \frac{F}{m} $$

where $F$ is the gravitational force on a test mass $m$. The unit of $g$ is $\text{N kg}^{-1}$, which is equivalent to $\text{m s}^{-2}$ (acceleration due to gravity). This equivalence is why objects in free fall accelerate at $g$ regardless of their mass — a key result from Galileo's experiments.

### Weight as a Force in Free-body Diagrams

In [[Free-body Diagrams]], weight is always drawn as an arrow pointing **vertically downward** from the center of mass of the object. The length of the arrow represents the magnitude $mg$. When analyzing forces, weight must be resolved into components if the object is on an inclined plane — this connects to [[Normal Contact Force]] and [[Friction]].

### Variation of Weight with Location

Weight is not constant — it varies with:
- **Altitude**: $g$ decreases with height above Earth's surface
- **Latitude**: $g$ is slightly larger at the poles than at the equator due to Earth's rotation and shape
- **Planetary body**: Different planets have different $g$ values

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Using mass and weight interchangeably** — always check units: mass in kg, weight in N
2. **Forgetting that $g$ varies** — exam problems often specify "on Earth" or "on another planet"
3. **Confusing $g$ as acceleration vs. field strength** — numerically equal but conceptually different

**中文:**

### 重量与质量——关键区别

学生最常见的错误是混淆重量和质量。**质量** ($m$) 是[[Scalars and Vectors|标量]]——它衡量物体所含物质的多少，在任何地方都恒定不变。**重量** ($W$) 是[[Scalars and Vectors|矢量]]力——它取决于质量和当地的引力场强度 $g$。

$$ W = mg $$

在地球上，$g \approx 9.81 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$，所以 1 kg 质量的物体重量约为 9.81 N。在月球上，$g \approx 1.62 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$，同样的 1 kg 质量物体重量仅为 1.62 N——但其质量仍然是 1 kg。

### 引力场强度 $g$

引力场强度定义为：

$$ g = \frac{F}{m} $$

其中 $F$ 是测试质量 $m$ 所受的引力。$g$ 的单位是 $\text{N kg}^{-1}$，等同于 $\text{m s}^{-2}$（重力加速度）。这种等价性解释了为什么自由落体中的物体无论质量大小都以 $g$ 加速——这是伽利略实验的关键结果。

### 自由体图中的重量作为力

在[[Free-body Diagrams]]中，重量总是从物体的质心画一个**垂直向下**的箭头。箭头的长度代表大小 $mg$。分析力时，如果物体在斜面上，必须将重量分解为分量——这与[[Normal Contact Force]]和[[Friction]]相关。

### 重量随位置的变化

重量不是恒定的——它随以下因素变化：
- **海拔**：$g$ 随地球表面以上高度增加而减小
- **纬度**：由于地球自转和形状，$g$ 在极地略大于赤道
- **行星体**：不同行星有不同的 $g$ 值

### 常见陷阱

1. **互换使用质量和重量**——始终检查单位：质量用 kg，重量用 N
2. **忘记 $g$ 会变化**——考试问题通常指定"在地球上"或"在另一颗行星上"
3. **混淆 $g$ 作为加速度与场强度**——数值相等但概念不同

# 4. Formulas / 公式

## Primary Formula: Weight

$$ W = mg $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
| ------------- | ------------ | ------------ | ----------- |
| $W$ | Weight | 重量 | N (newton) |
| $m$ | Mass | 质量 | kg |
| $g$ | Gravitational field strength | 引力场强度 | N kg⁻¹ (or m s⁻²) |

## Secondary Formula: Gravitational Field Strength

$$ g = \frac{F}{m} $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
| ------------- | ------------ | ------------ | ----------- |
| $g$ | Gravitational field strength | 引力场强度 | N kg⁻¹ |
| $F$ | Gravitational force on test mass | 测试质量所受引力 | N |
| $m$ | Test mass | 测试质量 | kg |

**Derivation / 推导:**
The formula $W = mg$ is a direct application of the definition of gravitational field strength. If $g$ is the force per unit mass, then the force (weight) on a mass $m$ is simply $m$ times $g$.

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- $g$ is assumed constant near the surface of a planet (uniform field approximation)
- For A-Level, $g = 9.81 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$ on Earth's surface unless stated otherwise
- The formula applies to any massive body, not just Earth

# 6. Worked Example / 典型例题

### Example 1: Basic Weight Calculation

### Question / 题目
**English:**
An astronaut has a mass of 75 kg. Calculate:
(a) Her weight on Earth ($g = 9.81 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$)
(b) Her weight on Mars ($g = 3.71 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$)
(c) Her mass on Mars

**中文:**
一名宇航员的质量为 75 kg。计算：
(a) 她在地球上的重量 ($g = 9.81 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$)
(b) 她在火星上的重量 ($g = 3.71 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$)
(c) 她在火星上的质量

### Solution / 解答

**(a) Weight on Earth:**
$$ W_{\text{Earth}} = mg = 75 \times 9.81 = 735.75 \text{ N} \approx 736 \text{ N} $$

**(b) Weight on Mars:**
$$ W_{\text{Mars}} = mg = 75 \times 3.71 = 278.25 \text{ N} \approx 278 \text{ N} $$

**(c) Mass on Mars:**
Mass is invariant — it does not change with location.
$$ m_{\text{Mars}} = 75 \text{ kg} $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** (a) 736 N, (b) 278 N, (c) 75 kg
**答案:** (a) 736 N, (b) 278 N, (c) 75 kg

### Quick Tip / 提示
Always check: if the question asks for "mass", the answer should be in kg and should not change with location. If it asks for "weight", the answer is in N and depends on $g$.

---

### Example 2: Finding $g$ from Weight

### Question / 题目
**English:**
A 500 g object has a weight of 4.90 N on a distant planet. Calculate the gravitational field strength on that planet.

**中文:**
一个 500 g 的物体在某遥远行星上的重量为 4.90 N。计算该行星上的引力场强度。

### Solution / 解答

**Step 1:** Convert mass to kg
$$ m = 500 \text{ g} = 0.500 \text{ kg} $$

**Step 2:** Use $W = mg$ rearranged for $g$
$$ g = \frac{W}{m} = \frac{4.90}{0.500} = 9.80 \text{ N kg}^{-1} $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** $g = 9.80 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$
**答案:** $g = 9.80 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$

### Quick Tip / 提示
Always convert mass to kg before using $W = mg$. A common exam trap is giving mass in grams.
