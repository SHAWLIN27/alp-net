# Gravitational Potential Energy (GPE) / 重力势能

---

## 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
Gravitational Potential Energy (GPE) is the energy stored in an object due to its position in a gravitational field. For A-Level Physics, we focus on the near-Earth approximation where GPE depends on an object's height above a reference level. This sub-topic is fundamental to understanding [[Conservation of Energy]] and connects directly to [[Kinetic Energy (KE)]] through energy transfers in free fall and projectile motion. GPE is a scalar quantity and forms the foundation for analyzing gravitational fields in more advanced topics.

**中文:**
重力势能（GPE）是物体由于在重力场中的位置而储存的能量。在A-Level物理中，我们关注近地近似，其中GPE取决于物体相对于参考水平面的高度。这个子知识点是理解[[Conservation of Energy]]的基础，并通过自由落体和抛体运动中的能量转换与[[Kinetic Energy (KE)]]直接相关。GPE是标量，为分析更高级的重力场主题奠定基础。

| ------------ | --------------- | --------------- |

## 3. Key Concepts / 关键概念

**English:**

### The Nature of GPE
Gravitational Potential Energy is not an absolute quantity — it is always measured relative to a chosen reference level. This is because only *changes* in GPE are physically meaningful. When an object falls, its GPE decreases, and this energy is converted into [[Kinetic Energy (KE)]] (or other forms like heat due to air resistance).

### Relationship to Work Done
The change in GPE ($\Delta E_p$) equals the [[Work Done by a Force|work done]] against gravity to move the object vertically. If you lift a book from the floor to a shelf, you do positive work against gravity, and the book gains GPE. Conversely, when the book falls, gravity does positive work, and GPE decreases.

### Key Points for A-Level:
- **Sign convention**: GPE increases with height; decreases as height decreases.
- **Reference level choice**: You can choose any level as zero — only differences matter. Common choices: ground level, tabletop, or the lowest point in a problem.
- **Path independence**: The change in GPE depends only on the vertical displacement, not the path taken. This is because gravity is a conservative force.
- **Limitation**: The formula $E_p = mgh$ assumes constant $g$ (near Earth's surface). For large heights where $g$ varies, a different formula is needed (not required at AS level).

### Common Pitfalls:
1. **Forgetting the reference level**: Always state or identify the zero level.
2. **Confusing GPE with gravitational potential**: GPE is energy (J); gravitational potential is energy per unit mass (J kg⁻¹).
3. **Using $mgh$ for non-vertical paths**: Remember, only the vertical height change matters.

**中文:**

### GPE的本质
重力势能不是绝对量——它总是相对于选定的参考水平面来测量。这是因为只有GPE的*变化*在物理上有意义。当物体下落时，其GPE减少，这部分能量转化为[[Kinetic Energy (KE)]]（或由于空气阻力转化为热量等其他形式）。

### 与做功的关系
GPE的变化（$\Delta E_p$）等于克服重力将物体垂直移动所做的[[Work Done by a Force|功]]。如果你把一本书从地板举到书架上，你克服重力做了正功，书获得了GPE。相反，当书下落时，重力做正功，GPE减少。

### A-Level关键点：
- **符号约定**：GPE随高度增加而增加；随高度降低而减少。
- **参考水平面的选择**：可以选择任何水平面作为零点——只有差值重要。常见选择：地面、桌面或问题中的最低点。
- **路径无关性**：GPE的变化只取决于垂直位移，与路径无关。这是因为重力是保守力。
- **局限性**：公式$E_p = mgh$假设$g$恒定（在地球表面附近）。对于$g$变化的大高度，需要使用不同的公式（AS级别不要求）。

### 常见错误：
1. **忘记参考水平面**：始终说明或确定零点。
2. **混淆GPE与重力势**：GPE是能量（J）；重力势是单位质量的能量（J kg⁻¹）。
3. **对非垂直路径使用$mgh$**：记住，只有垂直高度变化才重要。

## 4. Formulas / 公式

### Primary Formula:

$$ \Delta E_p = mg\Delta h $$

or equivalently:

$$ E_p = mgh $$

(where $h$ is height above the chosen reference level)

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
| ------------- | ------------ | ------------ | ----------- |
| $E_p$ | Gravitational Potential Energy | 重力势能 | J (joules) |
| $m$ | Mass of the object | 物体的质量 | kg |
| $g$ | Gravitational field strength (≈ 9.81 N kg⁻¹ on Earth) | 重力场强度（地球上≈ 9.81 N kg⁻¹） | N kg⁻¹ or m s⁻² |
| $h$ | Height above reference level (or change in height) | 参考水平面以上的高度（或高度变化） | m |

### Derivation / 推导

The formula derives directly from the definition of [[Work Done by a Force|work done]]:

1. To lift an object vertically at constant speed, the applied force $F$ must equal the weight $mg$.
2. Work done = Force × distance moved in the direction of the force
3. $W = F \times \Delta h = mg \times \Delta h$
4. This work done equals the gain in GPE: $\Delta E_p = mg\Delta h$

### Conditions / 适用条件

- **Constant $g$**: Only valid near Earth's surface where $g$ is approximately constant.
- **Small height changes**: Height changes should be small compared to Earth's radius (~6400 km).
- **Uniform gravitational field**: Assumes the gravitational field is uniform (parallel field lines).
- **Reference level**: The value of $E_p$ depends on the chosen reference level, but $\Delta E_p$ is independent of this choice.

## 6. Worked Example / 典型例题

### Example 1: Calculating GPE Change

### Question / 题目
**English:**
A student lifts a 2.5 kg backpack from the floor (height 0 m) onto a table that is 0.80 m high. Calculate:
(a) The gain in gravitational potential energy of the backpack.
(b) The work done by the student against gravity.
(Take $g = 9.81 \text{ m s}^{-2}$)

**中文:**
一名学生将一个2.5 kg的书包从地板（高度0 m）举到0.80 m高的桌子上。计算：
(a) 书包重力势能的增加量。
(b) 学生克服重力所做的功。
（取$g = 9.81 \text{ m s}^{-2}$）

### Solution / 解答

**Part (a):**
1. Identify known values:
   - $m = 2.5 \text{ kg}$
   - $g = 9.81 \text{ m s}^{-2}$
   - $\Delta h = 0.80 - 0 = 0.80 \text{ m}$

2. Apply the formula:
   $$ \Delta E_p = mg\Delta h $$
   $$ \Delta E_p = 2.5 \times 9.81 \times 0.80 $$

3. Calculate:
   $$ \Delta E_p = 2.5 \times 7.848 = 19.62 \text{ J} $$

**Part (b):**
The work done against gravity equals the gain in GPE:
$$ W = \Delta E_p = 19.62 \text{ J} $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** (a) 19.6 J (to 3 s.f.) (b) 19.6 J
**答案:** (a) 19.6 J (3位有效数字) (b) 19.6 J

### Quick Tip / 提示
**English:** Remember that work done against gravity always equals the change in GPE, regardless of the path taken. If the student carries the backpack up stairs (non-vertical path), the work done against gravity is still $mg\Delta h$ — only the vertical height matters!

**中文:** 记住，克服重力所做的功总是等于GPE的变化，与路径无关。如果学生背着书包上楼梯（非垂直路径），克服重力所做的功仍然是$mg\Delta h$——只有垂直高度才重要！

### Example 2: Energy Conversion in Free Fall

### Question / 题目
**English:**
A 0.50 kg ball is dropped from rest at a height of 12 m above the ground. Neglecting air resistance, calculate:
(a) The GPE of the ball at the top.
(b) The speed of the ball when it has fallen 4.0 m.
(c) The speed of the ball just before hitting the ground.
(Take $g = 9.81 \text{ m s}^{-2}$)

**中文:**
一个0.50 kg的球从离地面12 m的高度从静止释放。忽略空气阻力，计算：
(a) 球在顶部的GPE。
(b) 球下落4.0 m时的速度。
(c) 球即将撞击地面时的速度。
（取$g = 9.81 \text{ m s}^{-2}$）

### Solution / 解答

**Part (a):**
$$ E_p = mgh = 0.50 \times 9.81 \times 12 = 58.86 \text{ J} $$

**Part (b):**
Using [[Conservation of Energy]]:
- Initial total energy = GPE at top = 58.86 J
- After falling 4.0 m, height = 12 - 4.0 = 8.0 m
- GPE at 8.0 m: $E_p = 0.50 \times 9.81 \times 8.0 = 39.24 \text{ J}$
- KE gained = Loss in GPE = 58.86 - 39.24 = 19.62 J
- Using $KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$:
  $$ 19.62 = \frac{1}{2} \times 0.50 \times v^2 $$
  $$ v^2 = \frac{2 \times 19.62}{0.50} = 78.48 $$
  $$ v = \sqrt{78.48} = 8.86 \text{ m s}^{-1} $$

**Part (c):**
- Just before hitting ground, height = 0 m, so GPE = 0 J
- All initial GPE converted to KE:
  $$ \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = 58.86 $$
  $$ v^2 = \frac{2 \times 58.86}{0.50} = 235.44 $$
  $$ v = \sqrt{235.44} = 15.3 \text{ m s}^{-1} $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** (a) 58.9 J (b) 8.86 m s⁻¹ (c) 15.3 m s⁻¹
**答案:** (a) 58.9 J (b) 8.86 m s⁻¹ (c) 15.3 m s⁻¹

### Quick Tip / 提示
**English:** In free fall problems, always use energy conservation rather than SUVAT equations when the path is complex or when you need to find speed at a specific height. Energy methods are often simpler and avoid sign errors with acceleration.

**中文:** 在自由落体问题中，当路径复杂或需要求特定高度处的速度时，始终使用能量守恒而非SUVAT方程。能量方法通常更简单，且可避免加速度的符号错误。
