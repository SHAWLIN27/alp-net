# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This sub-topic introduces the fundamental definition of **power** in physics. Power is a measure of how quickly energy is transferred or work is done. It moves beyond simply calculating *how much* work is done ([[Work Done by a Force]]) to describing *how fast* that work happens. This concept is the foundation for understanding more complex applications like [[Power and Velocity]] and is critical for analysing the performance of machines and engines. It is a direct application of the principle of [[Conservation of Energy]].

**中文:**
本子知识点介绍物理学中**功率**的基本定义。功率是衡量能量转移或做功快慢的物理量。它超越了单纯计算*做了多少*功（[[Work Done by a Force]]），而是描述做功的*快慢*。这个概念是理解[[Power and Velocity]]等更复杂应用的基础，对于分析机器和发动机的性能至关重要。它是[[Conservation of Energy]]原理的直接应用。

# 2. Core Definition / 核心定义

| Term (EN/CN) | Definition (EN) | Definition (CN) |
| ------------ | --------------- | --------------- |
| **Power** / 功率 | The rate at which work is done or energy is transferred. | 做功或能量转移的速率。 |
| **Average Power** / 平均功率 | The total work done divided by the total time taken. | 总功除以总时间。 |
| **Instantaneous Power** / 瞬时功率 | The power at a specific moment in time. | 在某一特定时刻的功率。 |
| **Watt (W)** / 瓦特 | The SI unit of power. One watt is equal to one joule per second ($1 \text{ W} = 1 \text{ J s}^{-1}$). | 功率的国际单位。一瓦特等于每秒一焦耳 ($1 \text{ W} = 1 \text{ J s}^{-1}$)。 |

# 3. Key Concepts / 关键概念

**English:**
The core idea of power is **rate**. A powerful machine is not necessarily one that can do a huge amount of work, but one that can do a given amount of work very quickly. For example, a high-performance sports car and a family saloon car might both be able to climb the same steep hill (doing the same amount of work against gravity), but the sports car can do it in a much shorter time, meaning it has a higher power output.

The definition $P = \frac{W}{t}$ gives the **average power** over a time interval $t$. To find the power at a specific instant, we consider the limit as the time interval approaches zero, leading to the instantaneous power formula $P = Fv$ (covered in [[Power and Velocity]]).

**Common Pitfall:** Students often confuse power with force or work. A large force does not necessarily mean high power if the work is done slowly. Similarly, a large amount of work done does not imply high power if it takes a very long time.

**中文:**
功率的核心概念是**速率**。一台强大的机器不一定能做大量的功，而是能在非常短的时间内完成一定量的功。例如，一辆高性能跑车和一辆家用轿车都能爬上同一个陡坡（克服重力做相同的功），但跑车能在更短的时间内完成，这意味着它的功率输出更高。

定义 $P = \frac{W}{t}$ 给出的是时间间隔 $t$ 内的**平均功率**。要找到某一特定时刻的功率，我们需要考虑时间间隔趋近于零的极限，从而得出瞬时功率公式 $P = Fv$（详见[[Power and Velocity]]）。

**常见误区：** 学生经常将功率与力或功混淆。如果做功很慢，即使力很大，也不一定意味着高功率。同样，如果花费很长时间，即使做了大量的功，也不意味着高功率。

# 4. Formulas / 公式

The fundamental formula for power is:

$$ P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{\Delta E}{t} $$

Where $W$ is work done and $\Delta E$ is energy transferred.

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
| ------------- | ------------ | ------------ | ----------- |
| $P$ | Power | 功率 | W (Watt / 瓦特) |
| $W$ | Work done | 做功 | J (Joule / 焦耳) |
| $\Delta E$ | Energy transferred | 转移的能量 | J (Joule / 焦耳) |
| $t$ | Time taken | 所用时间 | s (Second / 秒) |

**Derivation / 推导:**
This formula is a direct definition. It is derived from the concept of rate: the amount of work done ($W$) per unit time ($t$).

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
This formula calculates **average power** over the time interval $t$. It applies to any situation where work is done or energy is transferred at a constant or varying rate.

# 6. Worked Example / 典型例题

### Question / 题目
**English:** A motor does 2400 J of work in lifting a load. If the motor has a power output of 400 W, how long does it take to lift the load?
**中文:** 一台电动机在提升重物时做了2400焦耳的功。如果电动机的输出功率为400瓦，提升重物需要多长时间？

### Solution / 解答
1.  **Identify knowns / 确定已知量:**
    * Work done, $W = 2400 \text{ J}$
    * Power, $P = 400 \text{ W}$

2.  **Choose the correct formula / 选择正确的公式:**
    $$ P = \frac{W}{t} $$

3.  **Rearrange for the unknown / 解出未知量:**
    $$ t = \frac{W}{P} $$

4.  **Substitute and solve / 代入并求解:**
    $$ t = \frac{2400 \text{ J}}{400 \text{ W}} = 6.0 \text{ s} $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** 6.0 s **答案:** 6.0 秒

### Quick Tip / 提示
Always ensure your units are consistent. Work must be in Joules (J) and Power in Watts (W) to get time in seconds (s). Remember that $1 \text{ W} = 1 \text{ J s}^{-1}$.
