# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This sub-topic distinguishes between the two fundamental models of gravitational fields used in A-Level Physics: the **radial field** and the **uniform field**. A radial field, produced by a point mass (like a planet or star), has field lines that converge radially towards the centre, with field strength decreasing with distance ($g \propto 1/r^2$). A uniform field, approximated near the surface of a large body, has parallel, equally spaced field lines and constant field strength ($g \approx \text{constant}$). Understanding this distinction is crucial for correctly applying [[Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation]] (radial) versus the simple $W = mg$ equation (uniform). It also forms the foundation for understanding [[Gravitational Potential]] and [[Circular Orbits]].

**中文:**
本子知识点区分了A-Level物理中使用的两种基本引力场模型：**径向场**和**均匀场**。径向场由点质量（如行星或恒星）产生，其场线径向汇聚于中心，场强随距离减小（$g \propto 1/r^2$）。均匀场在大质量天体表面附近近似成立，其场线平行且等间距，场强恒定（$g \approx \text{constant}$）。理解这一区别对于正确应用[[牛顿万有引力定律]]（径向场）与简单的 $W = mg$ 公式（均匀场）至关重要。它也是理解[[引力势]]和[[圆周轨道]]的基础。

# 3. Key Concepts / 关键概念

**English:**
The key to mastering this topic is visualizing the field lines.

1.  **Radial Field (Point Mass):**
    *   **Source:** Any spherical mass (planet, star) behaves as if all its mass is concentrated at its centre (a [[Point Mass]]) for points *outside* its surface.
    *   **Field Lines:** Arrows pointing directly towards the centre. The spacing between lines increases as you move away from the centre, visually representing the decrease in field strength.
    *   **Field Strength:** Given by $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$, derived from [[Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation]] and $F=mg$. This is an inverse square law.
    *   **Application:** Used for calculating orbital motion ([[Circular Orbits]]), escape velocity, and gravitational potential.

2.  **Uniform Field (Near Earth's Surface):**
    *   **Source:** A very small region of a very large radial field. Over a few kilometres, the curvature of the Earth is negligible.
    *   **Field Lines:** Parallel, equally spaced, vertical arrows pointing downwards.
    *   **Field Strength:** Constant, approximately $g \approx 9.81 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$ (or $9.81 \text{ m s}^{-2}$).
    *   **Application:** Used for projectile motion, simple weight calculations ($W=mg$), and gravitational potential difference ($\Delta V = g \Delta h$).

**Common Pitfall:** Students often try to use $g = GM/r^2$ for problems near the Earth's surface. While technically correct, it is far more convenient to use the constant $g = 9.81$. Conversely, using $W=mg$ for a satellite in orbit is incorrect because $g$ is no longer constant.

**中文:**
掌握本知识点的关键在于可视化场线。

1.  **径向场（点质量）：**
    *   **来源：** 任何球形质量（行星、恒星）在*其表面之外*的点上，其行为就像所有质量都集中在中心（一个[[点质量]]）一样。
    *   **场线：** 箭头直接指向中心。随着远离中心，线之间的间距增大，直观地表示了场强的减小。
    *   **场强：** 由 $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$ 给出，源自[[牛顿万有引力定律]]和 $F=mg$。这是一个平方反比定律。
    *   **应用：** 用于计算轨道运动（[[圆周轨道]]）、逃逸速度和引力势。

2.  **均匀场（地球表面附近）：**
    *   **来源：** 一个非常大的径向场中非常小的区域。在几公里的范围内，地球的曲率可以忽略不计。
    *   **场线：** 平行、等间距、垂直向下的箭头。
    *   **场强：** 恒定，约为 $g \approx 9.81 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$（或 $9.81 \text{ m s}^{-2}$）。
    *   **应用：** 用于抛体运动、简单的重量计算（$W=mg$）和引力势差（$\Delta V = g \Delta h$）。

**常见错误：** 学生经常尝试在地球表面附近的问题中使用 $g = GM/r^2$。虽然技术上正确，但使用常数 $g = 9.81$ 要方便得多。相反，对轨道上的卫星使用 $W=mg$ 是错误的，因为那里的 $g$ 不再是常数。

# 4. Formulas / 公式

### Formula 1: Radial Field Strength

$$ g = \frac{GM}{r^2} $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
| ------------- | ------------ | ------------ | ----------- |
| $g$ | Gravitational field strength | 引力场强度 | N kg⁻¹ (or m s⁻²) |
| $G$ | Gravitational constant ($6.67 \times 10^{-11}$) | 引力常数 | N m² kg⁻² |
| $M$ | Mass of the object creating the field | 产生场的物体的质量 | kg |
| $r$ | Distance from the centre of mass | 到质心的距离 | m |

**Derivation / 推导:**
From [[Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation]], the force on a test mass $m$ is $F = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$. By definition, $g = F/m$, therefore $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$.

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- The field is created by a point mass or a spherically symmetric mass distribution.
- $r$ is measured from the centre of the mass.
- The point where $g$ is measured is outside the mass.

### Formula 2: Uniform Field Strength

$$ g = \frac{F}{m} $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
| ------------- | ------------ | ------------ | ----------- |
| $g$ | Gravitational field strength (constant) | 引力场强度（常数） | N kg⁻¹ (or m s⁻²) |
| $F$ | Gravitational force on the mass | 作用在质量上的引力 | N |
| $m$ | Mass of the object in the field | 在场中的物体的质量 | kg |

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- The region of space is small compared to the size of the massive body.
- The field strength is constant in magnitude and direction.

# 6. Worked Example / 典型例题

### Example 1: Identifying Field Types

### Question / 题目
**English:** A satellite is in a circular orbit 200 km above the Earth's surface. A ball is dropped from a height of 2 m above the Earth's surface. State and explain whether the gravitational field acting on each object can be considered uniform or radial.
**中文:** 一颗卫星在距地球表面200公里的圆形轨道上运行。一个球从距地面2米的高度落下。请说明并解释作用在每个物体上的引力场可以被认为是均匀的还是径向的。

### Solution / 解答
**Step 1: The Satellite**
The satellite is far from the Earth's surface. The Earth's curvature is significant, and the field strength changes noticeably with altitude.
**Explanation:** The field acting on the satellite is **radial**. The force of gravity always points towards the Earth's centre, and the field strength is given by $g = GM/r^2$.

**Step 2: The Ball**
The ball is dropped from a height of only 2 m. Over this tiny distance, the Earth's surface is effectively flat, and the change in $g$ is negligible ($\approx 0.0006\%$).
**Explanation:** The field acting on the ball can be approximated as **uniform**. The force of gravity is constant in both magnitude ($mg$) and direction (vertically downwards).

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** Satellite: Radial. Ball: Uniform.
**答案：** 卫星：径向场。球：均匀场。

### Quick Tip / 提示
If the object is in orbit or very far from the surface, use the radial model. If the object is near the surface (within a few km), use the uniform model.
