# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This sub-topic covers the principles and practical construction of thermometers, which are devices used to measure temperature. It explores how a physical property that changes with temperature (a thermometric property) can be used to create a temperature scale. You will learn about the key features of different types of thermometers, including liquid-in-glass, thermocouple, and resistance thermometers, and understand their specific advantages, disadvantages, and ranges. This knowledge is fundamental to understanding how we quantify the concept of [[Temperature Scales (Celsius, Kelvin)]] and is a prerequisite for more advanced thermal physics.

**中文:**
本子知识点涵盖温度计的原理和实际构造，温度计是用于测量温度的仪器。它探讨了如何利用随温度变化的物理性质（测温性质）来建立温标。你将学习不同类型温度计的关键特性，包括液体温度计、热电偶温度计和电阻温度计，并理解它们各自的优点、缺点和测量范围。这些知识是理解我们如何量化[[Temperature Scales (Celsius, Kelvin)]]概念的基础，也是学习更高级热物理学的先决条件。

# 3. Core Definitions / 核心定义

| Term (EN/CN) | Definition (EN) | Definition (CN) | Common Mistakes / 常见错误 |
|--------------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| **Thermometric Property** / 测温性质 | A physical property that changes measurably and predictably with temperature. | 一种随温度可测量且可预测地变化的物理性质。 | Confusing the property (e.g., volume of mercury) with the device itself (the thermometer). |
| **Fixed Point** / 固定点 | A reproducible physical state (e.g., melting ice, boiling water) used to calibrate a thermometer. | 用于校准温度计的可重复的物理状态（例如，冰的熔点、水的沸点）。 | Thinking fixed points are arbitrary numbers; they are *reproducible physical phenomena*. |
| **Calibration** / 校准 | The process of marking a scale on a thermometer by assigning values to fixed points and dividing the interval. | 通过给固定点赋值并划分间隔，在温度计上标记刻度线的过程。 | Forgetting that calibration requires *two* fixed points to define a scale. |
| **Sensitivity** / 灵敏度 | The size of the change in the thermometric property per unit change in temperature. | 测温性质随单位温度变化而产生的变化量大小。 | Confusing sensitivity with accuracy or range. A high sensitivity means a large output change for a small temperature change. |
| **Range** / 量程 | The minimum and maximum temperatures a thermometer can measure. | 温度计能够测量的最低和最高温度。 | Assuming all thermometers have the same range. |
| **Responsiveness** / 响应速度 | How quickly a thermometer reaches thermal equilibrium with its surroundings. | 温度计与其周围环境达到热平衡的速度。 | Confusing this with sensitivity. A thermometer can be sensitive but slow to respond. |

# 4. Key Concepts Explained / 关键概念详解

## 4.1 The Need for Fixed Points / 固定点的必要性

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** To create a meaningful temperature scale, we need reproducible reference points. Without them, every thermometer would be arbitrary. The two most common fixed points are the **ice point** (melting point of pure ice at standard atmospheric pressure, 0°C) and the **steam point** (boiling point of pure water at standard atmospheric pressure, 100°C). The interval between these is divided into 100 equal degrees for the Celsius scale. This process is called [[Temperature Scales (Celsius, Kelvin)]].

**中文:** 为了建立一个有意义的温标，我们需要可重复的参考点。没有它们，每个温度计都将是任意的。两个最常见的固定点是**冰点**（标准大气压下纯冰的熔点，0°C）和**汽点**（标准大气压下纯水的沸点，100°C）。这两个点之间的间隔被划分为100个等份，用于摄氏温标。这个过程被称为[[Temperature Scales (Celsius, Kelvin)]]。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** Fixed points provide a universal, objective standard for temperature measurement, ensuring that a temperature reading of, say, 50°C means the same thing in London, Shanghai, or on a spacecraft.

**中文:** 固定点为温度测量提供了一个通用的、客观的标准，确保例如50°C的温度读数在伦敦、上海或航天器上都代表相同的含义。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **EN:** Thinking that the ice point is 0°C *because* it's cold. It's 0°C because we *defined* it as such.
- **CN:** 认为冰点是0°C是因为它冷。它是0°C是因为我们*定义*它为0°C。
- **EN:** Believing that any ice and water mixture will be at 0°C. It must be pure ice and pure water at standard pressure.
- **CN:** 认为任何冰水混合物都在0°C。必须是标准大气压下的纯冰和纯水。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **EN:** Always mention "pure ice" and "pure water" at "standard atmospheric pressure" when defining fixed points.
- **CN:** 在定义固定点时，务必提及“标准大气压”下的“纯冰”和“纯水”。

## 4.2 Liquid-in-Glass Thermometer / 液体温度计

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** This is the most common type of thermometer. It uses the **thermometric property** of thermal expansion of a liquid (usually mercury or colored alcohol). The liquid is contained in a bulb and a narrow capillary tube. As temperature increases, the liquid expands and rises up the capillary. The height of the liquid column is a measure of temperature. The scale is marked by calibrating at the ice and steam points.

**中文:** 这是最常见的温度计类型。它利用液体（通常是水银或染色酒精）热膨胀的**测温性质**。液体被封装在玻璃泡和细长的毛细管中。当温度升高时，液体膨胀并沿毛细管上升。液柱的高度是温度的度量。通过在冰点和汽点进行校准来标记刻度。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** The thermometer works by reaching [[Thermal Equilibrium and the Zeroth Law]] with the object being measured. The liquid expands until its temperature equals the object's temperature.

**中文:** 温度计通过与待测物体达到[[Thermal Equilibrium and the Zeroth Law]]来工作。液体膨胀直到其温度等于物体的温度。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **EN:** Thinking the thermometer measures the "amount of heat". It measures its own temperature at equilibrium.
- **CN:** 认为温度计测量“热量多少”。它测量的是自身在平衡时的温度。
- **EN:** Forgetting that the bore of the capillary must be uniform for a linear scale.
- **CN:** 忘记毛细管的孔径必须均匀才能得到线性刻度。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **EN:** Be able to describe how to improve sensitivity (use a larger bulb, narrower capillary) and range (use a liquid with a high boiling point and low freezing point).
- **CN:** 能够描述如何提高灵敏度（使用更大的玻璃泡、更细的毛细管）和量程（使用沸点高、凝固点低的液体）。

# 5. Essential Equations / 核心公式

For a liquid-in-glass thermometer, the temperature $\theta$ on an uncalibrated scale can be found using linear interpolation:

$$ \theta = \frac{l_\theta - l_0}{l_{100} - l_0} \times 100^\circ \text{C} $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $\theta$ | Unknown temperature | 未知温度 | °C |
| $l_\theta$ | Length of liquid column at $\theta$ | 在温度 $\theta$ 时的液柱长度 | mm or cm |
| $l_0$ | Length of liquid column at ice point (0°C) | 在冰点 (0°C) 时的液柱长度 | mm or cm |
| $l_{100}$ | Length of liquid column at steam point (100°C) | 在汽点 (100°C) 时的液柱长度 | mm or cm |

**Derivation / 推导:** This assumes a linear relationship between the thermometric property (length $l$) and temperature $\theta$.

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- **EN:** The thermometric property must vary linearly with temperature over the range of interest. The capillary bore must be uniform.
- **CN:** 测温性质必须在感兴趣的范围内随温度线性变化。毛细管孔径必须均匀。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
- **EN:** The expansion of different liquids is not perfectly linear with temperature, leading to slight inaccuracies. The thermometer cannot measure temperatures outside the liquid's liquid phase range.
- **CN:** 不同液体的膨胀与温度并非完全线性，会导致轻微的不准确。温度计无法测量超出液体液态范围以外的温度。

# 8. Worked Examples / 典型例题

## Example 1: Calibrating a Thermometer / 校准温度计

### Question / 题目
**English:** The length of the mercury column in an uncalibrated thermometer is 2.5 cm at the ice point and 22.5 cm at the steam point. When placed in a beaker of warm water, the mercury column length is 12.5 cm. Calculate the temperature of the warm water in °C.
**中文:** 一支未校准的温度计，其水银柱在冰点时的长度为2.5厘米，在汽点时的长度为22.5厘米。当放入一杯温水中时，水银柱长度为12.5厘米。计算温水的温度（以°C为单位）。

### Solution / 解答
**Step 1: Identify known values.**
$l_0 = 2.5 \text{ cm}$
$l_{100} = 22.5 \text{ cm}$
$l_\theta = 12.5 \text{ cm}$

**Step 2: Apply the formula.**
$$ \theta = \frac{l_\theta - l_0}{l_{100} - l_0} \times 100^\circ \text{C} $$

**Step 3: Substitute and calculate.**
$$ \theta = \frac{12.5 - 2.5}{22.5 - 2.5} \times 100^\circ \text{C} $$
$$ \theta = \frac{10.0}{20.0} \times 100^\circ \text{C} $$
$$ \theta = 0.5 \times 100^\circ \text{C} = 50^\circ \text{C} $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** 50°C | **答案：** 50°C

### Quick Tip / 提示
- **EN:** Always subtract the ice point length from both the measured length and the steam point length. This gives the change in length from the ice point.
- **CN:** 务必从测量长度和汽点长度中减去冰点长度。这给出了从冰点开始的长度变化。

# 10. Practical Skills Connections / 实验技能链接

**English:**
This sub-topic is directly linked to practical skills. You may be asked to:
- **Calibrate a thermometer:** Using an ice bath and a steam generator to mark fixed points.
- **Measure temperature:** Using a liquid-in-glass or thermocouple thermometer in an experiment (e.g., [[Specific Heat Capacity and Latent Heat]]).
- **Evaluate uncertainties:** The resolution of a liquid-in-glass thermometer is typically half the smallest scale division. For a thermocouple, the uncertainty comes from the voltmeter and the stability of the reference junction.
- **Plot graphs:** Plotting a calibration curve for a thermocouple (EMF vs. Temperature).

**中文:**
本子知识点与实验技能直接相关。你可能会被要求：
- **校准温度计：** 使用冰水浴和蒸汽发生器来标记固定点。
- **测量温度：** 在实验中使用液体温度计或热电偶温度计（例如，[[Specific Heat Capacity and Latent Heat]]）。
- **评估不确定度：** 液体温度计的分辨率通常是最小刻度的一半。对于热电偶，不确定度来自电压表和参考端的稳定性。
- **绘制图表：** 绘制热电偶的校准曲线（电动势 vs. 温度）。
