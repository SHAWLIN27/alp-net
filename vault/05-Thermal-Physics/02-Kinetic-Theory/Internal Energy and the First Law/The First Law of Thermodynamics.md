# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
The First Law of Thermodynamics is a cornerstone of thermal physics, essentially stating the principle of conservation of energy as it applies to thermodynamic systems. This sub-topic focuses on the relationship between the internal energy ($\Delta U$) of a system, the thermal energy ($Q$) transferred to it, and the work ($W$) done by or on the system. It provides a quantitative framework for understanding how energy is exchanged in processes like heating, cooling, expansion, and compression. This law is crucial for analyzing the behavior of gases in engines, refrigerators, and other thermal devices, and it connects directly to the study of [[Internal Energy (KE + PE of molecules)]] and [[Work Done by/on a Gas]].

**中文:**
热力学第一定律是热物理学的基石，它本质上是能量守恒定律在热力学系统中的应用。本子知识点侧重于系统的内能变化 ($\Delta U$)、传递给系统的热能 ($Q$) 以及系统对外或外界对系统所做的功 ($W$) 之间的关系。它提供了一个定量框架，用于理解在加热、冷却、膨胀和压缩等过程中能量如何交换。该定律对于分析发动机、制冷机和其他热设备中气体的行为至关重要，并且直接与[[Internal Energy (KE + PE of molecules)]]和[[Work Done by/on a Gas]]的研究相关联。

# 3. Core Definitions / 核心定义

| Term (EN/CN) | Definition (EN) | Definition (CN) | Common Mistakes / 常见错误 |
|--------------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| **First Law of Thermodynamics** / 热力学第一定律 | The increase in internal energy of a system is equal to the sum of the thermal energy transferred to the system and the work done on the system. | 系统内能的增加等于传递给系统的热能和对系统所做的功之和。 | Confusing the sign of $W$ (work done *on* vs. work done *by* the system). |

| **Work Done ($W$)** / 做功 | Energy transferred when a force moves an object. In thermodynamics, it is often the work done by or on a gas during expansion or compression. | 当力移动物体时传递的能量。在热力学中，通常指气体在膨胀或压缩过程中对外或外界对气体所做的功。 | $W$ is positive when work is done *on* the system (compression). |

# 4. Key Concepts Explained / 关键概念详解

## 4.1 The First Law Equation and Sign Convention / 第一定律方程与符号约定

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
The First Law is mathematically expressed as:
$$ \Delta U = Q + W $$
Where:
- $\Delta U$ is the change in internal energy of the system.
- $Q$ is the thermal energy transferred to the system.
- $W$ is the work done *on* the system.

The **sign convention** is critical:
- **$Q$**: Positive (+) when thermal energy is transferred *to* the system (heating). Negative (-) when thermal energy is transferred *from* the system (cooling).
- **$W$**: Positive (+) when work is done *on* the system (e.g., compressing a gas). Negative (-) when work is done *by* the system (e.g., a gas expanding).

This convention is the standard for both CAIE and Edexcel. It ensures that energy added to the system increases its internal energy.

**中文:**
热力学第一定律的数学表达式为：
$$ \Delta U = Q + W $$
其中：
- $\Delta U$ 是系统内能的变化。
- $Q$ 是传递给系统的热能。
- $W$ 是外界对系统所做的功。

**符号约定** 至关重要：
- **$Q$**: 当热能 *传递到* 系统（加热）时为正（+）。当热能 *从* 系统传出（冷却）时为负（-）。
- **$W$**: 当 *外界对* 系统做功（例如，压缩气体）时为正（+）。当系统 *对外* 做功（例如，气体膨胀）时为负（-）。

此约定是 CAIE 和 Edexcel 的标准。它确保添加到系统的能量会增加其内能。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** The law states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred. The total energy change of a system is the net result of all energy transfers across its boundary, whether as heat or work.
**中文:** 该定律指出能量既不能被创造也不能被消灭，只能被转移。系统的总能量变化是跨越其边界的所有能量传递（无论是作为热量还是功）的净结果。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **EN:** Thinking $W$ always represents work done *by* the system. Always check the sign convention.
- **CN:** 认为 $W$ 总是代表系统 *对外* 做的功。务必检查符号约定。
- **EN:** Confusing $Q$ and $W$ as "forms of energy" rather than "processes of energy transfer". A system does not "contain" heat or work; it contains internal energy.
- **CN:** 混淆 $Q$ 和 $W$ 为“能量形式”而非“能量传递过程”。系统并不“包含”热量或功；它包含内能。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **EN:** When solving problems, first identify the process (isothermal, adiabatic, etc.) to determine which variable ($\Delta U$, $Q$, or $W$) is zero or has a known relationship.
- **CN:** 解题时，首先确定过程（等温、绝热等），以确定哪个变量（$\Delta U$, $Q$ 或 $W$）为零或具有已知关系。
- **EN:** Draw a clear diagram showing the direction of energy flow to help assign correct signs.
- **CN:** 画一个清晰的图表显示能量流动方向，以帮助分配正确的符号。

# 5. Essential Equations / 核心公式

## 5.1 The First Law of Thermodynamics / 热力学第一定律

$$ \Delta U = Q + W $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $\Delta U$ | Change in internal energy | 内能变化 | J (Joule) |
| $Q$ | Thermal energy transferred to the system | 传递给系统的热能 | J (Joule) |
| $W$ | Work done on the system | 外界对系统做的功 | J (Joule) |

**Derivation / 推导:** This is a statement of the conservation of energy, not derived from other principles.
**Conditions / 适用条件:** Applies to all thermodynamic systems, but is most commonly applied to [[Ideal Gases|ideal gases]] in A-Level physics.
**Limitations / 局限性:** The law itself is universal. However, applying it requires knowing the specific relationships for $Q$ and $W$ for different processes (e.g., $W = p\Delta V$ for isobaric processes).

# 6. Graphs and Relationships / 图表与关系

## 6.1 Pressure-Volume ($p$-$V$) Diagram and the First Law / 压强-体积 ($p$-$V$) 图与第一定律

### Axes / 坐标轴 (EN+CN)
- x-axis: Volume ($V$) / 体积 ($V$)
- y-axis: Pressure ($p$) / 压强 ($p$)

### Shape / 形状 (EN+CN)
The shape depends on the thermodynamic process. For example, an isothermal expansion is a hyperbolic curve ($pV = \text{constant}$), while an isobaric expansion is a horizontal line.

### Gradient Meaning / 斜率含义 (EN+CN)
The gradient of a $p$-$V$ curve is not a standard physical quantity with a simple meaning like in kinematics.

### Area Meaning / 面积含义 (EN+CN)
The area under a $p$-$V$ curve represents the **work done by the gas** ($W_{by}$). Since $W_{on} = -W_{by}$, the area under the curve is equal to $-W_{on}$.
- **EN:** If the volume increases (expansion), the area is positive, meaning work is done *by* the gas ($W_{by} > 0$), so $W_{on} < 0$.
- **CN:** 如果体积增加（膨胀），面积为正，意味着气体 *对外* 做功 ($W_{by} > 0$)，因此 $W_{on} < 0$。

### Exam Interpretation / 考试解读 (EN+CN)
- **EN:** A $p$-$V$ diagram is essential for visualizing a thermodynamic cycle. The net work done in a cycle is the area enclosed by the cycle. You can then use the First Law to find the net heat transfer.
- **CN:** $p$-$V$ 图对于可视化热力学循环至关重要。一个循环中做的净功是循环所包围的面积。然后你可以使用第一定律来找到净热传递。

# 8. Worked Examples / 典型例题

## Example 1: Applying the First Law to an Adiabatic Process / 将第一定律应用于绝热过程

### Question / 题目
**English:**
A gas is compressed rapidly in an insulated cylinder. The work done on the gas is 150 J. Calculate the change in internal energy of the gas. State whether the temperature increases or decreases.

**中文:**
在一个绝热气缸中，气体被快速压缩。外界对气体做功 150 J。计算气体内能的变化。说明温度是升高还是降低。

### Solution / 解答
**Step 1: Identify the process.**
The cylinder is "insulated", meaning no thermal energy transfer occurs. This is an **adiabatic process**.
Therefore, $Q = 0$.

**Step 2: Apply the sign convention.**
The problem states "work done *on* the gas". According to the sign convention, $W$ is positive.
Therefore, $W = +150 \text{ J}$.

**Step 3: Apply the First Law.**
$$ \Delta U = Q + W = 0 + 150 = 150 \text{ J} $$

**Step 4: Interpret the result.**
$\Delta U$ is positive, meaning the internal energy of the gas has increased. For an [[Ideal Gases|ideal gas]], internal energy is directly proportional to temperature ($U \propto T$). Therefore, the temperature of the gas increases.

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** $\Delta U = 150 \text{ J}$. The temperature increases. | **答案：** $\Delta U = 150 \text{ J}$。温度升高。

### Quick Tip / 提示
(EN+CN)
- **EN:** "Insulated" or "rapid" are keywords for an adiabatic process ($Q=0$).
- **CN:** “绝热”或“快速”是绝热过程 ($Q=0$) 的关键词。

## Example 2: Applying the First Law to an Isothermal Process / 将第一定律应用于等温过程

### Question / 题目
**English:**
An ideal gas expands isothermally, absorbing 200 J of thermal energy. Calculate the work done by the gas.

**中文:**
理想气体等温膨胀，吸收了 200 J 的热能。计算气体对外做的功。

### Solution / 解答
**Step 1: Identify the process.**
The process is "isothermal", meaning the temperature is constant. For an [[Ideal Gases|ideal gas]], if temperature is constant, internal energy is constant.
Therefore, $\Delta U = 0$.

**Step 2: Apply the sign convention.**
The gas "absorbs" thermal energy, so $Q$ is positive.
Therefore, $Q = +200 \text{ J}$.

**Step 3: Apply the First Law.**
$$ \Delta U = Q + W $$
$$ 0 = 200 + W $$
$$ W = -200 \text{ J} $$

**Step 4: Interpret the result.**
$W = -200 \text{ J}$ means that 200 J of work is done *by* the gas. The question asks for the "work done by the gas", which is $W_{by} = -W_{on} = -(-200) = 200 \text{ J}$.

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** Work done by the gas = 200 J. | **答案：** 气体对外做功 = 200 J。

### Quick Tip / 提示
(EN+CN)
- **EN:** "Isothermal" for an ideal gas means $\Delta U = 0$. The First Law then simplifies to $Q = -W$.
- **CN:** 对于理想气体，“等温”意味着 $\Delta U = 0$。第一定律简化为 $Q = -W$。

# 10. Practical Skills Connections / 实验技能链接

**English:**
While the First Law itself is not directly tested in a specific practical, the concepts are crucial for understanding experiments involving gases.
- **Measurement of Work:** In experiments where a gas is compressed or expands (e.g., using a syringe and pressure sensor), the work done can be estimated from the area under a $p$-$V$ graph. Students must be able to estimate this area.
- **Measurement of $\Delta U$:** For an [[Ideal Gases|ideal gas]], $\Delta U$ can be inferred from a temperature change ($\Delta U \propto \Delta T$). This links to practical skills in measuring temperature accurately.
- **Uncertainties:** When calculating $\Delta U$ from $Q$ and $W$, uncertainties in the measurements of pressure, volume, and temperature must be propagated to find the uncertainty in $\Delta U$.

**中文:**
虽然第一定律本身不在特定的实验中直接测试，但其概念对于理解涉及气体的实验至关重要。
- **功的测量：** 在气体被压缩或膨胀的实验中（例如，使用注射器和压强传感器），所做的功可以从 $p$-$V$ 图下的面积估算。学生必须能够估算这个面积。
- **$\Delta U$ 的测量：** 对于理想气体，$\Delta U$ 可以从温度变化推断出来 ($\Delta U \propto \Delta T$)。这联系到准确测量温度的实验技能。
- **不确定度：** 当从 $Q$ 和 $W$ 计算 $\Delta U$ 时，压强、体积和温度测量中的不确定度必须被传递，以找到 $\Delta U$ 的不确定度。
