# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This sub-topic introduces the three fundamental subatomic particles that make up all atoms: protons, neutrons, and electrons. It covers their key properties — mass, charge, and location within the atom — and establishes the foundational understanding needed for [[The Nuclear Model of the Atom]]. Understanding these particles is essential for explaining atomic structure, chemical behaviour, and nuclear reactions. This knowledge directly supports later topics such as [[Isotopes and Nuclear Reactions]] and [[Fundamental Particles]].

**中文:**
本子知识点介绍构成所有原子的三种基本亚原子粒子：质子、中子和电子。内容包括它们的关键性质——质量、电荷以及在原子中的位置——并为理解[[The Nuclear Model of the Atom]]奠定基础。理解这些粒子对于解释原子结构、化学行为和核反应至关重要。这些知识直接支持后续主题，如[[Isotopes and Nuclear Reactions]]和[[Fundamental Particles]]。

# 4. Key Concepts Explained / 关键概念详解

## 4.1 Properties of Subatomic Particles / 亚原子粒子的性质

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Atoms consist of three types of subatomic particles. Protons and neutrons are located in the [[The Nuclear Model of the Atom|nucleus]] at the centre of the atom, while electrons occupy the space around the nucleus in regions called orbitals. The key properties are summarised below:

| Particle | Symbol | Relative Mass | Relative Charge | Location |
|----------|--------|---------------|-----------------|----------|
| Proton | p⁺ | 1 | +1 | Nucleus |
| Neutron | n⁰ | 1 | 0 | Nucleus |
| Electron | e⁻ | 1/1836 (≈0) | -1 | Orbitals around nucleus |

The mass of an electron is so small that it is considered negligible in most calculations involving atomic mass. The charge of an electron is equal in magnitude but opposite in sign to that of a proton, making atoms electrically neutral when the number of electrons equals the number of protons.

**中文:**
原子由三种亚原子粒子组成。质子和中子位于原子中心的[[The Nuclear Model of the Atom|原子核]]中，而电子占据原子核周围称为轨道的区域。关键性质总结如下：

| 粒子 | 符号 | 相对质量 | 相对电荷 | 位置 |
|------|------|----------|----------|------|
| 质子 | p⁺ | 1 | +1 | 原子核 |
| 中子 | n⁰ | 1 | 0 | 原子核 |
| 电子 | e⁻ | 1/1836 (≈0) | -1 | 原子核周围轨道 |

电子的质量非常小，在大多数涉及原子质量的计算中被视为可忽略。电子的电荷大小与质子相等但符号相反，因此当电子数等于质子数时，原子呈电中性。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
- **Protons** determine the element's identity — changing the number of protons changes the element.
- **Neutrons** provide stability to the nucleus by contributing to the [[The Strong Nuclear Force|strong nuclear force]] without adding repulsive charge.
- **Electrons** determine the chemical properties of an atom and are responsible for bonding and electrical conductivity.

**中文:**
- **质子**决定元素的身份——改变质子数会改变元素种类。
- **中子**通过贡献[[The Strong Nuclear Force|强核力]]而不增加排斥电荷，为原子核提供稳定性。
- **电子**决定原子的化学性质，负责成键和导电性。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:**
  - ❌ "Electrons orbit the nucleus like planets around the Sun." → ✅ Electrons exist in probability clouds (orbitals), not fixed orbits.
  - ❌ "Neutrons have no mass." → ✅ Neutrons have almost the same mass as protons.
  - ❌ "The mass number is the same as the atomic mass in grams." → ✅ Mass number is a count of nucleons, not a mass in grams.
- **中文:**
  - ❌ "电子像行星绕太阳一样绕原子核运行。" → ✅ 电子存在于概率云（轨道）中，而非固定轨道。
  - ❌ "中子没有质量。" → ✅ 中子与质子质量几乎相同。
  - ❌ "质量数与以克为单位的原子质量相同。" → ✅ 质量数是核子计数，不是以克为单位的质量。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **English:** Always use relative masses and charges in calculations. Remember that the mass of an electron is negligible — do not include it when calculating the mass of an atom.
- **中文:** 在计算中始终使用相对质量和电荷。记住电子的质量可忽略——计算原子质量时不要将其包括在内。

## 4.2 Atomic Number and Mass Number / 原子序数与质量数

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
The **atomic number (Z)** is the number of protons in the nucleus. It uniquely identifies an element — all atoms of the same element have the same atomic number. For example, all carbon atoms have Z = 6.

The **mass number (A)** is the total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus. It is also called the nucleon number. The number of neutrons (N) can be found using:
$$N = A - Z$$

In a neutral atom, the number of electrons equals the number of protons (Z).

**中文:**
**原子序数 (Z)** 是原子核中的质子数。它唯一地标识一种元素——同一种元素的所有原子具有相同的原子序数。例如，所有碳原子都有 Z = 6。

**质量数 (A)** 是原子核中质子与中子的总数。也称为核子数。中子数 (N) 可通过下式求得：
$$N = A - Z$$

在中性原子中，电子数等于质子数 (Z)。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
- Z determines the element and its position in the periodic table.
- A determines the isotope of an element — atoms with the same Z but different A are [[Isotopes and Nuclear Reactions|isotopes]].
- The difference A - Z gives the number of neutrons, which affects nuclear stability.

**中文:**
- Z 决定元素及其在元素周期表中的位置。
- A 决定元素的同位素——具有相同 Z 但不同 A 的原子是[[Isotopes and Nuclear Reactions|同位素]]。
- A - Z 的差值给出中子数，影响核稳定性。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:**
  - ❌ "Atomic number is the number of neutrons." → ✅ Atomic number is the number of protons.
  - ❌ "Mass number is the mass of the atom in atomic mass units." → ✅ Mass number is a count, not a mass measurement.
- **中文:**
  - ❌ "原子序数是中子数。" → ✅ 原子序数是质子数。
  - ❌ "质量数是以原子质量单位表示的原子质量。" → ✅ 质量数是计数，不是质量测量。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **English:** When given Z and A, always calculate N = A - Z for neutron count. In nuclide notation, Z is at the bottom left and A is at the top left of the element symbol.
- **中文:** 当给出 Z 和 A 时，始终计算 N = A - Z 得到中子数。在核素符号中，Z 在元素符号的左下角，A 在左上角。

## 4.3 Nuclide Notation / 核素符号

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Nuclide notation is a standard way to represent a specific nucleus (nuclide). The general form is:
$$^{A}_{Z}X$$
where:
- X = chemical symbol of the element
- A = mass number (top left)
- Z = atomic number (bottom left)

For example, carbon-12 is written as $$^{12}_{6}C$$, meaning it has 6 protons and 6 neutrons (12 - 6 = 6).

For ions, the charge is written as a superscript on the right:
$$^{A}_{Z}X^{q}$$
where q is the charge (e.g., +2, -1).

**中文:**
核素符号是表示特定原子核（核素）的标准方式。一般形式为：
$$^{A}_{Z}X$$
其中：
- X = 元素的化学符号
- A = 质量数（左上角）
- Z = 原子序数（左下角）

例如，碳-12 写作 $$^{12}_{6}C$$，表示它有 6 个质子和 6 个中子（12 - 6 = 6）。

对于离子，电荷写在右上角：
$$^{A}_{Z}X^{q}$$
其中 q 是电荷（例如 +2, -1）。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
Nuclide notation provides a compact way to convey all essential information about a nucleus: which element it is, how many nucleons it has, and (for ions) its charge state.

**中文:**
核素符号提供了一种紧凑的方式传达关于原子核的所有基本信息：它是哪种元素、有多少核子，以及（对于离子）其电荷状态。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:**
  - ❌ "The mass number goes at the bottom." → ✅ Mass number (A) is at the top left; atomic number (Z) is at the bottom left.
  - ❌ "The charge is written at the top left too." → ✅ Charge is written as a superscript on the right.
- **中文:**
  - ❌ "质量数写在下面。" → ✅ 质量数 (A) 在左上角；原子序数 (Z) 在左下角。
  - ❌ "电荷也写在左上角。" → ✅ 电荷写在右上角。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **English:** Practice writing and interpreting nuclide notation quickly. Remember: A on top, Z on bottom, charge on right.
- **中文:** 练习快速书写和解读核素符号。记住：A 在上，Z 在下，电荷在右。

# 5. Essential Equations / 核心公式

## Equation 1: Number of Neutrons / 中子数公式

$$N = A - Z$$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| N | Number of neutrons | 中子数 | dimensionless (无量纲) |
| A | Mass number (nucleon number) | 质量数（核子数） | dimensionless (无量纲) |
| Z | Atomic number (proton number) | 原子序数（质子数） | dimensionless (无量纲) |

**Derivation / 推导:**
Mass number = number of protons + number of neutrons → A = Z + N → N = A - Z

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- **English:** Applies to all atoms and ions. For ions, the number of electrons changes but N remains the same.
- **中文:** 适用于所有原子和离子。对于离子，电子数改变但 N 保持不变。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
- **English:** This is a counting formula, not a mass formula. It does not account for mass defect or binding energy.
- **中文:** 这是一个计数公式，不是质量公式。它不考虑质量亏损或结合能。

---

## Equation 2: Neutral Atom Condition / 中性原子条件

$$\text{Number of electrons} = Z$$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Z | Atomic number | 原子序数 | dimensionless (无量纲) |

**Derivation / 推导:**
In a neutral atom, total positive charge = total negative charge → Z × (+e) = number of electrons × (-e) → number of electrons = Z

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- **English:** Only applies to neutral atoms. For ions, the number of electrons differs from Z.
- **中文:** 仅适用于中性原子。对于离子，电子数与 Z 不同。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
- **English:** Does not apply to ions or excited states where electrons may be lost or gained.
- **中文:** 不适用于可能失去或获得电子的离子或激发态。

# 8. Worked Examples / 典型例题

## Example 1: Determining Subatomic Particles / 确定亚原子粒子数量

### Question / 题目
**English:**
An atom of uranium-238 has the nuclide notation $$^{238}_{92}U$$. Determine:
(a) The number of protons
(b) The number of neutrons
(c) The number of electrons in a neutral atom

**中文:**
铀-238 原子的核素符号为 $$^{238}_{92}U$$。确定：
(a) 质子数
(b) 中子数
(c) 中性原子中的电子数

### Solution / 解答

**Step 1: Identify Z and A / 步骤1：识别 Z 和 A**
From the notation $$^{238}_{92}U$$:
- Atomic number Z = 92
- Mass number A = 238

**Step 2: Find number of protons / 步骤2：求质子数**
Number of protons = Z = 92

**Step 3: Find number of neutrons / 步骤3：求中子数**
$$N = A - Z = 238 - 92 = 146$$

**Step 4: Find number of electrons / 步骤4：求电子数**
For a neutral atom: Number of electrons = Z = 92

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** (a) 92 protons, (b) 146 neutrons, (c) 92 electrons | **答案：** (a) 92个质子，(b) 146个中子，(c) 92个电子

### Quick Tip / 提示
- **English:** Always check: A = Z + N. If you have two of these, you can find the third.
- **中文:** 始终检查：A = Z + N。如果你有其中两个，可以求出第三个。

---

## Example 2: Nuclide Notation for an Ion / 离子的核素符号

### Question / 题目
**English:**
A calcium ion has 20 protons, 20 neutrons, and 18 electrons. Write its nuclide notation.

**中文:**
一个钙离子有20个质子、20个中子和18个电子。写出其核素符号。

### Solution / 解答

**Step 1: Determine Z and A / 步骤1：确定 Z 和 A**
- Z = number of protons = 20
- A = number of protons + number of neutrons = 20 + 20 = 40

**Step 2: Determine the charge / 步骤2：确定电荷**
- Protons = 20 (charge +20)
- Electrons = 18 (charge -18)
- Net charge = +20 + (-18) = +2

**Step 3: Write the nuclide notation / 步骤3：写出核素符号**
$$^{40}_{20}Ca^{2+}$$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** $$^{40}_{20}Ca^{2+}$$ | **答案：** $$^{40}_{20}Ca^{2+}$$

### Quick Tip / 提示
- **English:** For ions, the charge is the difference between protons and electrons. Positive charge means fewer electrons than protons.
- **中文:** 对于离子，电荷是质子与电子之差。正电荷意味着电子少于质子。

# 10. Practical Skills Connections / 实验技能链接

**English:**
While this sub-topic is primarily theoretical, it connects to practical work in the following ways:

1. **Mass Spectrometry:** Understanding the mass and charge of particles is essential for interpreting mass spectra, which are used to determine isotopic abundances.
2. **Charge-to-Mass Ratio Experiments:** Historical experiments (e.g., Thomson's cathode ray tube) measured the charge-to-mass ratio of electrons, linking theory to experimental physics.
3. **Uncertainty in Measurements:** When measuring masses of atoms or particles, understanding the precision of instruments and calculating uncertainties is important.
4. **Graph Plotting:** Plotting N vs Z for stable nuclei requires data collection and graph interpretation skills.
