# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This sub-topic explores the dual nature of ionising radiation: its beneficial applications in medicine, industry, and research, alongside its potential dangers to living organisms. Understanding the balance between risk and benefit is crucial for safe and effective use. We will examine how the different properties of [[Alpha, Beta and Gamma Radiation]] determine their suitability for specific uses (e.g., smoke detectors, medical tracers, sterilisation) and the nature of the hazards they pose (e.g., tissue damage, cancer). This knowledge is essential for evaluating safety protocols and making informed decisions about radiation exposure.

**中文:**
本子知识点探讨电离辐射的双重性：其在医学、工业和科研中的有益应用，以及对生物体构成的潜在危害。理解风险与收益之间的平衡对于安全有效地使用辐射至关重要。我们将研究[[Alpha, Beta and Gamma Radiation]]的不同特性如何决定其特定用途的适用性（例如，烟雾探测器、医用示踪剂、灭菌），以及它们所构成危害的性质（例如，组织损伤、癌症）。这些知识对于评估安全规程和就辐射暴露做出明智决策至关重要。

# 4. Key Concepts Explained / 关键概念详解

## 4.1 Biological Effects of Ionising Radiation / 电离辐射的生物效应

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** Ionising radiation damages living tissue primarily by ionising atoms and molecules within cells. This can directly damage DNA (direct action) or create highly reactive free radicals (e.g., from water) that then damage DNA and other cellular components (indirect action). The effects can be:
- **Somatic:** Affecting the exposed individual (e.g., radiation sickness, burns, cancer).
- **Genetic:** Affecting future generations through damage to reproductive cells (e.g., mutations).
- **Acute:** Occurring shortly after a high dose (e.g., nausea, hair loss).
- **Chronic/Latent:** Occurring years after exposure (e.g., cancer).

**中文:** 电离辐射主要通过电离细胞内的原子和分子来损伤活组织。这可以直接损伤DNA（直接作用），或产生高活性自由基（例如来自水），然后自由基再损伤DNA和其他细胞成分（间接作用）。其影响可以是：
- **躯体效应：** 影响受照个体（例如，辐射病、烧伤、癌症）。
- **遗传效应：** 通过损伤生殖细胞影响后代（例如，突变）。
- **急性效应：** 在高剂量照射后短时间内发生（例如，恶心、脱发）。
- **慢性/潜伏效应：** 在照射多年后发生（例如，癌症）。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** The key is the *ionisation* process. The energy deposited by radiation disrupts chemical bonds, leading to biological damage. The denser the ionisation along the track (high LET – Linear Energy Transfer), the greater the biological damage per unit absorbed dose. Alpha particles have very high LET, gamma rays have low LET.

**中文:** 关键在于*电离*过程。辐射沉积的能量会破坏化学键，导致生物损伤。沿着径迹的电离越密集（高LET——线性能量转移），每单位吸收剂量造成的生物损伤就越大。α粒子具有非常高的LET，γ射线具有低LET。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **Misconception:** All radiation is equally dangerous.
  **Truth:** Alpha is extremely dangerous *internally* (if ingested/inhaled) but harmless externally. Gamma is dangerous both internally and externally.
- **Misconception:** Any exposure to radiation causes cancer.
  **Truth:** The risk is dose-dependent. Low doses have a very low probability of causing harm. The body has repair mechanisms.
- **Misconception:** Irradiated objects become radioactive.
  **Truth:** Exposure to radiation does *not* make an object radioactive. Only contamination with radioactive material does.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **EN:** Be able to explain why alpha emitters are particularly hazardous if ingested (high ionising power, short range means all energy is deposited in a small volume of tissue).
- **CN:** 能够解释为什么α发射体如果被摄入体内特别危险（高电离能力，短射程意味着所有能量都沉积在小体积的组织中）。

## 4.2 Absorbed Dose vs. Equivalent Dose / 吸收剂量与等效剂量

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** Absorbed dose (D) is a purely physical quantity: the energy deposited per unit mass. However, different types of radiation cause different amounts of biological damage for the same absorbed dose. For example, 1 Gy of alpha radiation is about 20 times more damaging than 1 Gy of gamma radiation. To account for this, we use equivalent dose (H), which is the absorbed dose multiplied by a radiation weighting factor (wR).

$$ H = D \times w_R $$

- For gamma and beta: wR = 1
- For alpha: wR = 20
- For neutrons: wR varies with energy (typically 5-20)

**中文:** 吸收剂量 (D) 是一个纯粹的物理量：单位质量沉积的能量。然而，对于相同的吸收剂量，不同类型的辐射会造成不同量的生物损伤。例如，1 Gy 的 α 辐射的破坏性大约是 1 Gy 的 γ 辐射的 20 倍。为了解释这一点，我们使用等效剂量 (H)，即吸收剂量乘以辐射权重因子 (wR)。

$$ H = D \times w_R $$

- 对于伽马和贝塔：wR = 1
- 对于阿尔法：wR = 20
- 对于中子：wR 随能量变化（通常为 5-20）

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** Equivalent dose is a measure of the *biological effect* of radiation, not just the energy deposited. The sievert (Sv) is the unit that allows us to compare the risk from different types of radiation.

**中文:** 等效剂量是辐射*生物效应*的量度，而不仅仅是沉积的能量。西弗特 (Sv) 是允许我们比较不同类型辐射风险的单位。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **Misconception:** 1 Gy = 1 Sv.
  **Truth:** This is only true for gamma and beta radiation (where wR = 1). For alpha radiation, 1 Gy = 20 Sv.
- **Misconception:** Equivalent dose is the final measure of risk.
  **Truth:** Effective dose (which also accounts for the sensitivity of different organs) is a better measure of overall risk.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **EN:** You may be asked to calculate equivalent dose from absorbed dose and vice versa. Always check the type of radiation.
- **CN:** 你可能会被要求从吸收剂量计算等效剂量，反之亦然。始终检查辐射类型。

# 5. Essential Equations / 核心公式

## 5.1 Equivalent Dose / 等效剂量

$$ H = D \times w_R $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $H$ | Equivalent dose | 等效剂量 | Sv (sievert / 西弗特) |
| $D$ | Absorbed dose | 吸收剂量 | Gy (gray / 戈瑞) |
| $w_R$ | Radiation weighting factor | 辐射权重因子 | No unit (无量纲) |

**Conditions / 适用条件:** Always applicable when comparing biological effects of different radiations.
**Limitations / 局限性:** wR values are averages; the actual biological effect can depend on other factors (e.g., dose rate, energy of radiation).

## 5.2 Effective Dose / 有效剂量

$$ E = H \times w_T $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $E$ | Effective dose | 有效剂量 | Sv (sievert / 西弗特) |
| $H$ | Equivalent dose | 等效剂量 | Sv |
| $w_T$ | Tissue weighting factor | 组织权重因子 | No unit (无量纲) |

**Conditions / 适用条件:** Used to calculate the overall risk to a whole organism from partial exposure.
**Limitations / 局限性:** wT values are population averages; individual sensitivity can vary.

# 6. Graphs and Relationships / 图表与关系

## 6.1 Dose-Response Curve for Radiation / 辐射剂量-反应曲线

### Axes / 坐标轴
- **X-axis:** Dose (Sv) / 剂量 (Sv)
- **Y-axis:** Probability of Effect (e.g., cancer) / 效应概率 (例如癌症)

### Shape / 形状
**English:** For stochastic effects (e.g., cancer), the curve is often modelled as a linear no-threshold (LNT) model. This means there is no safe dose; any dose, no matter how small, carries some risk. The risk is proportional to the dose. For deterministic effects (e.g., radiation sickness), there is a threshold dose below which no effect is seen.

**中文:** 对于随机性效应（例如癌症），该曲线通常被建模为线性无阈值 (LNT) 模型。这意味着没有安全剂量；任何剂量，无论多小，都带有一些风险。风险与剂量成正比。对于确定性效应（例如辐射病），存在一个阈值剂量，低于该剂量则看不到效应。

### Gradient Meaning / 斜率含义
**English:** The gradient represents the risk per unit dose (e.g., cancer risk per Sv).

**中文:** 斜率代表每单位剂量的风险（例如，每 Sv 的癌症风险）。

### Exam Interpretation / 考试解读
**EN:** Be able to sketch the LNT curve and explain its implications for radiation safety (i.e., the ALARA principle). Contrast it with a threshold curve.
**CN:** 能够绘制 LNT 曲线并解释其对辐射安全的影响（即 ALARA 原则）。将其与阈值曲线进行对比。

# 8. Worked Examples / 典型例题

## Example 1: Calculating Equivalent Dose / 计算等效剂量

### Question / 题目
**English:** A worker receives an absorbed dose of 2.0 mGy from gamma radiation and 0.5 mGy from alpha radiation. Calculate the total equivalent dose.
**中文:** 一名工人受到 2.0 mGy 的伽马辐射和 0.5 mGy 的阿尔法辐射的吸收剂量。计算总等效剂量。

### Solution / 解答
**Step 1: Calculate equivalent dose from gamma.**
$$ H_{\gamma} = D_{\gamma} \times w_{R,\gamma} = 2.0 \text{ mGy} \times 1 = 2.0 \text{ mSv} $$

**Step 2: Calculate equivalent dose from alpha.**
$$ H_{\alpha} = D_{\alpha} \times w_{R,\alpha} = 0.5 \text{ mGy} \times 20 = 10 \text{ mSv} $$

**Step 3: Add them together.**
$$ H_{total} = H_{\gamma} + H_{\alpha} = 2.0 \text{ mSv} + 10 \text{ mSv} = 12 \text{ mSv} $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** 12 mSv | **答案：** 12 mSv

### Quick Tip / 提示
**EN:** Always convert to the same units (e.g., mGy to mSv) and remember the wR for alpha is 20.
**CN:** 始终转换为相同单位（例如，mGy 到 mSv），并记住 α 的 wR 是 20。

## Example 2: Choosing a Source for Medical Tracer / 为医用示踪剂选择放射源

### Question / 题目
**English:** A hospital needs a radioactive tracer for imaging a patient's kidneys. The tracer must be detectable outside the body and must not deliver a high dose to the patient. Explain why a gamma emitter with a short half-life is chosen.
**中文:** 一家医院需要一种放射性示踪剂来对患者的肾脏进行成像。示踪剂必须在体外可检测到，并且不能给患者带来高剂量。解释为什么选择具有短半衰期的伽马发射体。

### Solution / 解答
**Step 1: Detectability.**
Gamma radiation is highly penetrating and can easily pass through the body to be detected by an external gamma camera. Alpha and beta particles would be absorbed by the body and not be detectable externally.

**Step 2: Minimising patient dose.**
A short half-life means the activity of the tracer decreases rapidly. This ensures the patient is not exposed to radiation for a long time after the imaging is complete. The low ionising power of gamma (wR = 1) also means a lower equivalent dose compared to alpha for the same absorbed dose.

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** A gamma emitter is chosen for its high penetration (detectable externally), and a short half-life is chosen to minimise the duration of radiation exposure to the patient. | **答案：** 选择伽马发射体是因为其高穿透力（可在体外检测到），选择短半衰期是为了最大限度地减少患者受到辐射暴露的持续时间。

### Quick Tip / 提示
**EN:** Always link the *property* of the radiation (penetration, ionising power, half-life) to the *requirement* of the application.
**CN:** 始终将辐射的*特性*（穿透力、电离能力、半衰期）与应用*要求*联系起来。

# 10. Practical Skills Connections / 实验技能链接

**English:**
- **Handling Sources:** You must know the correct procedures for handling radioactive sources in the lab (use tongs, point source away from body, minimise time, maximise distance, use shielding).
- **GM Tube Experiment:** The [[Absorption and Penetration]] experiment (measuring count rate through different absorbers) is directly linked to understanding which type of radiation is suitable for which application.
- **Background Radiation:** You must account for background radiation in any experiment involving radioactive sources. This is a key uncertainty.
- **Safety Risk Assessment:** You should be able to write a simple risk assessment for an experiment involving a radioactive source, identifying hazards (exposure, contamination) and control measures (ALARA).

**中文:**
- **处理放射源：** 你必须知道在实验室中处理放射源的正确程序（使用镊子，将源指向远离身体的方向，最小化时间，最大化距离，使用屏蔽）。
- **盖革-米勒管实验：** [[Absorption and Penetration]] 实验（测量通过不同吸收体的计数率）与理解哪种辐射适用于哪种应用直接相关。
- **本底辐射：** 在任何涉及放射源的实验中，你必须考虑本底辐射。这是一个关键的不确定度。
- **安全风险评估：** 你应该能够为涉及放射源的实验编写简单的风险评估，识别危害（暴露、污染）和控制措施（ALARA）。
