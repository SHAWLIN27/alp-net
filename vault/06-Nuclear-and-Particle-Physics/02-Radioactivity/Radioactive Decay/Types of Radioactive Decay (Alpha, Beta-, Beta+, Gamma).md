# Types of Radioactive Decay (Alpha, Beta-, Beta+, Gamma)
# 放射性衰变类型 (α, β⁻, β⁺, γ)

---

# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This sub-topic explores the four main types of radioactive decay: alpha (α), beta-minus (β⁻), beta-plus (β⁺), and gamma (γ). Each decay type involves a different transformation of the atomic nucleus, governed by the fundamental forces of nature. Understanding these decay modes is essential for explaining nuclear stability, predicting the products of radioactive decay, and applying nuclear physics to real-world contexts such as medical imaging, radiotherapy, and nuclear energy.

This leaf node builds directly on [[Atomic Structure and the Nucleus]] and serves as the foundation for [[Decay Equations and Conservation]], [[Neutrinos and Antineutrinos]], and [[The Exponential Decay Law]]. It is a core component of the broader [[Radioactive Decay]] topic.

**中文:**
本子知识点探讨四种主要的放射性衰变类型：α衰变、β⁻衰变、β⁺衰变和γ衰变。每种衰变类型都涉及原子核的不同转变，受自然基本力支配。理解这些衰变模式对于解释核稳定性、预测放射性衰变产物以及将核物理应用于医学成像、放射治疗和核能等现实场景至关重要。

本叶节点直接建立在[[原子结构与原子核]]的基础上，并为[[衰变方程与守恒定律]]、[[中微子与反中微子]]和[[指数衰变定律]]奠定基础。它是更广泛的[[放射性衰变]]主题的核心组成部分。

|--------------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| **Alpha (α) decay** / α衰变 | The emission of an alpha particle (⁴₂He nucleus) from an unstable nucleus, decreasing A by 4 and Z by 2. | 不稳定的原子核发射出一个α粒子（⁴₂He原子核），A减少4，Z减少2。 | Confusing α particle with helium atom (it's a nucleus, not an atom). |

# 4. Key Concepts Explained / 关键概念详解

## 4.1 Alpha (α) Decay / α衰变

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Alpha decay occurs in heavy, unstable nuclei (typically Z > 82). The nucleus emits an alpha particle, which is identical to a helium-4 nucleus (2 protons + 2 neutrons). The parent nucleus loses 4 nucleons, so its nucleon number (A) decreases by 4 and its proton number (Z) decreases by 2. The daughter nucleus is a different element, located two places to the left in the periodic table.

The general equation is:
$$ \text{Parent} \rightarrow \text{Daughter} + \alpha + \text{energy} $$
$$ ^A_Z X \rightarrow ^{A-4}_{Z-2} Y + ^4_2 \text{He} $$

Alpha particles are highly ionising but have a short range in air (a few cm) and are stopped by a sheet of paper. They are deflected by electric and magnetic fields (towards the negative plate in an electric field, since they are positively charged).

**中文:**
α衰变发生在重的不稳定原子核中（通常Z > 82）。原子核发射出一个α粒子，它与氦-4原子核（2个质子+2个中子）相同。母核失去4个核子，因此其核子数（A）减少4，质子数（Z）减少2。子核是一种不同的元素，在元素周期表中向左移动两位。

一般方程为：
$$ \text{母核} \rightarrow \text{子核} + \alpha + \text{能量} $$
$$ ^A_Z X \rightarrow ^{A-4}_{Z-2} Y + ^4_2 \text{He} $$

α粒子电离能力强，但在空气中的射程短（几厘米），一张纸即可阻挡。它们在电场和磁场中会发生偏转（在电场中偏向负极板，因为它们带正电）。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
Alpha decay is a manifestation of the strong nuclear force being unable to overcome the electrostatic repulsion between protons in very large nuclei. The emission of an alpha particle reduces the size of the nucleus, making it more stable.

**中文:**
α衰变是强核力无法克服非常大原子核中质子间静电排斥的表现。发射α粒子减小了原子核的尺寸，使其更加稳定。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- ❌ **"Alpha particles are helium atoms."** → They are helium *nuclei* (no electrons).
- ❌ **"Alpha decay changes the element by 4 places."** → It changes by 2 places (Z decreases by 2).
- ❌ **"Alpha particles are the most penetrating."** → They are the *least* penetrating.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- ✅ Always write the alpha particle as $^4_2 \text{He}$ or $^4_2 \alpha$.
- ✅ Remember: A decreases by 4, Z decreases by 2.
- ✅ Alpha particles are deflected *less* than beta particles in a magnetic field (due to larger mass).

## 4.2 Beta-Minus (β⁻) Decay / β⁻衰变

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Beta-minus decay occurs in neutron-rich nuclei. A neutron in the nucleus is converted into a proton, an electron (the β⁻ particle), and an antineutrino (ν̄ₑ). The electron and antineutrino are emitted from the nucleus. The proton number (Z) increases by 1, while the nucleon number (A) remains unchanged. The daughter nucleus is one place to the right in the periodic table.

The general equation is:
$$ ^A_Z X \rightarrow ^A_{Z+1} Y + ^0_{-1} e + \bar{\nu}_e $$

The underlying quark transformation is:
$$ d \rightarrow u + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e $$

Beta-minus particles are moderately ionising and have a range of a few metres in air. They are stopped by a few mm of aluminium. They are deflected by electric and magnetic fields (towards the positive plate in an electric field, since they are negatively charged).

**中文:**
β⁻衰变发生在中子过多的原子核中。原子核内的一个中子转化为一个质子、一个电子（β⁻粒子）和一个反中微子（ν̄ₑ）。电子和反中微子从原子核中发射出来。质子数（Z）增加1，而核子数（A）保持不变。子核在元素周期表中向右移动一位。

一般方程为：
$$ ^A_Z X \rightarrow ^A_{Z+1} Y + ^0_{-1} e + \bar{\nu}_e $$

底层的夸克变换为：
$$ d \rightarrow u + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e $$

β⁻粒子电离能力中等，在空气中的射程为几米。几毫米厚的铝板即可阻挡。它们在电场和磁场中会发生偏转（在电场中偏向正极板，因为它们带负电）。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
Beta-minus decay is mediated by the [[weak nuclear force]]. The conversion of a down quark to an up quark changes a neutron into a proton, increasing the atomic number. The antineutrino is emitted to conserve lepton number and energy-momentum.

**中文:**
β⁻衰变由[[弱核力]]介导。下夸克转化为上夸克使中子变为质子，原子序数增加。反中微子的发射是为了守恒轻子数和能量动量。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- ❌ **"The electron comes from the electron cloud."** → It comes from the *nucleus* (neutron conversion).
- ❌ **"Only an electron is emitted."** → An antineutrino is also emitted.
- ❌ **"Beta particles are the same as cathode rays."** → They are both electrons, but beta particles come from the nucleus.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- ✅ Always include the antineutrino in the equation (even if not explicitly asked).
- ✅ Remember: Z increases by 1, A is unchanged.
- ✅ Beta particles are deflected *more* than alpha particles in a magnetic field (due to smaller mass).

## 4.3 Beta-Plus (β⁺) Decay / β⁺衰变

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Beta-plus decay occurs in proton-rich nuclei. A proton in the nucleus is converted into a neutron, a positron (the β⁺ particle), and a neutrino (νₑ). The positron and neutrino are emitted from the nucleus. The proton number (Z) decreases by 1, while the nucleon number (A) remains unchanged. The daughter nucleus is one place to the left in the periodic table.

The general equation is:
$$ ^A_Z X \rightarrow ^A_{Z-1} Y + ^0_{+1} e + \nu_e $$

The underlying quark transformation is:
$$ u \rightarrow d + e^+ + \nu_e $$

Beta-plus particles (positrons) are the antiparticles of electrons. They have the same mass as electrons but opposite charge. When a positron meets an electron, they annihilate, producing two gamma photons. This is the basis of PET (Positron Emission Tomography) scans.

**中文:**
β⁺衰变发生在质子过多的原子核中。原子核内的一个质子转化为一个中子、一个正电子（β⁺粒子）和一个中微子（νₑ）。正电子和中微子从原子核中发射出来。质子数（Z）减少1，而核子数（A）保持不变。子核在元素周期表中向左移动一位。

一般方程为：
$$ ^A_Z X \rightarrow ^A_{Z-1} Y + ^0_{+1} e + \nu_e $$

底层的夸克变换为：
$$ u \rightarrow d + e^+ + \nu_e $$

β⁺粒子（正电子）是电子的反粒子。它们与电子质量相同但电荷相反。当正电子遇到电子时，它们会湮灭，产生两个γ光子。这是PET（正电子发射断层扫描）的基础。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
Beta-plus decay is also mediated by the [[weak nuclear force]]. The conversion of an up quark to a down quark changes a proton into a neutron. The neutrino is emitted to conserve lepton number. Positron emission is a form of antimatter production.

**中文:**
β⁺衰变也由[[弱核力]]介导。上夸克转化为下夸克使质子变为中子。中微子的发射是为了守恒轻子数。正电子发射是一种反物质产生形式。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- ❌ **"Beta-plus decay is the same as beta-minus decay."** → They are different processes (proton→neutron vs neutron→proton).
- ❌ **"Positrons are protons."** → Positrons are antielectrons, not protons.
- ❌ **"No neutrino is emitted."** → A neutrino is always emitted in β⁺ decay.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- ✅ Always include the neutrino in the equation.
- ✅ Remember: Z decreases by 1, A is unchanged.
- ✅ β⁺ decay only occurs in proton-rich nuclei (often artificially produced).

> 📋 **Edexcel Only:** Edexcel specifically requires you to distinguish between β⁻ and β⁺ decay in terms of the particles emitted and the changes to the nucleus. You may be asked to write quark-level equations.

## 4.4 Gamma (γ) Decay / γ衰变

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Gamma decay occurs when a nucleus is in an excited energy state (often following α or β decay). The nucleus de-excites by emitting a high-energy photon (gamma ray). There is no change in the proton number (Z) or nucleon number (A) — only the energy state of the nucleus changes.

The general equation is:
$$ ^A_Z X^* \rightarrow ^A_Z X + \gamma $$

The asterisk (*) denotes an excited state of the nucleus.

Gamma rays are electromagnetic radiation with very high frequency (typically > 10¹⁹ Hz) and very short wavelength. They are weakly ionising but highly penetrating — they can pass through several cm of lead and require thick concrete for shielding. They are not deflected by electric or magnetic fields (they are neutral photons).

**中文:**
γ衰变发生在原子核处于激发态时（通常在α或β衰变之后）。原子核通过发射高能光子（γ射线）退激。质子数（Z）和核子数（A）不变——只有原子核的能量状态发生变化。

一般方程为：
$$ ^A_Z X^* \rightarrow ^A_Z X + \gamma $$

星号（*）表示原子核的激发态。

γ射线是频率非常高（通常 > 10¹⁹ Hz）、波长非常短的电磁辐射。它们电离能力弱但穿透力强——可以穿透几厘米厚的铅板，需要厚混凝土进行屏蔽。它们不受电场或磁场偏转（它们是中性光子）。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
Gamma decay is analogous to the emission of a photon when an electron in an atom drops to a lower energy level. However, the energy differences in the nucleus are much larger (MeV scale vs eV scale for atomic transitions), so gamma rays are much more energetic than X-rays or visible light.

**中文:**
γ衰变类似于原子中电子跃迁到较低能级时发射光子。然而，原子核中的能量差要大得多（MeV量级 vs 原子跃迁的eV量级），因此γ射线比X射线或可见光能量高得多。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- ❌ **"Gamma decay changes the element."** → No change in Z or A.
- ❌ **"Gamma rays are particles."** → They are electromagnetic waves (photons).
- ❌ **"Gamma rays are the same as X-rays."** → Gamma rays come from the nucleus; X-rays come from atomic electron transitions.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- ✅ Gamma decay often follows α or β decay — the daughter nucleus is left in an excited state.
- ✅ Gamma rays are the most penetrating but least ionising.
- ✅ No deflection in electric/magnetic fields.

# 5. Essential Equations / 核心公式

## 5.1 Alpha Decay Equation / α衰变方程

$$ ^A_Z X \rightarrow ^{A-4}_{Z-2} Y + ^4_2 \alpha $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $^A_Z X$ | Parent nucleus | 母核 | - |
| $^{A-4}_{Z-2} Y$ | Daughter nucleus | 子核 | - |
| $^4_2 \alpha$ | Alpha particle (⁴₂He) | α粒子 | - |

**Conditions / 适用条件:** Heavy nuclei (Z > 82) with too many nucleons. | 重原子核（Z > 82），核子过多。

## 5.2 Beta-Minus Decay Equation / β⁻衰变方程

$$ ^A_Z X \rightarrow ^A_{Z+1} Y + ^0_{-1} e + \bar{\nu}_e $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $^0_{-1} e$ | Beta-minus particle (electron) | β⁻粒子（电子） | - |
| $\bar{\nu}_e$ | Electron antineutrino | 电子反中微子 | - |

**Conditions / 适用条件:** Neutron-rich nuclei (N/Z ratio too high). | 中子过多的原子核（N/Z比过高）。

## 5.3 Beta-Plus Decay Equation / β⁺衰变方程

$$ ^A_Z X \rightarrow ^A_{Z-1} Y + ^0_{+1} e + \nu_e $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $^0_{+1} e$ | Beta-plus particle (positron) | β⁺粒子（正电子） | - |
| $\nu_e$ | Electron neutrino | 电子中微子 | - |

**Conditions / 适用条件:** Proton-rich nuclei (N/Z ratio too low). | 质子过多的原子核（N/Z比过低）。

## 5.4 Gamma Decay Equation / γ衰变方程

$$ ^A_Z X^* \rightarrow ^A_Z X + \gamma $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $X^*$ | Excited nucleus | 激发态原子核 | - |
| $\gamma$ | Gamma photon | γ光子 | - |

**Conditions / 适用条件:** Nucleus in an excited energy state (often after α or β decay). | 原子核处于激发态（通常在α或β衰变后）。

# 6. Graphs and Relationships / 图表与关系

## 6.1 N-Z Plot (Segrè Chart) / N-Z图（核素图）

### Axes / 坐标轴
- **X-axis:** Proton number (Z) / 质子数 (Z)
- **Y-axis:** Neutron number (N) / 中子数 (N)

### Shape / 形状
A scatter plot of stable nuclei forms a "valley of stability". Unstable nuclei lie above (neutron-rich) or below (proton-rich) this valley.

### Interpretation / 解读
- Nuclei above the valley of stability (too many neutrons) → β⁻ decay
- Nuclei below the valley of stability (too many protons) → β⁺ decay or electron capture
- Heavy nuclei at the end of the valley → α decay

### Exam Importance / 考试重要性
You may be asked to predict the type of decay based on the position of a nucleus on the N-Z plot.

# 8. Worked Examples / 典型例题

## Example 1: Identifying Decay Type / 识别衰变类型

### Question / 题目
**English:**
A radioactive nucleus of Polonium-210 ($^{210}_{84}\text{Po}$) decays to Lead-206 ($^{206}_{82}\text{Pb}$). Identify the type of decay and write a balanced nuclear equation.

**中文:**
一个钋-210 ($^{210}_{84}\text{Po}$) 放射性原子核衰变为铅-206 ($^{206}_{82}\text{Pb}$)。识别衰变类型并写出平衡的核方程。

### Solution / 解答

**Step 1: Determine the changes in Z and A / 确定Z和A的变化**
- Initial: Z = 84, A = 210
- Final: Z = 82, A = 206
- Change: ΔZ = 82 - 84 = -2, ΔA = 206 - 210 = -4

**Step 2: Identify the decay type / 识别衰变类型**
A decrease of 4 in A and 2 in Z is characteristic of **alpha decay**.

**Step 3: Write the equation / 写出方程**
$$ ^{210}_{84}\text{Po} \rightarrow ^{206}_{82}\text{Pb} + ^4_2\alpha $$

**Step 4: Verify conservation / 验证守恒**
- Nucleon number: 210 = 206 + 4 ✓
- Proton number: 84 = 82 + 2 ✓

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** Alpha decay | **答案：** α衰变
$$ ^{210}_{84}\text{Po} \rightarrow ^{206}_{82}\text{Pb} + ^4_2\alpha $$

### Quick Tip / 提示
**English:** If A decreases by 4 and Z decreases by 2, it's always alpha decay. | **中文：** 如果A减少4且Z减少2，则一定是α衰变。

## Example 2: Beta-Minus Decay Equation / β⁻衰变方程

### Question / 题目
**English:**
Carbon-14 ($^{14}_{6}\text{C}$) undergoes beta-minus decay. Write the balanced nuclear equation, including the antineutrino. Identify the daughter nucleus.

**中文:**
碳-14 ($^{14}_{6}\text{C}$) 发生β⁻衰变。写出平衡的核方程，包括反中微子。识别子核。

### Solution / 解答

**Step 1: Determine the changes / 确定变化**
- β⁻ decay: Z increases by 1, A unchanged
- New Z = 6 + 1 = 7
- New A = 14 (unchanged)

**Step 2: Identify the daughter nucleus / 识别子核**
- Element with Z = 7 is Nitrogen (N)
- Daughter nucleus: $^{14}_{7}\text{N}$

**Step 3: Write the equation / 写出方程**
$$ ^{14}_{6}\text{C} \rightarrow ^{14}_{7}\text{N} + ^0_{-1}e + \bar{\nu}_e $$

**Step 4: Verify conservation / 验证守恒**
- Nucleon number: 14 = 14 + 0 + 0 ✓
- Proton number: 6 = 7 - 1 + 0 ✓
- Charge: 0 = 0 - 1 + 1 ✓ (antineutrino is neutral)

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** $^{14}_{6}\text{C} \rightarrow ^{14}_{7}\text{N} + ^0_{-1}e + \bar{\nu}_e$ | **答案：** $^{14}_{6}\text{C} \rightarrow ^{14}_{7}\text{N} + ^0_{-1}e + \bar{\nu}_e$

### Quick Tip / 提示
**English:** In β⁻ decay, the daughter nucleus has the same A but Z increases by 1. Always include the antineutrino! | **中文：** 在β⁻衰变中，子核的A相同但Z增加1。始终包含反中微子！

# 10. Practical Skills Connections / 实验技能链接

**English:**
This sub-topic connects to practical work in several ways:

1. **Cloud Chamber Experiment:** Observing tracks of α, β, and γ radiation. You should be able to identify radiation types from track characteristics (thickness, length, curvature).

2. **Absorption Experiment:** Measuring the penetration of different radiations through various materials (paper, aluminium, lead). You would plot a graph of count rate against absorber thickness.

3. **Deflection Experiment:** Using electric or magnetic fields to deflect radiation and identify charge and mass.

4. **GM Tube and Counter:** Using a Geiger-Müller tube to detect radiation and measure count rates. Understanding that GM tubes cannot distinguish between radiation types without additional absorbers.

5. **Uncertainties:** When measuring count rates, you must account for background radiation and statistical fluctuations. Count rate follows Poisson statistics, so the uncertainty is $\sqrt{N}$ where N is the number of counts.

**中文:**
本子知识点通过多种方式与实验工作联系：

1. **云室实验：** 观察α、β和γ辐射的径迹。你应该能够根据径迹特征（粗细、长度、弯曲程度）识别辐射类型。

2. **吸收实验：** 测量不同辐射通过各种材料（纸、铝、铅）的穿透能力。你将绘制计数率与吸收体厚度的关系图。

3. **偏转实验：** 使用电场或磁场偏转辐射，识别电荷和质量。

4. **GM管和计数器：** 使用盖革-米勒管检测辐射并测量计数率。理解GM管无法在没有额外吸收体的情况下区分辐射类型。

5. **不确定度：** 测量计数率时，必须考虑本底辐射和统计涨落。计数率遵循泊松统计，因此不确定度为$\sqrt{N}$，其中N是计数。

# 12. Quick Revision Sheet / 速查表

| Category / 类别 | Key Points / 要点 |
|----------------|------------------|
| **Definition / 定义** | Radioactive decay is the spontaneous, random emission of particles or photons from an unstable nucleus. / 放射性衰变是不稳定原子核自发、随机地发射粒子或光子。 |
| **Alpha (α) / α衰变** | $^A_Z X \rightarrow ^{A-4}_{Z-2} Y + ^4_2 \alpha$ <br> High ionisation, short range (paper). / 电离能力强，射程短（纸）。 |
| **Beta-minus (β⁻) / β⁻衰变** | $^A_Z X \rightarrow ^A_{Z+1} Y + ^0_{-1} e + \bar{\nu}_e$ <br> Moderate ionisation, medium range (Al). / 电离能力中等，射程中等（铝）。 |
| **Beta-plus (β⁺) / β⁺衰变** | $^A_Z X \rightarrow ^A_{Z-1} Y + ^0_{+1} e + \nu_e$ <br> Positron emission, antimatter. / 正电子发射，反物质。 |
| **Gamma (γ) / γ衰变** | $^A_Z X^* \rightarrow ^A_Z X + \gamma$ <br> Low ionisation, high penetration (Pb/concrete). / 电离能力弱，穿透力强（铅/混凝土）。 |
| **Key Formula / 核心公式** | α: A-4, Z-2 <br> β⁻: A same, Z+1 <br> β⁺: A same, Z-1 <br> γ: No change |
| **Key Graph / 核心图表** | N-Z plot: β⁻ above stability, β⁺ below, α at high Z. / N-Z图：β⁻在稳定线上方，β⁺在下方，α在高Z处。 |
| **Exam Tip / 考试提示** | Always include neutrinos/antineutrinos in β decay equations. Know deflection directions in E and B fields. / 始终在β衰变方程中包含中微子/反中微子。知道在电场和磁场中的偏转方向。 |
| **Common Mistake / 常见错误** | Thinking β particles come from electron cloud (they come from nucleus). / 认为β粒子来自电子云（它们来自原子核）。 |
| **Practical Link / 实验联系** | Cloud chamber tracks: α (short, thick), β (long, thin, curved), γ (faint). / 云室径迹：α（短、粗），β（长、细、弯曲），γ（模糊）。 |
