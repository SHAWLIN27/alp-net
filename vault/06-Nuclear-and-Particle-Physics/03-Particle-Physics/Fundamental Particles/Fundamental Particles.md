# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This topic, Fundamental Particles, is the cornerstone of modern particle physics. It explores the ultimate building blocks of matter and the forces that govern their interactions. We move beyond the classical model of the atom to investigate the subatomic world of quarks, leptons, and the bosons that mediate fundamental forces. This study is crucial for understanding the [[Standard Model]] of particle physics, which is our most successful theoretical framework for describing the universe at its most fundamental level.

In the context of Cambridge 9702 and Edexcel IAL A-Level Physics, this topic introduces the particle zoo, the classification of particles into hadrons and leptons, and the conservation laws (baryon number, lepton number, charge, and strangeness) that dictate which particle interactions are possible. Real-world applications include the operation of particle accelerators like the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), medical imaging (PET scans), and our understanding of the early universe moments after the Big Bang. This knowledge is essential for explaining phenomena from nuclear decay to the stability of matter itself.

**中文：**
本主题“基本粒子”是现代粒子物理学的基石。它探索物质的基本组成单元以及支配它们相互作用的力。我们超越经典的原子模型，深入研究夸克、轻子和传递基本力的玻色子的亚原子世界。这项研究对于理解[[标准模型]]至关重要，该模型是我们在最基础层面描述宇宙最成功的理论框架。

在剑桥 9702 和爱德思 IAL A-Level 物理的背景下，本主题介绍了“粒子动物园”、粒子分类（强子和轻子）以及决定哪些粒子相互作用可能发生的守恒定律（重子数、轻子数、电荷和奇异数）。实际应用包括大型强子对撞机（LHC）等粒子加速器的运行、医学成像（PET 扫描）以及我们对大爆炸后早期宇宙的理解。这些知识对于解释从核衰变到物质稳定性的一切现象都是必不可少的。

# 4. Key Concepts Explained / 关键概念详解

## 4.1 The Particle Zoo and Classification / 粒子动物园与分类

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
In the mid-20th century, physicists discovered a bewildering array of new particles in cosmic rays and particle accelerators. This was called the "particle zoo." The [[Standard Model]] brought order by classifying them into two main families: **hadrons** (which feel the [[strong nuclear force]]) and **leptons** (which do not). Hadrons are further divided into **baryons** (three quarks) and **mesons** (quark-antiquark pair). Leptons are fundamental particles, meaning they have no internal structure.

**中文：**
在 20 世纪中期，物理学家在宇宙射线和粒子加速器中发现了一系列令人困惑的新粒子。这被称为“粒子动物园”。[[标准模型]]通过将它们分为两个主要家族来建立秩序：**强子**（受[[强核力]]作用）和**轻子**（不受[[强核力]]作用）。强子进一步分为**重子**（三个夸克）和**介子**（一个夸克和一个反夸克对）。轻子是基本粒子，意味着它们没有内部结构。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
This classification explains why protons and neutrons (baryons) are found in the nucleus, held together by the [[strong nuclear force]], while electrons (leptons) orbit the nucleus, interacting only via the [[electromagnetic force]]. It also explains why particles like pions (mesons) are produced in high-energy collisions and quickly decay.

**中文：**
这种分类解释了为什么质子和中子（重子）在原子核内被[[强核力]]束缚在一起，而电子（轻子）绕原子核运行，仅通过[[电磁力]]相互作用。它也解释了为什么像π介子（介子）这样的粒子在高能碰撞中产生并迅速衰变。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:** Students often think that all particles are fundamental. They must remember that hadrons are composite.
- **中文：** 学生常认为所有粒子都是基本的。他们必须记住强子是复合粒子。
- **English:** Confusing baryons and mesons. Baryons have 3 quarks; mesons have a quark-antiquark pair.
- **中文：** 混淆重子和介子。重子有 3 个夸克；介子有一个夸克-反夸克对。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:** Be prepared to classify a given particle (e.g., "Is a kaon a hadron? Is it a baryon or a meson?"). Know the quark composition of common particles like protons (uud), neutrons (udd), and pions (e.g., π⁺ = u d̄).
**中文：** 准备好对给定粒子进行分类（例如，“K介子是强子吗？它是重子还是介子？”）。了解常见粒子的夸克组成，如质子 (uud)、中子 (udd) 和π介子（例如，π⁺ = u d̄）。

## 4.2 Quarks and Their Properties / 夸克及其性质

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Quarks are fundamental particles that make up hadrons. There are six "flavors" of quarks: up (u), down (d), charm (c), strange (s), top (t), and bottom (b). For A-Level, we focus on the first two generations: u, d, s, and c. Each quark has a fractional electric charge (+2/3 e for u, c, t; -1/3 e for d, s, b). They also carry a "color charge" (red, green, blue) which is the source of the [[strong nuclear force]].

**中文：**
夸克是构成强子的基本粒子。有六种“味”的夸克：上 (u)、下 (d)、粲 (c)、奇 (s)、顶 (t) 和底 (b)。在 A-Level 中，我们关注前两代：u、d、s 和 c。每个夸克都有分数电荷（u、c、t 为 +2/3 e；d、s、b 为 -1/3 e）。它们还带有“色荷”（红、绿、蓝），这是[[强核力]]的来源。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
The fractional charges of quarks explain why protons and neutrons have integer charges. A proton (uud) has charge +2/3 + 2/3 - 1/3 = +1 e. A neutron (udd) has charge +2/3 - 1/3 - 1/3 = 0 e. The property of "color" ensures that only "colorless" combinations (like red+green+blue or red+antired) can exist as free particles (confinement).

**中文：**
夸克的分数电荷解释了为什么质子和中子具有整数电荷。质子 (uud) 的电荷为 +2/3 + 2/3 - 1/3 = +1 e。中子 (udd) 的电荷为 +2/3 - 1/3 - 1/3 = 0 e。“色”的性质确保只有“无色”的组合（如红+绿+蓝或红+反红）才能作为自由粒子存在（夸克禁闭）。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:** Students think quarks can exist in isolation. They cannot due to [[quark confinement]].
- **中文：** 学生认为夸克可以单独存在。由于[[夸克禁闭]]，它们不能。
- **English:** Forgetting that antiquarks have opposite charge and baryon number.
- **中文：** 忘记反夸克具有相反的电荷和重子数。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:** You must know the quark composition of common hadrons. Be able to calculate the charge and baryon number of a particle from its quark content. Questions often ask: "Determine the quark composition of a K⁺ meson."
**中文：** 你必须知道常见强子的夸克组成。能够根据夸克含量计算粒子的电荷和重子数。问题常问：“确定 K⁺ 介子的夸克组成。”

## 4.3 Leptons / 轻子

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Leptons are fundamental particles that do not experience the [[strong nuclear force]]. There are six leptons: electron (e⁻), muon (μ⁻), tau (τ⁻), and their corresponding neutrinos (νₑ, ν_μ, ν_τ). Each lepton has an associated lepton number (Lₑ, L_μ, L_τ) which is conserved in interactions. For A-Level, the electron and its neutrino are most important.

**中文：**
轻子是不受[[强核力]]作用的基本粒子。有六种轻子：电子 (e⁻)、μ子 (μ⁻)、τ子 (τ⁻) 及其相应的中微子 (νₑ, ν_μ, ν_τ)。每个轻子都有一个相关的轻子数 (Lₑ, L_μ, L_τ)，在相互作用中守恒。在 A-Level 中，电子及其反中微子最为重要。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
Leptons are involved in [[weak nuclear force]] interactions, such as beta decay (n → p + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ). Neutrinos are extremely light, neutral particles that interact very weakly with matter, making them very difficult to detect. They are produced in nuclear reactions in the Sun and in particle accelerators.

**中文：**
轻子参与[[弱核力]]相互作用，例如β衰变 (n → p + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ)。中微子是极轻的中性粒子，与物质的相互作用非常弱，因此极难探测。它们产生于太阳内部的核反应和粒子加速器中。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:** Students think neutrinos have no mass. They have a very small, non-zero mass.
- **中文：** 学生认为中微子没有质量。它们有非常小但非零的质量。
- **English:** Forgetting that each lepton has its own lepton number (e.g., an electron has Lₑ = +1, a muon has L_μ = +1).
- **中文：** 忘记每个轻子都有自己的轻子数（例如，电子有 Lₑ = +1，μ子有 L_μ = +1）。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:** Be able to write beta decay equations and check lepton number conservation. Know that an antineutrino is emitted in β⁻ decay and a neutrino in β⁺ decay.
**中文：** 能够写出β衰变方程并检查轻子数守恒。知道在 β⁻ 衰变中发射反中微子，在 β⁺ 衰变中发射中微子。

## 4.4 Exchange Particles and Forces / 交换粒子与力

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Forces are mediated by the exchange of virtual particles, called gauge bosons. The [[electromagnetic force]] is mediated by the photon (γ). The [[strong nuclear force]] is mediated by gluons (g). The [[weak nuclear force]] is mediated by the W⁺, W⁻, and Z⁰ bosons. The [[gravitational force]] is thought to be mediated by the graviton, but this has not been observed. The range of the force is inversely proportional to the mass of the exchange particle: photons (massless) have infinite range; W and Z bosons (massive) have a very short range (~10⁻¹⁸ m).

**中文：**
力是通过交换虚粒子（称为规范玻色子）来传递的。[[电磁力]]由光子 (γ) 传递。[[强核力]]由胶子 (g) 传递。[[弱核力]]由 W⁺、W⁻ 和 Z⁰ 玻色子传递。[[引力]]被认为由引力子传递，但尚未被观测到。力的作用范围与交换粒子的质量成反比：光子（无质量）具有无限作用范围；W 和 Z 玻色子（有质量）的作用范围非常短（约 10⁻¹⁸ 米）。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
This concept explains why the [[weak nuclear force]] is so short-ranged (it requires massive exchange particles) while [[electromagnetism]] is long-ranged (massless photon). It also explains why the [[strong nuclear force]] is so strong (gluons themselves carry color charge and interact strongly).

**中文：**
这个概念解释了为什么[[弱核力]]的作用范围如此之短（它需要大质量的交换粒子），而[[电磁力]]的作用范围很长（无质量光子）。它也解释了为什么[[强核力]]如此之强（胶子本身带有色荷并发生强相互作用）。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:** Students think exchange particles are real particles. They are virtual and exist only during the interaction, violating energy conservation for a short time (allowed by the [[Heisenberg uncertainty principle]]).
- **中文：** 学生认为交换粒子是真实粒子。它们是虚粒子，仅在相互作用期间存在，在短时间内违反能量守恒（由[[海森堡不确定性原理]]允许）。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:** Be able to draw Feynman diagrams (simple ones) showing the exchange of a virtual particle. Know which exchange particle mediates which force.
**中文：** 能够画出费曼图（简单的），显示虚粒子的交换。知道哪种交换粒子传递哪种力。

## 4.5 Conservation Laws / 守恒定律

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
In any particle interaction, several quantities must be conserved. These include:
1. **Charge (Q):** Total electric charge before and after must be equal.
2. **Baryon Number (B):** Baryons have B = +1, antibaryons have B = -1, mesons and leptons have B = 0.
3. **Lepton Number (L):** Each lepton family has its own number (Lₑ, L_μ, L_τ). Leptons have L = +1, antileptons have L = -1.
4. **Strangeness (S):** Conserved in [[strong nuclear force]] interactions but not in [[weak nuclear force]] interactions.
5. **Energy and Momentum:** Always conserved.

**中文：**
在任何粒子相互作用中，必须守恒几个量。这些包括：
1. **电荷 (Q)：** 相互作用前后的总电荷必须相等。
2. **重子数 (B)：** 重子有 B = +1，反重子有 B = -1，介子和轻子有 B = 0。
3. **轻子数 (L)：** 每个轻子家族都有自己的轻子数 (Lₑ, L_μ, L_τ)。轻子有 L = +1，反轻子有 L = -1。
4. **奇异数 (S)：** 在[[强核力]]相互作用中守恒，但在[[弱核力]]相互作用中不守恒。
5. **能量和动量：** 始终守恒。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
These laws determine which particle reactions are possible and which are forbidden. For example, a proton cannot decay into a positron and a photon because baryon number would not be conserved (proton has B=1, positron has B=0, photon has B=0).

**中文：**
这些定律决定了哪些粒子反应是可能的，哪些是被禁止的。例如，质子不能衰变为正电子和光子，因为重子数不守恒（质子有 B=1，正电子有 B=0，光子有 B=0）。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:** Students forget to check all conservation laws. They might only check charge.
- **中文：** 学生忘记检查所有守恒定律。他们可能只检查电荷。
- **English:** Confusing baryon number with lepton number.
- **中文：** 混淆重子数和轻子数。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:** This is a very common exam topic. You will be given a reaction and asked to determine if it is possible by checking conservation laws. Always check charge, baryon number, lepton number, and strangeness (if applicable).
**中文：** 这是一个非常常见的考试主题。你会得到一个反应，并被要求通过检查守恒定律来确定它是否可能。始终检查电荷、重子数、轻子数和奇异数（如果适用）。

# 5. Essential Equations / 核心公式

## 5.1 Conservation of Baryon Number / 重子数守恒

**Equation / 公式:**
$$ \sum B_{\text{before}} = \sum B_{\text{after}} $$

**Variables / 变量:**
| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| B | Baryon number | 重子数 | Dimensionless (无量纲) |

**Derivation / 推导:**
**English:** This is a fundamental conservation law derived from experimental observation. It is not derived from first principles.
**中文：** 这是一个从实验观察中得出的基本守恒定律。它不是从第一性原理推导出来的。

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
**English:** Applies to all particle interactions.
**中文：** 适用于所有粒子相互作用。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
**English:** None known at this level.
**中文：** 在这个级别没有已知的限制。

**Rearrangements / 变形:**
**English:** N/A (it's a sum rule).
**中文：** 不适用（它是一个求和规则）。

## 5.2 Conservation of Lepton Number / 轻子数守恒

**Equation / 公式:**
$$ \sum L_{\text{before}} = \sum L_{\text{after}} $$
Where L can be Lₑ, L_μ, or L_τ.

**Variables / 变量:**
| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Lₑ | Electron lepton number | 电子轻子数 | Dimensionless (无量纲) |
| L_μ | Muon lepton number | μ子轻子数 | Dimensionless (无量纲) |
| L_τ | Tau lepton number | τ子轻子数 | Dimensionless (无量纲) |

**Derivation / 推导:**
**English:** This is a fundamental conservation law derived from experimental observation.
**中文：** 这是一个从实验观察中得出的基本守恒定律。

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
**English:** Applies to all particle interactions.
**中文：** 适用于所有粒子相互作用。

# 8. Worked Examples / 典型例题

## Example 1: Checking Conservation Laws / 检查守恒定律

### Question / 题目
**English:** Determine whether the following interaction is possible. If not, state which conservation law(s) are violated.
$$ p + p \rightarrow p + p + \pi^0 $$
Where p is a proton and π⁰ is a neutral pion (quark composition: uū or dd̄).

**中文：** 确定以下相互作用是否可能。如果不可能，说明违反了哪些守恒定律。
$$ p + p \rightarrow p + p + \pi^0 $$
其中 p 是质子，π⁰ 是中性π介子（夸克组成：uū 或 dd̄）。

### Image Prompt / 图片提示
> 📷 **IMAGE PROMPT — [WE01]: Proton-Proton Collision Producing a Pion**
>
> A simple diagram showing two protons approaching each other, colliding, and then two protons and a neutral pion moving away. Use arrows to indicate motion. Label the particles. Use a clean, educational style.

### Solution / 解答
**Step 1: Identify the particles and their quantum numbers.**
- Proton (p): Q = +1 e, B = +1, L = 0, S = 0
- Pion (π⁰): Q = 0 e, B = 0, L = 0, S = 0

**Step 2: Check conservation of charge.**
- Before: (+1) + (+1) = +2 e
- After: (+1) + (+1) + (0) = +2 e
- **Charge is conserved.**

**Step 3: Check conservation of baryon number.**
- Before: (+1) + (+1) = +2
- After: (+1) + (+1) + (0) = +2
- **Baryon number is conserved.**

**Step 4: Check conservation of lepton number.**
- Before: 0 + 0 = 0
- After: 0 + 0 + 0 = 0
- **Lepton number is conserved.**

**Step 5: Check conservation of strangeness.**
- Before: 0 + 0 = 0
- After: 0 + 0 + 0 = 0
- **Strangeness is conserved.**

**Step 6: Check conservation of energy and momentum.**
- This is possible if the incoming protons have enough kinetic energy to create the mass of the pion (m_π⁰ c² ≈ 135 MeV).
- **Energy and momentum can be conserved.**

**Conclusion:** The interaction is possible. All conservation laws are satisfied.

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** The interaction is possible. | **答案：** 该相互作用是可能的。

### Examiner Notes / 考官点评
**English:** This is a classic example. Candidates often forget to check all laws. Always list each law and show the calculation. The creation of a pion requires sufficient energy, which is a key point.
**中文：** 这是一个经典例子。考生常忘记检查所有定律。始终列出每个定律并展示计算。产生π介子需要足够的能量，这是一个关键点。

### Alternative Method / 替代方法
**English:** You can also check by considering the quark composition. The reaction involves only u and d quarks, which are conserved.
**中文：** 你也可以通过考虑夸克组成来检查。该反应只涉及 u 和 d 夸克，它们是守恒的。

## Example 2: Determining Quark Composition / 确定夸克组成

### Question / 题目
**English:** A K⁺ meson has a charge of +1 e and a strangeness of +1. Determine its quark composition. (Hint: The strange quark has charge -1/3 e and strangeness -1.)

**中文：** 一个 K⁺ 介子的电荷为 +1 e，奇异数为 +1。确定其夸克组成。（提示：奇夸克的电荷为 -1/3 e，奇异数为 -1。）

### Solution / 解答
**Step 1: Identify the particle type.**
- K⁺ is a meson, so it is composed of a quark and an antiquark.

**Step 2: Set up equations.**
Let the quark be q₁ and the antiquark be q̄₂.
- Charge: Q(q₁) + Q(q̄₂) = +1 e
- Strangeness: S(q₁) + S(q̄₂) = +1

**Step 3: Consider the strange quark.**
- A strange quark (s) has Q = -1/3 e and S = -1.
- Its antiparticle, the anti-strange quark (s̄), has Q = +1/3 e and S = +1.

**Step 4: Test the anti-strange quark.**
If q̄₂ = s̄, then S(q̄₂) = +1. For the total strangeness to be +1, S(q₁) must be 0.
- S(q₁) + S(s̄) = S(q₁) + 1 = 1 → S(q₁) = 0.

**Step 5: Find a quark with S=0 and appropriate charge.**
- An up quark (u) has Q = +2/3 e and S = 0.
- A down quark (d) has Q = -1/3 e and S = 0.

**Step 6: Check the charge.**
If q₁ = u and q̄₂ = s̄:
- Q(u) + Q(s̄) = (+2/3) + (+1/3) = +1 e.
- This matches the charge of K⁺.

**Conclusion:** The K⁺ meson is composed of an up quark and an anti-strange quark: K⁺ = u s̄.

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** K⁺ = u s̄ | **答案：** K⁺ = u s̄

### Examiner Notes / 考官点评
**English:** This is a common type of question. Candidates must know the properties of quarks (charge, strangeness) and the concept of antiparticles. Always start by identifying the particle type (baryon or meson).
**中文：** 这是一种常见题型。考生必须知道夸克的性质（电荷、奇异数）和反粒子的概念。始终从识别粒子类型（重子或介子）开始。

### Alternative Method / 替代方法
**English:** You can also use a systematic approach by listing all possible quark-antiquark combinations and checking which one satisfies the charge and strangeness conditions.
**中文：** 你也可以使用系统的方法，列出所有可能的夸克-反夸克组合，并检查哪个满足电荷和奇异数条件。

| **Key Facts / 关键事实** | - The [[Standard Model]] describes all known fundamental particles and three of the four fundamental forces (not gravity).<br>- Quarks have fractional charges (+2/3 e or -1/3 e).<br>- Leptons have integer charges (0 or -1 e).<br>- The [[strong nuclear force]] is mediated by gluons and confines quarks inside hadrons.<br>- The [[weak nuclear force]] is mediated by W and Z bosons and is responsible for beta decay.<br>- Strangeness is conserved in strong interactions but not in weak interactions. |
