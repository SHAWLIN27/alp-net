# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This sub-topic explores the fundamental rules governing how particles interact and transform. In the quantum world, particles are not static; they constantly interact via the four fundamental forces (strong, weak, electromagnetic, gravitational), exchanging force-carrying particles called gauge bosons. Crucially, every interaction must obey strict conservation laws — these are the "rules of the game" that determine whether a particle reaction or decay is possible. This sub-topic covers the key conservation laws (energy, momentum, charge, baryon number, lepton number, strangeness) and how they apply to particle interactions and decays. Understanding these laws is essential for predicting particle behaviour, analysing Feynman diagrams, and explaining why certain reactions (like proton decay) are forbidden. This builds directly on [[Atomic Structure and the Nucleus]] and connects to [[The Standard Model Overview]], [[Hadrons (Baryons and Mesons)]], and [[Leptons]].

**中文:**
本子知识点探讨支配粒子相互作用和转化的基本规则。在量子世界中，粒子并非静止不动；它们通过四种基本力（强、弱、电磁、引力）不断相互作用，交换称为规范玻色子的载力粒子。至关重要的是，每一次相互作用都必须遵守严格的守恒定律——这些是决定粒子反应或衰变是否可能发生的“游戏规则”。本子知识点涵盖关键的守恒定律（能量、动量、电荷、重子数、轻子数、奇异数）及其在粒子相互作用和衰变中的应用。理解这些定律对于预测粒子行为、分析费曼图以及解释为什么某些反应（如质子衰变）是被禁止的至关重要。这直接建立在[[Atomic Structure and the Nucleus]]的基础上，并与[[The Standard Model Overview]]、[[Hadrons (Baryons and Mesons)]]和[[Leptons]]相关联。

# 4. Key Concepts Explained / 关键概念详解

## 4.1 Conservation Laws in Particle Interactions / 粒子相互作用中的守恒定律

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Particle interactions and decays are governed by a set of conservation laws. These laws are not optional — they are fundamental rules that nature must obey. For any interaction or decay to be possible, ALL relevant conservation laws must be satisfied simultaneously. The key conserved quantities are:
- **Charge (Q):** Total electric charge is always conserved.
- **Baryon Number (B):** Total baryon number is always conserved.
- **Lepton Number (L):** Total lepton number is always conserved (including family-specific lepton numbers Lₑ, Lₘ, Lₜ).
- **Strangeness (S):** Conserved in strong and electromagnetic interactions, but NOT in weak interactions.
- **Energy and Momentum:** Always conserved (relativistically).
- **Spin/Angular Momentum:** Always conserved.

**中文:**
粒子相互作用和衰变受一组守恒定律的支配。这些定律不是可选的——它们是自然必须遵守的基本规则。对于任何相互作用或衰变，要使其可能发生，所有相关的守恒定律必须同时满足。关键的守恒量包括：
- **电荷 (Q)：** 总电荷始终守恒。
- **重子数 (B)：** 总重子数始终守恒。
- **轻子数 (L)：** 总轻子数始终守恒（包括特定族的轻子数 Lₑ、Lₘ、Lₜ）。
- **奇异数 (S)：** 在强相互作用和电磁相互作用中守恒，但在弱相互作用中不守恒。
- **能量和动量：** 始终守恒（相对论性）。
- **自旋/角动量：** 始终守恒。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** These conservation laws reflect deep symmetries in nature. For example, charge conservation is linked to the symmetry of electromagnetism (gauge invariance). Baryon number conservation explains why the proton (the lightest baryon) is stable — it cannot decay into lighter particles without violating baryon number conservation. Lepton number conservation explains why processes like μ⁻ → e⁻ + γ are forbidden (it would violate lepton family number).

**中文:** 这些守恒定律反映了自然界深层的对称性。例如，电荷守恒与电磁学的对称性（规范不变性）相关。重子数守恒解释了为什么质子（最轻的重子）是稳定的——它不能衰变成更轻的粒子而不违反重子数守恒。轻子数守恒解释了为什么像 μ⁻ → e⁻ + γ 这样的过程是被禁止的（它会违反轻子族数）。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:**
  - Thinking strangeness is always conserved — it is NOT conserved in weak interactions.
  - Forgetting to check lepton family numbers (Lₑ, Lₘ, Lₜ) separately — they are conserved individually, not just total L.
  - Assuming that if one conservation law is violated, the interaction is still possible — ALL must be satisfied.
  - Confusing baryon number with baryon mass — they are unrelated.
- **中文:**
  - 认为奇异数总是守恒——它在弱相互作用中不守恒。
  - 忘记分别检查轻子族数（Lₑ、Lₘ、Lₜ）——它们是各自守恒的，不仅仅是总 L。
  - 假设如果一条守恒定律被违反，相互作用仍然可能发生——所有定律都必须满足。
  - 混淆重子数和重子质量——它们无关。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **English:** Always write a systematic table checking Q, B, L, and S (if applicable) for each interaction. Show your working clearly — examiners award marks for each correct check.
- **中文:** 始终编写一个系统性的表格，检查每个相互作用的 Q、B、L 和 S（如适用）。清晰地展示你的计算过程——考官会为每个正确的检查给分。

## 4.2 Exchange Particles and Fundamental Forces / 交换粒子与基本力

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
All fundamental forces are mediated by the exchange of virtual particles called gauge bosons. This is described by quantum field theory. The exchange particle "carries" the force between interacting particles. The four fundamental forces and their exchange particles are:

| Force | Exchange Particle | Acts on | Range | Relative Strength |
|-------|------------------|---------|-------|-------------------|
| Strong | Gluon (g) | Quarks and gluons | ~10⁻¹⁵ m | 1 |
| Electromagnetic | Photon (γ) | Charged particles | Infinite | 10⁻² |
| Weak | W⁺, W⁻, Z⁰ | Quarks and leptons | ~10⁻¹⁸ m | 10⁻⁶ |
| Gravitational | Graviton (hypothetical) | All particles with mass | Infinite | 10⁻³⁹ |

**中文:**
所有基本力都是通过交换称为规范玻色子的虚粒子来传递的。这由量子场论描述。交换粒子在相互作用的粒子之间“携带”力。四种基本力及其交换粒子如下：

| 力 | 交换粒子 | 作用对象 | 作用范围 | 相对强度 |
|----|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| 强相互作用 | 胶子 (g) | 夸克和胶子 | ~10⁻¹⁵ m | 1 |
| 电磁相互作用 | 光子 (γ) | 带电粒子 | 无限远 | 10⁻² |
| 弱相互作用 | W⁺, W⁻, Z⁰ | 夸克和轻子 | ~10⁻¹⁸ m | 10⁻⁶ |
| 引力相互作用 | 引力子（假设的） | 所有有质量的粒子 | 无限远 | 10⁻³⁹ |

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** The exchange particle is "virtual" — it exists only for a very short time (Δt) allowed by the Heisenberg uncertainty principle (ΔE·Δt ≥ ħ/2). This is why the range of a force is related to the mass of its exchange particle: heavier exchange particles have shorter ranges because they require more energy and can only exist for a shorter time. The photon (massless) has infinite range; the W and Z bosons (very massive, ~80-90 GeV/c²) have a very short range.

**中文:** 交换粒子是“虚”的——它只在海森堡不确定性原理 (ΔE·Δt ≥ ħ/2) 允许的极短时间内存在。这就是为什么力的作用范围与其交换粒子的质量相关：更重的交换粒子作用范围更短，因为它们需要更多能量，只能存在更短时间。光子（无质量）作用范围无限远；W 和 Z 玻色子（质量很大，约 80-90 GeV/c²）作用范围非常短。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:**
  - Thinking exchange particles are real particles that can be detected — they are virtual and cannot be directly observed.
  - Confusing the range of the weak force with the size of the nucleus — the weak force range is much smaller than a nucleus.
  - Thinking the strong force acts on all particles — it only acts on particles with colour charge (quarks and gluons).
- **中文:**
  - 认为交换粒子是可以被探测到的真实粒子——它们是虚粒子，无法被直接观测。
  - 混淆弱力的作用范围与原子核的大小——弱力的作用范围远小于原子核。
  - 认为强力作用于所有粒子——它只作用于带有色荷的粒子（夸克和胶子）。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **English:** Be able to state which exchange particle mediates which force. For the weak interaction, remember that W⁺ and W⁻ carry charge and change the flavour of quarks (e.g., in beta decay), while Z⁰ is neutral and does not change flavour.
- **中文:** 能够说明哪种交换粒子传递哪种力。对于弱相互作用，记住 W⁺ 和 W⁻ 带有电荷并改变夸克的味道（例如在 β 衰变中），而 Z⁰ 是中性的，不改变味道。

## 4.3 Feynman Diagrams / 费曼图

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Feynman diagrams are spacetime diagrams that represent particle interactions. They show:
- **Time axis:** Usually vertical (upwards) or horizontal (rightwards) — check the convention used.
- **Particle lines:** Straight lines with arrows showing the direction of particle flow. Arrows pointing forward in time represent particles; arrows pointing backward in time represent antiparticles.
- **Exchange particles:** Wavy or dashed lines (depending on convention) representing virtual gauge bosons.
- **Vertices:** Points where particle lines meet — these are the interaction points where conservation laws must be checked.

**中文:**
费曼图是表示粒子相互作用的时空图。它们显示：
- **时间轴：** 通常垂直（向上）或水平（向右）——检查使用的惯例。
- **粒子线：** 带有箭头的直线，显示粒子流动方向。指向时间前进方向的箭头代表粒子；指向时间后退方向的箭头代表反粒子。
- **交换粒子：** 波浪线或虚线（取决于惯例），代表虚规范玻色子。
- **顶点：** 粒子线交汇的点——这些是相互作用点，必须在此检查守恒定律。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** Feynman diagrams are not just pictures — they are precise mathematical tools for calculating the probability of an interaction. At each vertex, the following must be conserved: charge, baryon number, lepton number, energy, and momentum. The exchange particle is "off-shell" (virtual), meaning its mass does not need to equal its rest mass — it can borrow energy from the uncertainty principle.

**中文:** 费曼图不仅仅是图画——它们是计算相互作用概率的精确数学工具。在每个顶点，以下量必须守恒：电荷、重子数、轻子数、能量和动量。交换粒子是“离壳”（虚）的，意味着它的质量不需要等于其静止质量——它可以从不确定性原理中借取能量。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:**
  - Misinterpreting the arrow direction — arrows show particle flow, not just direction of motion. An antiparticle is represented as a particle moving backward in time.
  - Thinking the exchange particle is real — it is virtual and cannot be observed.
  - Forgetting to check conservation laws at EACH vertex — each vertex is an independent interaction point.
- **中文:**
  - 误解箭头方向——箭头显示粒子流，而不仅仅是运动方向。反粒子被表示为在时间中向后移动的粒子。
  - 认为交换粒子是真实的——它是虚的，无法被观测。
  - 忘记检查每个顶点的守恒定律——每个顶点都是一个独立的相互作用点。

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **English:** When drawing or completing Feynman diagrams:
  1. Identify the type of interaction (strong, EM, weak).
  2. Draw the correct exchange particle.
  3. Ensure arrows are correct (particles forward, antiparticles backward).
  4. Check conservation laws at each vertex.
  5. For weak interactions involving quarks, remember that W bosons can change quark flavour (e.g., d → u + W⁻).
- **中文:** 在绘制或完成费曼图时：
  1. 识别相互作用的类型（强、电磁、弱）。
  2. 绘制正确的交换粒子。
  3. 确保箭头正确（粒子向前，反粒子向后）。
  4. 检查每个顶点的守恒定律。
  5. 对于涉及夸克的弱相互作用，记住 W 玻色子可以改变夸克的味道（例如 d → u + W⁻）。

# 5. Essential Equations / 核心公式

## 5.1 Conservation Law Check Equation / 守恒定律检查方程

$$ \sum_{\text{before}} Q = \sum_{\text{after}} Q, \quad \sum_{\text{before}} B = \sum_{\text{after}} B, \quad \sum_{\text{before}} L = \sum_{\text{after}} L $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Q | Electric charge | 电荷 | e (elementary charge) |
| B | Baryon number | 重子数 | dimensionless |
| L | Lepton number | 轻子数 | dimensionless |

**Conditions / 适用条件:** These conservation laws apply to ALL particle interactions and decays. Strangeness (S) conservation applies only to strong and electromagnetic interactions.

**Limitations / 局限性:** These are necessary but not sufficient conditions — even if all conservation laws are satisfied, the interaction may still be forbidden by other factors (e.g., energy conservation, quantum mechanical selection rules).

## 5.2 Strangeness Conservation Condition / 奇异数守恒条件

$$ \Delta S = 0 \quad \text{(strong and EM interactions)} $$
$$ \Delta S = \pm 1 \quad \text{(weak interactions, allowed)} $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| S | Strangeness | 奇异数 | dimensionless |
| ΔS | Change in strangeness | 奇异数变化 | dimensionless |

**Conditions / 适用条件:** Strangeness is conserved in strong and electromagnetic interactions. In weak interactions, strangeness can change by ±1 (but not by 2 or more in a single step).

**Limitations / 局限性:** Strangeness is not a fundamental conserved quantity like charge — it is conserved only in certain interactions.

## 5.3 Range of Force and Exchange Particle Mass / 力的作用范围与交换粒子质量

$$ R \approx \frac{\hbar}{m c} $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| R | Range of the force | 力的作用范围 | m |
| ħ | Reduced Planck constant (h/2π) | 约化普朗克常数 | J·s |
| m | Mass of the exchange particle | 交换粒子的质量 | kg |
| c | Speed of light | 光速 | m/s |

**Derivation / 推导:** From the Heisenberg uncertainty principle: ΔE·Δt ≥ ħ/2. The exchange particle has rest energy ΔE = mc². The maximum time it can exist is Δt ≈ ħ/(2mc²). The maximum distance it can travel in that time is R ≈ c·Δt ≈ ħ/(2mc). The factor of 2 is often omitted for order-of-magnitude estimates.

**Conditions / 适用条件:** This is an order-of-magnitude estimate based on the uncertainty principle. It applies to forces mediated by massive exchange particles.

**Limitations / 局限性:** For massless exchange particles (photon, gluon), this formula gives R → ∞, which is correct for the electromagnetic force but not for the strong force (which has finite range due to confinement). The strong force range is limited by quark confinement, not by gluon mass.

## 7.1 Feynman Diagram for Beta Minus Decay / β⁻ 衰变的费曼图

# 8. Worked Examples / 典型例题

## Example 1: Checking Conservation Laws for a Proposed Interaction / 检查提议相互作用的守恒定律

### Question / 题目
**English:**
Determine whether the following interaction is possible according to the conservation laws:
$$ p + \pi^- \rightarrow K^0 + \Lambda^0 $$
Given particle properties:
- p (proton): Q = +1, B = +1, S = 0
- π⁻ (pion): Q = -1, B = 0, S = 0
- K⁰ (kaon): Q = 0, B = 0, S = +1
- Λ⁰ (lambda): Q = 0, B = +1, S = -1

**中文:**
根据守恒定律判断以下相互作用是否可能发生：
$$ p + \pi^- \rightarrow K^0 + \Lambda^0 $$
给定粒子性质：
- p（质子）：Q = +1, B = +1, S = 0
- π⁻（π介子）：Q = -1, B = 0, S = 0
- K⁰（K介子）：Q = 0, B = 0, S = +1
- Λ⁰（Λ超子）：Q = 0, B = +1, S = -1

### Solution / 解答

**Step 1: Check Charge (Q) / 检查电荷 (Q)**
- Before: Q = (+1) + (-1) = 0
- After: Q = (0) + (0) = 0
- ✓ Conserved

**Step 2: Check Baryon Number (B) / 检查重子数 (B)**
- Before: B = (+1) + (0) = +1
- After: B = (0) + (+1) = +1
- ✓ Conserved

**Step 3: Check Strangeness (S) / 检查奇异数 (S)**
- Before: S = (0) + (0) = 0
- After: S = (+1) + (-1) = 0
- ✓ Conserved (if this is a strong interaction)

**Step 4: Determine Interaction Type / 确定相互作用类型**
Since strangeness is conserved, this could be a strong interaction. All conservation laws are satisfied.

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** The interaction is POSSIBLE via the strong interaction. | **答案：** 该相互作用可能通过强相互作用发生。

### Quick Tip / 提示
**English:** Always check strangeness conservation first — if ΔS = 0, it could be strong or EM; if ΔS ≠ 0, it must be weak (if other laws allow). | **中文：** 始终先检查奇异数守恒——如果 ΔS = 0，可能是强或电磁相互作用；如果 ΔS ≠ 0，则必须是弱相互作用（如果其他定律允许）。

## Example 2: Completing a Feynman Diagram / 完成费曼图

### Question / 题目
**English:**
The following Feynman diagram shows a weak interaction decay. Identify the missing particle X:
$$ \mu^- \rightarrow e^- + \bar{\nu}_e + X $$
The diagram shows a muon (μ⁻) decaying. At the vertex, a W⁻ boson is emitted. The W⁻ decays into an electron (e⁻) and an electron antineutrino (ν̄ₑ). What is X?

**中文:**
以下费曼图显示一个弱相互作用衰变。识别缺失的粒子 X：
$$ \mu^- \rightarrow e^- + \bar{\nu}_e + X $$
该图显示一个 μ 子 (μ⁻) 衰变。在顶点处，发射一个 W⁻ 玻色子。W⁻ 衰变为一个电子 (e⁻) 和一个电子反中微子 (ν̄ₑ)。X 是什么？

### Solution / 解答

**Step 1: Identify the interaction / 识别相互作用**
This is muon decay: μ⁻ → e⁻ + ν̄ₑ + νₘ (muon neutrino)

**Step 2: Check lepton numbers / 检查轻子数**
- Before: Lₘ = +1 (μ⁻ is a muon)
- After: Lₑ = +1 (e⁻) + (-1) (ν̄ₑ) = 0, so we need Lₘ = +1 from X
- Therefore X must be a muon neutrino (νₘ)

**Step 3: Verify / 验证**
- Charge: Before = -1, After = (-1) + (0) + (0) = -1 ✓
- Lepton numbers: Lₑ: 0 → (+1) + (-1) + 0 = 0 ✓; Lₘ: +1 → 0 + 0 + (+1) = +1 ✓

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** X = νₘ (muon neutrino) | **答案：** X = νₘ（μ子中微子）

### Quick Tip / 提示
**English:** In weak interactions involving leptons, always check lepton family numbers (Lₑ, Lₘ, Lₜ) separately — they are conserved individually, not just total L. | **中文：** 在涉及轻子的弱相互作用中，始终分别检查轻子族数（Lₑ、Lₘ、Lₜ）——它们是各自守恒的，不仅仅是总 L。

# 10. Practical Skills Connections / 实验技能链接

**English:**
While particle interactions are not directly studied in A-Level practical papers, the conservation laws have important experimental implications:

1. **Particle Detection:** Conservation laws are used to identify particles in detectors like cloud chambers, bubble chambers, and spark chambers. By tracking particle paths and applying conservation laws, physicists can deduce the properties of unseen particles (e.g., the neutrino was postulated to conserve energy and momentum in beta decay).

2. **Experimental Design:** When designing experiments to study particle interactions, physicists must ensure that conservation laws are satisfied. For example, in particle colliders, the initial state must have the same quantum numbers as the final state.

3. **Data Analysis:** Conservation laws are used to check the consistency of experimental data. If a proposed decay violates a conservation law, the data must be reinterpreted.

4. **Uncertainties:** In practical work, conservation laws provide a check on measurements. For example, in a scattering experiment, the total momentum before and after must be equal within experimental uncertainty.

**中文:**
虽然粒子相互作用在 A-Level 实验考试中不直接研究，但守恒定律具有重要的实验意义：

1. **粒子探测：** 守恒定律用于在云室、气泡室和火花室等探测器中识别粒子。通过追踪粒子路径并应用守恒定律，物理学家可以推断出不可见粒子的性质（例如，中微子是为了在 β 衰变中守恒能量和动量而被假设存在的）。

2. **实验设计：** 在设计研究粒子相互作用的实验时，物理学家必须确保守恒定律得到满足。例如，在粒子对撞机中，初始态必须与最终态具有相同的量子数。

3. **数据分析：** 守恒定律用于检查实验数据的一致性。如果提议的衰变违反了守恒定律，则必须重新解释数据。

4. **不确定度：** 在实际工作中，守恒定律为测量提供了检查。例如，在散射实验中，实验不确定度范围内，前后的总动量必须相等。

| **Key Formula / 核心公式** | ΣQ_before = ΣQ_after; ΣB_before = ΣB_after; ΣL_before = ΣL_after; ΔS = 0 (strong/EM), ΔS = ±1 (weak); R ≈ ħ/(mc) for force range |

| **Conservation Laws Summary / 守恒定律总结** | **Always conserved:** Q, B, L (including Lₑ, Lₘ, Lₜ), energy, momentum. **Conserved in strong/EM only:** S. **Not conserved in weak:** S. |
| **Exchange Particles / 交换粒子** | Strong: gluon (g); EM: photon (γ); Weak: W⁺, W⁻, Z⁰; Gravity: graviton (hypothetical) |
