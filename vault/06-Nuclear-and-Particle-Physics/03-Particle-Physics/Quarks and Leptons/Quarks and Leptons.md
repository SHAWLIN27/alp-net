# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This topic, **Quarks and Leptons**, forms the foundation of modern particle physics. It explores the fundamental building blocks of matter, moving beyond the proton, neutron, and electron to the more elementary particles from which they are composed. Quarks are the constituents of hadrons (like protons and neutrons), while leptons are a family of particles that includes the electron and its heavier cousins (muon, tau), along with their associated neutrinos. This topic also introduces the Standard Model of particle physics, the conservation laws (baryon number, lepton number, strangeness) that govern particle interactions, and explains phenomena like beta decay at the quark level.

**Why it matters:** Understanding quarks and leptons is essential for explaining the structure of the atom, the nature of radioactive decay, and the fundamental forces of nature. It bridges the gap between nuclear physics and quantum field theory, providing a unified framework for understanding matter and energy.

**Real-world applications:** Particle accelerators (like the LHC at CERN), medical imaging (PET scans using positrons), neutrino astronomy, and the study of the early universe (cosmology). The discovery of the Higgs boson, which gives mass to quarks and leptons, was a major triumph of the Standard Model.

**Examination importance:** This is a core topic in both Cambridge 9702 (A2 Level, Paper 4) and Edexcel IAL (Unit 4). Questions frequently test the quark composition of hadrons, conservation laws in particle reactions, and the interpretation of Feynman diagrams. It is a high-difficulty topic requiring precise recall of particle properties and the ability to apply conservation rules.

**中文：**
本主题 **夸克与轻子** 构成了现代粒子物理学的基础。它探索物质的基本组成单元，超越了质子、中子和电子，深入到构成它们的更基本粒子。夸克是强子（如质子和中子）的组成部分，而轻子则是一个粒子家族，包括电子及其更重的表亲（μ子、τ子），以及它们相关的（中微子）。本主题还介绍了粒子物理学的标准模型、支配粒子相互作用的守恒定律（重子数、轻子数、奇异数），并在夸克层面解释了β衰变等现象。

**重要性：** 理解夸克和轻子对于解释原子结构、放射性衰变的本质以及自然界的基本力至关重要。它连接了核物理学和量子场论，为理解物质和能量提供了一个统一的框架。

**实际应用：** 粒子加速器（如欧洲核子研究组织的大型强子对撞机LHC）、医学成像（使用正电子的PET扫描）、中微子天文学以及早期宇宙的研究（宇宙学）。希格斯玻色子的发现（它赋予夸克和轻子质量）是标准模型的一项重大胜利。

**考试重要性：** 这是剑桥9702（A2 Level，试卷4）和爱德思IAL（单元4）的核心主题。问题经常测试强子的夸克组成、粒子反应中的守恒定律以及费曼图的解释。这是一个高难度主题，需要精确记忆粒子性质并能够应用守恒规则。

# 4. Key Concepts Explained / 关键概念详解

## 4.1 Fundamental Particles / 基本粒子

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
The Standard Model of particle physics classifies all known matter into two fundamental families: **quarks** and **leptons**. These are considered "fundamental" because they have no internal structure—they are not made of anything smaller. There are six types (or "flavours") of quarks: up (u), down (d), strange (s), charm (c), top (t), and bottom (b). Similarly, there are six leptons: electron (e⁻), muon (μ⁻), tau (τ⁻), and their associated neutrinos (νₑ, ν_μ, ν_τ). Each particle also has a corresponding antiparticle.

**中文：**
粒子物理学的标准模型将所有已知物质分为两个基本家族：**夸克** 和 **轻子**。它们被认为是“基本的”，因为它们没有内部结构——它们不是由任何更小的东西组成的。有六种类型（或“味”）的夸克：上夸克 (u)、下夸克 (d)、奇异夸克 (s)、粲夸克 (c)、顶夸克 (t) 和底夸克 (b)。同样，有六种轻子：电子 (e⁻)、μ子 (μ⁻)、τ子 (τ⁻) 及其相关的中微子 (νₑ, ν_μ, ν_τ)。每个粒子也有一个对应的反粒子。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
These particles are the "alphabet" from which all matter in the universe is built. Protons and neutrons (which make up atomic nuclei) are composed of up and down quarks. Electrons orbit the nucleus. Neutrinos are produced in nuclear reactions (e.g., the Sun) and pass through matter almost without interaction.

**中文：**
这些粒子是构成宇宙中所有物质的“字母表”。质子和中子（构成原子核）由上夸克和下夸克组成。电子绕原子核运行。中微子在核反应（如太阳）中产生，并且几乎不与物质相互作用地穿过物质。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **Misconception:** Protons and neutrons are fundamental particles.
  **Truth:** They are composite particles made of quarks.
- **Misconception:** There are only three quarks (up, down, strange).
  **Truth:** There are six quarks, though the top and bottom are very heavy and only produced in high-energy collisions.
- **Misconception:** Neutrinos have no mass.
  **Truth:** Neutrinos have a very small, non-zero mass (as evidenced by neutrino oscillations).

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:**
- Memorise the six quark flavours and their charges.
- Be able to state that quarks and leptons are fundamental particles.
- CIE may ask you to recall the properties of all six quarks; Edexcel focuses more on up, down, and strange.

**中文：**
- 记住六种夸克味及其电荷。
- 能够说明夸克和轻子是基本粒子。
- 剑桥可能要求你回忆所有六种夸克的性质；爱德思更侧重于上、下和奇异夸克。

## 4.2 Quark Composition of Hadrons / 强子的夸克组成

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Hadrons are composite particles made of quarks. They are divided into two main categories:
- **Baryons:** Made of three quarks (qqq). Examples: proton (uud), neutron (udd).
- **Mesons:** Made of a quark and an antiquark (q\bar{q}). Examples: pion⁺ (u\bar{d}), kaon⁺ (u\bar{s}).

The charge of a hadron is the sum of the charges of its constituent quarks. For example, a proton (uud) has charge: (+⅔) + (+⅔) + (-⅓) = +1e.

**中文：**
强子是由夸克组成的复合粒子。它们分为两大类：
- **重子：** 由三个夸克组成 (qqq)。例子：质子 (uud)，中子 (udd)。
- **介子：** 由一个夸克和一个反夸克组成 (q\bar{q})。例子：π⁺介子 (u\bar{d})，K⁺介子 (u\bar{s})。

强子的电荷是其组成夸克电荷的总和。例如，质子 (uud) 的电荷为：(+⅔) + (+⅔) + (-⅓) = +1e。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
This explains why protons are positively charged and neutrons are neutral. It also explains the existence of many other short-lived particles (e.g., pions, kaons) that are produced in high-energy collisions.

**中文：**
这解释了为什么质子带正电而中子不带电。它也解释了许多其他短寿命粒子（如π介子、K介子）在高能碰撞中产生的原因。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **Misconception:** A neutron is made of three down quarks (ddd).
  **Truth:** A neutron is udd (one up, two down).
- **Misconception:** Mesons are not hadrons.
  **Truth:** Mesons are a subclass of hadrons.
- **Misconception:** The proton's charge comes from a single quark.
  **Truth:** The charge is the sum of the fractional charges of three quarks.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:**
- You must memorise the quark composition of the proton (uud) and neutron (udd).
- Be able to deduce the quark composition of other hadrons from their charge and strangeness.
- CIE may ask you to determine the quark composition of a meson given its charge.

**中文：**
- 你必须记住质子 (uud) 和中子 (udd) 的夸克组成。
- 能够根据其他强子的电荷和奇异数推断其夸克组成。
- 剑桥可能会要求你根据电荷确定介子的夸克组成。

## 4.3 Beta Decay in Terms of Quarks / 以夸克表述的β衰变

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Beta decay is a weak nuclear interaction that can be understood at the quark level.
- **Beta-minus (β⁻) decay:** A neutron (udd) transforms into a proton (uud). At the quark level, a down quark changes into an up quark: d → u + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ. An electron and an antineutrino are emitted.
- **Beta-plus (β⁺) decay:** A proton (uud) transforms into a neutron (udd). At the quark level, an up quark changes into a down quark: u → d + e⁺ + νₑ. A positron and a neutrino are emitted.

**中文：**
β衰变是一种弱核相互作用，可以在夸克层面理解。
- **β⁻ 衰变：** 中子 (udd) 转变为质子 (uud)。在夸克层面，一个下夸克变为一个上夸克：d → u + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ。发射出一个电子和一个反中微子。
- **β⁺ 衰变：** 质子 (uud) 转变为中子 (udd)。在夸克层面，一个上夸克变为一个下夸克：u → d + e⁺ + νₑ。发射出一个正电子和一个中微子。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
This explains why beta decay changes the atomic number (Z) by ±1 while the mass number (A) remains constant. It also shows that the weak interaction can change quark flavour.

**中文：**
这解释了为什么β衰变使原子序数 (Z) 改变 ±1，而质量数 (A) 保持不变。它也表明弱相互作用可以改变夸克味。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **Misconception:** The electron emitted in β⁻ decay comes from the nucleus.
  **Truth:** The electron is created at the moment of decay from the energy released.
- **Misconception:** Beta decay is a strong interaction.
  **Truth:** Beta decay is a weak interaction.
- **Misconception:** The neutrino is inside the nucleus before decay.
  **Truth:** The neutrino is created during the decay.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:**
- Be able to write the quark-level equations for both β⁻ and β⁺ decay.
- Understand that the weak interaction is responsible for changing quark flavour.
- Edexcel explicitly requires this understanding; CIE also covers it.

**中文：**
- 能够写出β⁻和β⁺衰变的夸克级方程。
- 理解弱相互作用负责改变夸克味。
- 爱德思明确要求这种理解；剑桥也涵盖此内容。

## 4.4 Conservation Laws / 守恒定律

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
Particle interactions must obey several conservation laws. The key ones for this topic are:
1. **Charge (Q):** Total electric charge is conserved.
2. **Baryon Number (B):** Total baryon number is conserved in all interactions.
3. **Lepton Number (L):** Total lepton number is conserved in all interactions. (Note: In weak interactions, lepton number is conserved separately for each family: electron, muon, tau.)
4. **Strangeness (S):** Conserved in strong interactions but NOT in weak interactions.

**中文：**
粒子相互作用必须遵守几个守恒定律。本主题的关键定律是：
1. **电荷 (Q)：** 总电荷守恒。
2. **重子数 (B)：** 总重子数在所有相互作用中守恒。
3. **轻子数 (L)：** 总轻子数在所有相互作用中守恒。（注意：在弱相互作用中，轻子数对每个家族分别守恒：电子、μ子、τ子。）
4. **奇异数 (S)：** 在强相互作用中守恒，但在弱相互作用中不守恒。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
These laws determine which particle reactions are possible and which are forbidden. For example, a reaction that violates baryon number conservation cannot occur. The non-conservation of strangeness in weak interactions explains why particles like kaons (which contain strange quarks) can decay via the weak force.

**中文：**
这些定律决定了哪些粒子反应是可能的，哪些是被禁止的。例如，违反重子数守恒的反应不可能发生。弱相互作用中奇异数不守恒解释了为什么像K介子（含有奇异夸克）这样的粒子可以通过弱力衰变。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **Misconception:** Strangeness is always conserved.
  **Truth:** Strangeness is conserved in strong interactions but not in weak interactions.
- **Misconception:** Lepton number is a single global number.
  **Truth:** In weak interactions, lepton number is conserved separately for each family (e.g., electron lepton number, muon lepton number).
- **Misconception:** Baryon number can change in nuclear reactions.
  **Truth:** Baryon number is always conserved.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
**English:**
- When checking if a reaction is possible, always check charge, baryon number, lepton number, and (for strong interactions) strangeness.
- For weak interactions, do not require strangeness conservation.
- Be able to calculate the missing quantum number in a reaction.

**中文：**
- 检查反应是否可能时，始终检查电荷、重子数、轻子数以及（对于强相互作用）奇异数。
- 对于弱相互作用，不要求奇异数守恒。
- 能够计算反应中缺失的量子数。

# 5. Essential Equations / 核心公式

## 5.1 Quark Charges / 夸克电荷

**Equation / 公式:**
$$ Q_u = +\frac{2}{3}e, \quad Q_d = -\frac{1}{3}e, \quad Q_s = -\frac{1}{3}e, \quad Q_c = +\frac{2}{3}e, \quad Q_t = +\frac{2}{3}e, \quad Q_b = -\frac{1}{3}e $$

**Variables / 变量:**
| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Q_u | Charge of up quark | 上夸克的电荷 | e (elementary charge) |
| Q_d | Charge of down quark | 下夸克的电荷 | e |
| Q_s | Charge of strange quark | 奇异夸克的电荷 | e |
| Q_c | Charge of charm quark | 粲夸克的电荷 | e |
| Q_t | Charge of top quark | 顶夸克的电荷 | e |
| Q_b | Charge of bottom quark | 底夸克的电荷 | e |
| e | Elementary charge (1.60 × 10⁻¹⁹ C) | 基本电荷 | C |

**Derivation / 推导:**
**English:** These charges are fundamental properties of the quarks, determined experimentally. They are not derived from other equations. The fractional nature of quark charges is a key feature of the Standard Model.

**中文：** 这些电荷是夸克的基本性质，由实验确定。它们不是从其他方程推导出来的。夸克电荷的分数性质是标准模型的一个关键特征。

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
**English:** Always applicable. These are intrinsic properties of quarks.

**中文：** 始终适用。这些是夸克的内在属性。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
**English:** Quarks are never observed in isolation due to confinement. Their charges are inferred from the charges of hadrons.

**中文：** 由于禁闭，夸克从未被单独观察到。它们的电荷是从强子的电荷推断出来的。

**Rearrangements / 变形:**
**English:** The total charge of a hadron is the sum of the charges of its constituent quarks: Q_total = Σ Q_i.

**中文：** 强子的总电荷是其组成夸克电荷的总和：Q_total = Σ Q_i。

## 5.2 Baryon Number Conservation / 重子数守恒

**Equation / 公式:**
$$ \sum B_{\text{initial}} = \sum B_{\text{final}} $$

**Variables / 变量:**
| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| B | Baryon number | 重子数 | dimensionless |
| Σ B_initial | Sum of baryon numbers before interaction | 相互作用前的重子数总和 | dimensionless |
| Σ B_final | Sum of baryon numbers after interaction | 相互作用后的重子数总和 | dimensionless |

**Derivation / 推导:**
**English:** This is a fundamental conservation law of nature, not derived from other principles. It is an empirical law based on experimental observations.

**中文：** 这是一个基本的自然守恒定律，不是从其他原理推导出来的。它是基于实验观察的经验定律。

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
**English:** Applies to all particle interactions (strong, weak, electromagnetic).

**中文：** 适用于所有粒子相互作用（强、弱、电磁）。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
**English:** Baryon number conservation is believed to be violated in some Grand Unified Theories (GUTs), but this has not been experimentally observed.

**中文：** 在一些大统一理论 (GUT) 中，重子数守恒被认为会被违反，但这尚未被实验观察到。

**Rearrangements / 变形:**
**English:** If one baryon number is unknown, it can be calculated: B_unknown = Σ B_initial - Σ B_known_final.

**中文：** 如果某个重子数未知，可以计算：B_unknown = Σ B_initial - Σ B_known_final。

## 5.3 Lepton Number Conservation / 轻子数守恒

**Equation / 公式:**
$$ \sum L_{\text{initial}} = \sum L_{\text{final}} $$

**Variables / 变量:**
| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| L | Lepton number | 轻子数 | dimensionless |
| Σ L_initial | Sum of lepton numbers before interaction | 相互作用前的轻子数总和 | dimensionless |
| Σ L_final | Sum of lepton numbers after interaction | 相互作用后的轻子数总和 | dimensionless |

**Derivation / 推导:**
**English:** Same as baryon number—an empirical conservation law.

**中文：** 与重子数相同——一个经验守恒定律。

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
**English:** Applies to all interactions. In weak interactions, lepton number is conserved separately for each family (electron, muon, tau).

**中文：** 适用于所有相互作用。在弱相互作用中，轻子数对每个家族（电子、μ子、τ子）分别守恒。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
**English:** Neutrino oscillations imply that lepton family number is not absolutely conserved, but total lepton number is still conserved.

**中文：** 中微子振荡意味着轻子家族数并非绝对守恒，但总轻子数仍然守恒。

**Rearrangements / 变形:**
**English:** Similar to baryon number, unknown lepton numbers can be calculated.

**中文：** 与重子数类似，可以计算未知的轻子数。

## 5.4 Strangeness Conservation / 奇异数守恒

**Equation / 公式:**
$$ \sum S_{\text{initial}} = \sum S_{\text{final}} \quad \text{(for strong interactions only)} $$

**Variables / 变量:**
| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| S | Strangeness | 奇异数 | dimensionless |
| Σ S_initial | Sum of strangeness before interaction | 相互作用前的奇异数总和 | dimensionless |
| Σ S_final | Sum of strangeness after interaction | 相互作用后的奇异数总和 | dimensionless |

**Derivation / 推导:**
**English:** Strangeness is conserved in strong interactions because the strong force does not change quark flavour. In weak interactions, quark flavour can change (e.g., s → u), so strangeness is not conserved.

**中文：** 奇异数在强相互作用中守恒，因为强力不改变夸克味。在弱相互作用中，夸克味可以改变（例如 s → u），因此奇异数不守恒。

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
**English:** Only applies to strong interactions. Does NOT apply to weak interactions.

**中文：** 仅适用于强相互作用。不适用于弱相互作用。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
**English:** Strangeness is not a conserved quantity in weak interactions; it can change by ±1.

**中文：** 奇异数在弱相互作用中不是守恒量；它可以改变 ±1。

**Rearrangements / 变形:**
**English:** For strong interactions, unknown strangeness can be calculated.

**中文：** 对于强相互作用，可以计算未知的奇异数。

# 8. Worked Examples / 典型例题

## Example 1: Determining Quark Composition / 确定夸克组成

### Question / 题目
**English:**
A particle has a charge of +1e and a strangeness of -1. It is a meson. Determine its quark composition.

**中文：**
一个粒子电荷为+1e，奇异数为-1。它是一个介子。确定其夸克组成。

### Solution / 解答
**English:**
1. **Identify the particle type:** It is a meson, so it is composed of a quark and an antiquark.
2. **Use strangeness:** Strangeness S = -1. A strange quark (s) has S = -1. A strange antiquark (\bar{s}) has S = +1. Therefore, the particle must contain a strange quark (s).
3. **Use charge:** The total charge is +1e. The strange quark has charge -⅓e. The other quark (or antiquark) must provide the remaining charge: +1e - (-⅓e) = +4/3e. This is not possible for a single quark or antiquark.
   *Wait, let's reconsider.* A meson is q\bar{q}. If it contains a strange quark (s, charge -⅓e), then the antiquark must have charge +4/3e to give a total of +1e. No antiquark has charge +4/3e.
   *Alternative:* The particle could contain a strange antiquark (\bar{s}, charge +⅓e, S = +1). But then S would be +1, not -1.
   *Correct approach:* The particle is a K⁺ meson. K⁺ = u\bar{s}.
   - u (up quark): charge +⅔e, S = 0
   - \bar{s} (strange antiquark): charge +⅓e, S = +1
   - Total charge: +⅔e + (+⅓e) = +1e
   - Total strangeness: 0 + (+1) = +1. This gives S = +1, not -1.
   *Let's try another combination:* K⁰ = d\bar{s}.
   - d (down quark): charge -⅓e, S = 0
   - \bar{s} (strange antiquark): charge +⅓e, S = +1
   - Total charge: -⅓e + (+⅓e) = 0
   - Total strangeness: 0 + (+1) = +1.
   *What about a particle with S = -1?* That would be a kaon with a strange quark, not a strange antiquark. For example, \bar{K⁰} = \bar{d}s.
   - \bar{d} (down antiquark): charge +⅓e, S = 0
   - s (strange quark): charge -⅓e, S = -1
   - Total charge: +⅓e + (-⅓e) = 0
   - Total strangeness: 0 + (-1) = -1.
   *For a charged particle with S = -1:* Consider a particle with a strange quark and an up antiquark: \bar{u}s.
   - \bar{u} (up antiquark): charge -⅔e, S = 0
   - s (strange quark): charge -⅓e, S = -1
   - Total charge: -⅔e + (-⅓e) = -1e. This gives charge -1e, not +1e.
   *Therefore, a meson with charge +1e and strangeness -1 does not exist in the Standard Model.*
   *Let's re-read the question:* "A particle has a charge of +1e and a strangeness of -1. It is a meson."
   *The only meson with charge +1e and strangeness -1 is not possible. Let's check if the question meant a baryon.*
   *If it were a baryon (3 quarks):* Charge +1e, S = -1.
   - It must contain one strange quark (s, charge -⅓e, S = -1).
   - The remaining two quarks must provide a total charge of +1e - (-⅓e) = +4/3e.
   - Possible combination: u (charge +⅔e) and u (charge +⅔e) gives +4/3e.
   - So the composition is uus. This is the Σ⁺ baryon.
   - Check strangeness: u (S=0) + u (S=0) + s (S=-1) = -1. Correct.
   *Conclusion: The question likely intended a baryon, not a meson. The answer is uus (Σ⁺).*

**中文：**
1. **确定粒子类型：** 它是一个介子，因此由一个夸克和一个反夸克组成。
2. **使用奇异数：** 奇异数 S = -1。奇异夸克 (s) 的 S = -1。奇异反夸克 (\bar{s}) 的 S = +1。因此，该粒子必须包含一个奇异夸克 (s)。
3. **使用电荷：** 总电荷为 +1e。奇异夸克的电荷为 -⅓e。另一个夸克（或反夸克）必须提供剩余电荷：+1e - (-⅓e) = +4/3e。这对于单个夸克或反夸克来说是不可能的。
   *等等，让我们重新考虑。* 介子是 q\bar{q}。如果它包含一个奇异夸克 (s, 电荷 -⅓e)，那么反夸克必须具有电荷 +4/3e 才能得到总电荷 +1e。没有反夸克具有电荷 +4/3e。
   *替代方案：* 该粒子可能包含一个奇异反夸克 (\bar{s}, 电荷 +⅓e, S = +1)。但那样 S 将是 +1，而不是 -1。
   *正确方法：* 该粒子是 K⁺ 介子。K⁺ = u\bar{s}。
   - u（上夸克）：电荷 +⅔e，S = 0
   - \bar{s}（奇异反夸克）：电荷 +⅓e，S = +1
   - 总电荷：+⅔e + (+⅓e) = +1e
   - 总奇异数：0 + (+1) = +1。这给出 S = +1，而不是 -1。
   *让我们尝试另一种组合：* K⁰ = d\bar{s}。
   - d（下夸克）：电荷 -⅓e，S = 0
   - \bar{s}（奇异反夸克）：电荷 +⅓e，S = +1
   - 总电荷：-⅓e + (+⅓e) = 0
   - 总奇异数：0 + (+1) = +1。
   *那么 S = -1 的粒子呢？* 那将是一个带有奇异夸克（而不是奇异反夸克）的K介子。例如，\bar{K⁰} = \bar{d}s。
   - \bar{d}（下反夸克）：电荷 +⅓e，S = 0
   - s（奇异夸克）：电荷 -⅓e，S = -1
   - 总电荷：+⅓e + (-⅓e) = 0
   - 总奇异数：0 + (-1) = -1。
   *对于 S = -1 的带电粒子：* 考虑一个带有奇异夸克和上反夸克的粒子：\bar{u}s。
   - \bar{u}（上反夸克）：电荷 -⅔e，S = 0
   - s（奇异夸克）：电荷 -⅓e，S = -1
   - 总电荷：-⅔e + (-⅓e) = -1e。这给出电荷 -1e，而不是 +1e。
   *因此，在标准模型中不存在电荷为+1e且奇异数为-1的介子。*
   *让我们重新阅读问题：* "一个粒子电荷为+1e，奇异数为-1。它是一个介子。"
   *唯一电荷为+1e且奇异数为-1的介子是不可能的。让我们检查一下问题是否指的是重子。*
   *如果它是一个重子（3个夸克）：* 电荷 +1e，S = -1。
   - 它必须包含一个奇异夸克 (s, 电荷 -⅓e, S = -1)。
   - 剩下的两个夸克必须提供总电荷 +1e - (-⅓e) = +4/3e。
   - 可能的组合：u (电荷 +⅔e) 和 u (电荷 +⅔e) 给出 +4/3e。
   - 所以组成是 uus。这是 Σ⁺ 重子。
   - 检查奇异数：u (S=0) + u (S=0) + s (S=-1) = -1。正确。
   *结论：问题可能本意是重子，而不是介子。答案是 uus (Σ⁺)。*

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** uus (the Σ⁺ baryon) | **答案：** uus (Σ⁺ 重子)

- 始终首先检查粒子是重子还是介子。

### Alternative Method / 替代方法
**English:**
Use a systematic approach: Write down the possible quark combinations that give the correct charge and strangeness, then check if they form a valid hadron.

**中文：**
使用系统方法：写下给出正确电荷和奇异数的可能夸克组合，然后检查它们是否形成有效的强子。

## Example 2: Applying Conservation Laws / 应用守恒定律

### Question / 题目
**English:**
Determine whether the following reaction is possible:
$$ p + \bar{p} \rightarrow \pi^+ + \pi^- + \pi^0 $$
where p is a proton, \bar{p} is an antiproton, and π⁺, π⁻, π⁰ are pions.

**中文：**
确定以下反应是否可能：
$$ p + \bar{p} \rightarrow \pi^+ + \pi^- + \pi^0 $$
其中 p 是质子，\bar{p} 是反质子，π⁺、π⁻、π⁰ 是π介子。

### Solution / 解答
**English:**
1. **Check Charge (Q):**
   - Initial: p (+1e) + \bar{p} (-1e) = 0
   - Final: π⁺ (+1e) + π⁻ (-1e) + π⁰ (0) = 0
   - Charge is conserved.

2. **Check Baryon Number (B):**
   - Initial: p (B = +1) + \bar{p} (B = -1) = 0
   - Final: π⁺ (B = 0) + π⁻ (B = 0) + π⁰ (B = 0) = 0
   - Baryon number is conserved.

3. **Check Lepton Number (L):**
   - Initial: p (L = 0) + \bar{p} (L = 0) = 0
   - Final: π⁺ (L = 0) + π⁻ (L = 0) + π⁰ (L = 0) = 0
   - Lepton number is conserved.

4. **Check Strangeness (S):**
   - Initial: p (S = 0) + \bar{p} (S = 0) = 0
   - Final: π⁺ (S = 0) + π⁻ (S = 0) + π⁰ (S = 0) = 0
   - Strangeness is conserved.

5. **Conclusion:** All conservation laws are satisfied. The reaction is possible. (It is a common annihilation reaction.)

**中文：**
1. **检查电荷 (Q)：**
   - 初始：p (+1e) + \bar{p} (-1e) = 0
   - 最终：π⁺ (+1e) + π⁻ (-1e) + π⁰ (0) = 0
   - 电荷守恒。

2. **检查重子数 (B)：**
   - 初始：p (B = +1) + \bar{p} (B = -1) = 0
   - 最终：π⁺ (B = 0) + π⁻ (B = 0) + π⁰ (B = 0) = 0
   - 重子数守恒。

3. **检查轻子数 (L)：**
   - 初始：p (L = 0) + \bar{p} (L = 0) = 0
   - 最终：π⁺ (L = 0) + π⁻ (L = 0) + π⁰ (L = 0) = 0
   - 轻子数守恒。

4. **检查奇异数 (S)：**
   - 初始：p (S = 0) + \bar{p} (S = 0) = 0
   - 最终：π⁺ (S = 0) + π⁻ (S = 0) + π⁰ (S = 0) = 0
   - 奇异数守恒。

5. **结论：** 所有守恒定律都满足。该反应是可能的。（这是一个常见的湮灭反应。）

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** Yes, the reaction is possible. | **答案：** 是的，该反应是可能的。

### Examiner Notes / 考官点评
**English:**
- This is a standard exam question type.
- Always check all four conservation laws (charge, baryon number, lepton number, strangeness).
- For strong interactions, strangeness must be conserved. For weak interactions, it does not need to be.
- A common mistake is to forget to check baryon number for antiparticles.

**中文：**
- 这是一种标准的考试题型。
- 始终检查所有四个守恒定律（电荷、重子数、轻子数、奇异数）。
- 对于强相互作用，奇异数必须守恒。对于弱相互作用，则不需要。
- 一个常见的错误是忘记检查反粒子的重子数。

### Alternative Method / 替代方法
**English:**
You can also check energy and momentum conservation, but these are not typically required for A-Level questions on this topic.

**中文：**
你也可以检查能量和动量守恒，但A-Level关于此主题的问题通常不要求这样做。
