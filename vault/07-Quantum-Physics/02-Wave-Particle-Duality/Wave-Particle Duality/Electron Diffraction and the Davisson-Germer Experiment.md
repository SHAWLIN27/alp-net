# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This sub-topic explores the experimental verification of [[de Broglie Wavelength|de Broglie's hypothesis]] that matter exhibits wave-like properties. The [[Davisson-Germer Experiment]] (1927) was the first direct experimental confirmation that electrons behave as waves, demonstrating [[Electron Diffraction]] when scattered by a nickel crystal. This experiment is historically significant because it established [[Wave-Particle Duality for Matter]] as a fundamental principle of quantum mechanics. The diffraction pattern observed matches the de Broglie wavelength prediction, proving that particles have wave nature. This concept is essential for understanding modern technologies like [[The Electron Microscope]], which relies on electron diffraction to achieve much higher resolution than optical microscopes.

**中文:**
本子知识点探讨了[[de Broglie Wavelength|德布罗意假说]]的实验验证，即物质表现出波动性质。[[Davisson-Germer Experiment|戴维森-革末实验]]（1927年）是第一个直接证明电子具有波动行为的实验，展示了电子在镍晶体表面散射时产生的[[Electron Diffraction|电子衍射]]。该实验在历史上具有重要意义，因为它确立了[[Wave-Particle Duality for Matter|物质的波粒二象性]]作为量子力学的基本原理。观察到的衍射图案与德布罗意波长预测一致，证明了粒子具有波动性质。这一概念对于理解[[The Electron Microscope|电子显微镜]]等现代技术至关重要，电子显微镜利用电子衍射实现比光学显微镜高得多的分辨率。

| Term (EN/CN) | Definition (EN) | Definition (CN) | Common Mistakes / 常见错误 |
|--------------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| **Electron Diffraction** / 电子衍射 | The scattering of electrons by a crystalline material, producing an interference pattern that demonstrates wave behavior | 电子被晶体材料散射产生干涉图案，证明波动行为 | Confusing with X-ray diffraction; electrons are particles but show wave patterns |
| **Davisson-Germer Experiment** / 戴维森-革末实验 | A 1927 experiment where electrons were scattered by a nickel crystal, producing a diffraction pattern that confirmed de Broglie's hypothesis | 1927年实验，电子被镍晶体散射产生衍射图案，证实德布罗意假说 | Thinking it only works with nickel; any crystal lattice works |

# 4. Key Concepts Explained / 关键概念详解

## 4.1 The Davisson-Germer Experimental Setup / 戴维森-革末实验装置

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
The [[Davisson-Germer Experiment]] used a heated filament to emit electrons, which were accelerated through a known [[Accelerating Voltage]] V. The electron beam was directed at a [[Crystal Lattice|nickel crystal]] target. A detector (Faraday cup) could be rotated to measure the intensity of scattered electrons at different angles θ from the crystal surface. The entire apparatus was in a vacuum to prevent electron scattering by air molecules. The key observation was that the scattered electron intensity showed peaks at specific angles, exactly as predicted by Bragg's law for wave diffraction: nλ = 2d sin θ, where d is the atomic spacing in the nickel crystal.

**中文:**
[[Davisson-Germer Experiment|戴维森-革末实验]]使用加热灯丝发射电子，电子通过已知的[[Accelerating Voltage|加速电压]]V加速。电子束射向[[Crystal Lattice|镍晶体]]靶。探测器（法拉第杯）可以旋转，测量不同角度θ处散射电子的强度。整个装置处于真空中，防止电子被空气分子散射。关键观察结果是散射电子强度在特定角度出现峰值，这与布拉格定律对波衍射的预测完全一致：nλ = 2d sin θ，其中d是镍晶体中的原子间距。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
The experiment demonstrates that electrons, traditionally considered particles, produce interference patterns characteristic of waves. The diffraction angle depends on the electron's de Broglie wavelength, which is controlled by the accelerating voltage. This provides direct evidence for [[Wave-Particle Duality for Matter]].

**中文:**
该实验证明，传统上被视为粒子的电子会产生波特有的干涉图案。衍射角度取决于电子的德布罗意波长，而波长由加速电压控制。这为[[Wave-Particle Duality for Matter|物质的波粒二象性]]提供了直接证据。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:**
  - Thinking electrons are "really" particles that just happen to diffract — they exhibit both properties simultaneously
  - Believing the diffraction pattern comes from individual electrons interacting with individual atoms — it's a collective wave phenomenon
  - Confusing the Davisson-Germer experiment with the double-slit experiment — this uses crystal lattice diffraction
- **中文:**
  - 认为电子"实际上"是粒子，只是碰巧发生衍射——它们同时表现出两种性质
  - 认为衍射图案来自单个电子与单个原子的相互作用——这是集体波动现象
  - 混淆戴维森-革末实验与双缝实验——本实验使用晶格衍射

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **English:** Always state that the experiment confirms λ = h/p. For calculations, remember to convert electron volts to joules (1 eV = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J). Know that increasing accelerating voltage decreases wavelength.
- **中文:** 始终说明实验证实了λ = h/p。计算时记得将电子伏特转换为焦耳（1 eV = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J）。知道增加加速电压会减小波长。

## 4.2 Bragg's Law and Electron Diffraction / 布拉格定律与电子衍射

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
When electrons strike a crystal lattice, they are scattered by the regularly spaced atoms. For constructive interference to occur, the path difference between waves scattered from adjacent atomic planes must be an integer multiple of the wavelength. This is given by [[Bragg's Law]]: nλ = 2d sin θ, where n is the order of diffraction (1, 2, 3...), λ is the de Broglie wavelength, d is the interatomic spacing, and θ is the angle between the incident beam and the crystal surface. The experiment confirmed that electrons obey this wave equation, with λ = h/p matching the de Broglie prediction.

**中文:**
当电子撞击晶格时，它们被规则排列的原子散射。要发生相长干涉，从相邻原子面散射的波之间的光程差必须是波长的整数倍。这由[[Bragg's Law|布拉格定律]]给出：nλ = 2d sin θ，其中n是衍射级数（1, 2, 3...），λ是德布罗意波长，d是原子间距，θ是入射束与晶体表面之间的角度。实验证实电子遵循这个波动方程，且λ = h/p与德布罗意预测一致。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
The fact that electrons satisfy Bragg's law proves they have wave nature. The wavelength is not arbitrary but is precisely given by de Broglie's relation. This connects the particle properties (momentum p) to wave properties (wavelength λ) through Planck's constant h.

**中文:**
电子满足布拉格定律这一事实证明了它们具有波动性质。波长不是任意的，而是由德布罗意关系精确给出。这通过普朗克常数h将粒子性质（动量p）与波动性质（波长λ）联系起来。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:**
  - Thinking θ is the angle from the normal — it's from the surface plane
  - Believing only first-order diffraction (n=1) is possible — higher orders exist but are weaker
  - Forgetting that d is the atomic spacing, not the crystal size
- **中文:**
  - 认为θ是从法线测量的角度——实际上是从表面平面测量的
  - 认为只有一级衍射（n=1）是可能的——存在更高级数但强度较弱
  - 忘记d是原子间距，不是晶体尺寸

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **English:** For CAIE, you need to explain qualitatively. For Edexcel, be prepared to calculate λ from V, then use Bragg's law to find d or θ. Remember: λ = h/√(2meV) for electrons accelerated through voltage V.
- **中文:** CAIE需要定性解释。Edexcel需要从V计算λ，然后用布拉格定律求d或θ。记住：对于通过电压V加速的电子，λ = h/√(2meV)。

## 4.3 Electron Diffraction Pattern Interpretation / 电子衍射图案解读

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
The diffraction pattern from a crystal consists of bright spots (maxima) arranged in a symmetrical pattern corresponding to the crystal's atomic structure. For a polycrystalline sample (many small crystals randomly oriented), the pattern becomes concentric rings. The radius of each ring depends on the electron wavelength and the crystal spacing. As accelerating voltage increases, the rings become smaller (shorter wavelength → smaller diffraction angle). This is analogous to [[The Photoelectric Effect|photoelectric effect]] in that both experiments reveal quantum behavior, but diffraction shows wave nature while photoelectric effect shows particle nature.

**中文:**
晶体的衍射图案由对称排列的亮斑（极大值）组成，对应晶体的原子结构。对于多晶样品（许多随机取向的小晶体），图案变为同心环。每个环的半径取决于电子波长和晶体间距。随着加速电压增加，环变小（波长更短→衍射角更小）。这与[[The Photoelectric Effect|光电效应]]类似，两者都揭示量子行为，但衍射显示波动性质，而光电效应显示粒子性质。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
The diffraction pattern is a direct visualization of the electron's wave nature. Each bright spot corresponds to constructive interference from a specific set of atomic planes. The pattern contains information about the crystal structure, which is why [[The Electron Microscope]] can image atomic arrangements.

**中文:**
衍射图案是电子波动性质的直接可视化。每个亮斑对应特定原子面的相长干涉。图案包含晶体结构信息，这就是[[The Electron Microscope|电子显微镜]]能够成像原子排列的原因。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **English:**
  - Thinking the pattern is from single-slit diffraction — it's from crystal lattice diffraction
  - Believing the pattern changes with electron intensity — it depends on wavelength, not intensity
  - Confusing ring patterns with powder X-ray diffraction — the principle is the same
- **中文:**
  - 认为图案来自单缝衍射——实际上来自晶格衍射
  - 认为图案随电子强度变化——取决于波长，而非强度
  - 混淆环状图案与粉末X射线衍射——原理相同

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **English:** Be able to sketch diffraction patterns for single crystal (spots) vs polycrystalline (rings). Explain why rings form: random orientation of crystals gives all possible diffraction angles.
- **中文:** 能够画出单晶（斑点）和多晶（环）的衍射图案。解释环形成的原因：晶体随机取向给出所有可能的衍射角。

# 5. Essential Equations / 核心公式

## 5.1 de Broglie Wavelength / 德布罗意波长

$$ \lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv} $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| λ | de Broglie wavelength | 德布罗意波长 | m |
| h | Planck's constant (6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s) | 普朗克常数 | J·s |
| p | Momentum of particle | 粒子动量 | kg·m·s⁻¹ |
| m | Mass of particle | 粒子质量 | kg |
| v | Velocity of particle | 粒子速度 | m·s⁻¹ |

**Derivation / 推导:**
Proposed by Louis de Broglie in 1924, inspired by Einstein's photon theory (E = hf = hc/λ) and the momentum of photons (p = h/λ). De Broglie hypothesized that if light (a wave) has particle properties, then matter (particles) should have wave properties with the same relation.

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- **English:** Applies to all moving particles. For macroscopic objects, λ is too small to detect. Only significant for particles with very small mass (electrons, neutrons, atoms).
- **中文:** 适用于所有运动粒子。对于宏观物体，λ太小无法检测。仅对质量极小的粒子（电子、中子、原子）有意义。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
- **English:** Does not describe the wave function itself — only gives the wavelength. The actual wave behavior requires quantum mechanics (Schrödinger equation).
- **中文:** 不描述波函数本身——仅给出波长。实际波动行为需要量子力学（薛定谔方程）。

## 5.2 Electron Wavelength from Accelerating Voltage / 从加速电压计算电子波长

$$ \lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2meV}} $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| e | Elementary charge (1.60 × 10⁻¹⁹ C) | 元电荷 | C |
| V | Accelerating voltage | 加速电压 | V |
| m | Electron mass (9.11 × 10⁻³¹ kg) | 电子质量 | kg |

**Derivation / 推导:**
Kinetic energy of electron: $E_k = eV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$
Therefore: $v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m}}$
Momentum: $p = mv = m\sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m}} = \sqrt{2meV}$
Substitute into λ = h/p: $\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2meV}}$

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- **English:** Valid for non-relativistic electrons (V < 50 kV). For higher voltages, relativistic corrections are needed. Assumes all electrical potential energy converts to kinetic energy.
- **中文:** 适用于非相对论电子（V < 50 kV）。更高电压需要相对论修正。假设所有电势能转化为动能。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
- **English:** Does not account for relativistic effects at high voltages. For A-Level, this formula is sufficient for typical accelerating voltages (50-500 V).
- **中文:** 不适用于高电压下的相对论效应。A-Level考试中，此公式对典型加速电压（50-500 V）足够。

## 5.3 Bragg's Law for Electron Diffraction / 电子衍射的布拉格定律

$$ n\lambda = 2d\sin\theta $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| n | Order of diffraction (1, 2, 3...) | 衍射级数 | dimensionless |
| d | Interatomic spacing in crystal | 晶体原子间距 | m |
| θ | Angle between incident beam and crystal surface | 入射束与晶体表面夹角 | degrees or radians |

**Derivation / 推导:**
Consider two parallel atomic planes separated by distance d. The path difference between waves scattered from adjacent planes is 2d sin θ. For constructive interference, this must equal nλ.

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- **English:** Valid for crystalline materials with regular atomic spacing. The crystal must be oriented correctly relative to the incident beam. Only works when λ ≤ 2d (otherwise no diffraction possible).
- **中文:** 适用于具有规则原子间距的晶体材料。晶体必须相对于入射束正确取向。仅当λ ≤ 2d时有效（否则不可能发生衍射）。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
- **English:** Assumes perfect crystal structure. Real crystals have defects that affect diffraction patterns. Only describes the direction of diffraction maxima, not their intensity.
- **中文:** 假设完美晶体结构。实际晶体有缺陷，影响衍射图案。仅描述衍射极大值的方向，不描述强度。

# 6. Graphs and Relationships / 图表与关系

## 6.1 Electron Intensity vs Scattering Angle / 电子强度与散射角关系

### Axes / 坐标轴
- **X-axis:** Scattering angle θ (degrees) / 散射角θ（度）
- **Y-axis:** Scattered electron intensity (arbitrary units) / 散射电子强度（任意单位）

### Shape / 形状
**English:** The graph shows sharp peaks at specific angles corresponding to Bragg diffraction conditions. Between peaks, intensity is low. As accelerating voltage increases, peaks shift to smaller angles (shorter wavelength → smaller diffraction angle). The first-order peak (n=1) is strongest; higher-order peaks (n=2, 3...) are progressively weaker.

**中文:** 图形在对应布拉格衍射条件的特定角度出现尖锐峰值。峰值之间强度较低。随着加速电压增加，峰值向更小角度移动（波长更短→衍射角更小）。一级峰值（n=1）最强；更高级峰值（n=2, 3...）逐渐减弱。

### Gradient Meaning / 斜率含义
**English:** The gradient at any point represents the rate of change of intensity with angle. Near peaks, gradient changes from positive to negative (maximum). The steepness indicates how sharp the diffraction peak is.

**中文:** 任意点的斜率表示强度随角度的变化率。峰值附近，斜率从正变为负（极大值）。陡峭程度表示衍射峰的尖锐程度。

### Area Meaning / 面积含义
**English:** The area under a peak represents the total number of electrons diffracted in that direction. This relates to the scattering probability and crystal structure.

**中文:** 峰值下的面积表示在该方向衍射的电子总数。这与散射概率和晶体结构有关。

### Exam Interpretation / 考试解读
**English:** Be able to: (1) Identify which peak corresponds to which order of diffraction, (2) Explain how changing V shifts peak positions, (3) Calculate d from peak position and known λ, (4) Predict what happens if crystal is rotated.

**中文:** 能够：(1) 识别哪个峰值对应哪个衍射级数，(2) 解释改变V如何移动峰值位置，(3) 从峰值位置和已知λ计算d，(4) 预测旋转晶体时会发生什么。

**English:** Two diffraction patterns: (a) Single crystal — a regular array of bright spots arranged in a symmetrical pattern reflecting the crystal's atomic structure; (b) Polycrystalline — concentric bright rings of varying intensity, with the innermost ring being brightest.

**中文:** 两种衍射图案：(a) 单晶——规则排列的亮斑，对称分布，反映晶体的原子结构；(b) 多晶——不同强度的同心亮环，最内环最亮。

# 8. Worked Examples / 典型例题

## Example 1: Calculating de Broglie Wavelength / 例1：计算德布罗意波长

### Question / 题目
**English:**
Electrons are accelerated through a potential difference of 150 V in a Davisson-Germer experiment.
(a) Calculate the de Broglie wavelength of these electrons.
(b) If the nickel crystal has an atomic spacing of 0.215 nm, at what angle θ will the first-order diffraction maximum (n=1) be observed?

Given: h = 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s, mₑ = 9.11 × 10⁻³¹ kg, e = 1.60 × 10⁻¹⁹ C

**中文:**
在戴维森-革末实验中，电子通过150 V的电势差加速。
(a) 计算这些电子的德布罗意波长。
(b) 如果镍晶体的原子间距为0.215 nm，一级衍射极大值（n=1）将在什么角度θ观察到？

已知：h = 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s，mₑ = 9.11 × 10⁻³¹ kg，e = 1.60 × 10⁻¹⁹ C

### Solution / 解答

**Part (a):**

Step 1: Use the formula for electron wavelength from accelerating voltage:
$$ \lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2meV}} $$

Step 2: Substitute values:
$$ \lambda = \frac{6.63 \times 10^{-34}}{\sqrt{2 \times 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \times 1.60 \times 10^{-19} \times 150}} $$

Step 3: Calculate the denominator:
$$ 2 \times 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \times 1.60 \times 10^{-19} \times 150 = 4.37 \times 10^{-47} $$
$$ \sqrt{4.37 \times 10^{-47}} = 6.61 \times 10^{-24} $$

Step 4: Calculate λ:
$$ \lambda = \frac{6.63 \times 10^{-34}}{6.61 \times 10^{-24}} = 1.00 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m} = 0.100 \text{ nm} $$

**Part (b):**

Step 1: Use Bragg's law: nλ = 2d sin θ
For n = 1: λ = 2d sin θ

Step 2: Rearrange for θ:
$$ \sin\theta = \frac{\lambda}{2d} = \frac{0.100 \times 10^{-9}}{2 \times 0.215 \times 10^{-9}} = \frac{0.100}{0.430} = 0.233 $$

Step 3: Calculate θ:
$$ \theta = \sin^{-1}(0.233) = 13.5^\circ $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** (a) λ = 0.100 nm | **答案：** (a) λ = 0.100 nm
**Answer:** (b) θ = 13.5° | **答案：** (b) θ = 13.5°

### Quick Tip / 提示
**English:** Always check units — convert nm to m before calculation. Remember that λ is on the order of 0.1 nm for typical accelerating voltages (100-200 V), which is comparable to atomic spacing in crystals.

**中文:** 始终检查单位——计算前将nm转换为m。记住对于典型加速电压（100-200 V），λ约为0.1 nm，与晶体中的原子间距相当。

## Example 2: Interpreting Diffraction Pattern Changes / 例2：解读衍射图案变化

### Question / 题目
**English:**
In an electron diffraction experiment using a polycrystalline graphite sample, the accelerating voltage is increased from 100 V to 400 V.
(a) Explain what happens to the diffraction rings.
(b) Calculate the ratio of the ring radii (r₄₀₀ / r₁₀₀) for the first-order ring.

**中文:**
在使用多晶石墨样品的电子衍射实验中，加速电压从100 V增加到400 V。
(a) 解释衍射环会发生什么变化。
(b) 计算一级环的半径比（r₄₀₀ / r₁₀₀）。

### Solution / 解答

**Part (a):**

Step 1: Relationship between voltage and wavelength:
$$ \lambda \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{V}} $$
Increasing V from 100 V to 400 V (factor of 4) decreases λ by factor of √4 = 2.

Step 2: From Bragg's law: sin θ = λ/(2d)
Since λ decreases, sin θ decreases, so θ decreases.

Step 3: For a given screen distance L, ring radius r = L tan θ ≈ Lθ (for small angles).
Since θ decreases, the rings become smaller (contract inward).

**Answer:** The diffraction rings become smaller (contract toward the center) as voltage increases. The ring radius decreases by approximately a factor of 2.

**Part (b):**

Step 1: For small angles, r ∝ θ ∝ λ ∝ 1/√V

Step 2: Ratio of radii:
$$ \frac{r_{400}}{r_{100}} = \frac{\lambda_{400}}{\lambda_{100}} = \frac{\sqrt{V_{100}}}{\sqrt{V_{400}}} = \frac{\sqrt{100}}{\sqrt{400}} = \frac{10}{20} = 0.5 $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** (a) Rings contract inward, becoming smaller | **答案：** (a) 环向内收缩，变小
**Answer:** (b) r₄₀₀ / r₁₀₀ = 0.5 | **答案：** (b) r₄₀₀ / r₁₀₀ = 0.5

### Quick Tip / 提示
**English:** Remember the inverse square root relationship: λ ∝ 1/√V. This is a common exam relationship. For polycrystalline samples, ring radius is proportional to λ, so r ∝ 1/√V.

**中文:** 记住反平方根关系：λ ∝ 1/√V。这是常见的考试关系。对于多晶样品，环半径与λ成正比，所以r ∝ 1/√V。

# 10. Practical Skills Connections / 实验技能链接

**English:**
This sub-topic connects to practical skills in several ways:

1. **Measurements:** Measuring accelerating voltage (voltmeter), scattering angle (protractor/goniometer), and electron current (ammeter/galvanometer).

2. **Uncertainties:** The accelerating voltage has uncertainty from the power supply (±1% typical). The angle measurement has uncertainty from the detector positioning (±0.5° typical). These propagate to uncertainty in calculated λ and d.

3. **Graph Plotting:** Plotting intensity vs angle to identify diffraction peaks. Determining peak positions accurately requires careful interpolation.

4. **Experimental Design:** Understanding why vacuum is necessary (prevents electron scattering by air). Why a crystal is used (regular atomic spacing acts as diffraction grating). Why the detector must rotate (to measure angular distribution).

5. **Data Analysis:** Using Bragg's law to calculate atomic spacing from measured angles. Comparing calculated λ with theoretical de Broglie wavelength.

6. **Control Variables:** Keeping accelerating voltage constant during angular scans. Maintaining vacuum pressure. Ensuring crystal surface is clean.

**中文:**
本子知识点在多个方面与实验技能相关：

1. **测量：** 测量加速电压（电压表）、散射角（测角仪/分度器）和电子电流（电流计/检流计）。

2. **不确定度：** 加速电压来自电源的不确定度（典型±1%）。角度测量来自探测器定位的不确定度（典型±0.5°）。这些传播到计算出的λ和d的不确定度。

3. **绘图：** 绘制强度与角度关系图以识别衍射峰。准确确定峰值位置需要仔细插值。

4. **实验设计：** 理解为什么需要真空（防止电子被空气散射）。为什么使用晶体（规则原子间距充当衍射光栅）。为什么探测器必须旋转（测量角度分布）。

5. **数据分析：** 使用布拉格定律从测量角度计算原子间距。将计算出的λ与理论德布罗意波长比较。

6. **控制变量：** 在角度扫描期间保持加速电压恒定。维持真空压力。确保晶体表面清洁。

> 📋 **Edexcel Only:** Edexcel may require students to describe how to determine the atomic spacing of a crystal using electron diffraction, including error analysis and how to improve accuracy.

> 📋 **CIE Only:** CIE focuses more on qualitative understanding and interpretation of diffraction patterns rather than detailed experimental procedures.
