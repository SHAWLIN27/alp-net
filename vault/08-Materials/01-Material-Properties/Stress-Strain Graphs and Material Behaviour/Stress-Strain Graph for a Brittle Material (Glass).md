# Stress-Strain Graph for a Brittle Material (Glass)

---

# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This sub-topic focuses on the characteristic stress-strain graph for a **brittle material**, using glass as the primary example. Unlike ductile materials (e.g., copper) which undergo significant plastic deformation before fracture, brittle materials exhibit **very little or no plastic deformation** — they fracture suddenly at or near the elastic limit. Understanding this graph is crucial for material selection in engineering applications where sudden, catastrophic failure must be avoided (e.g., glass windows, ceramic components). This leaf node connects directly to [[Stress-Strain Graphs and Material Behaviour]] and contrasts with [[Stress-Strain Graph for a Ductile Material (Copper)]] and [[Stress-Strain Graph for a Polymeric Material (Rubber)]].

**中文:**
本子知识点专注于**脆性材料**的特征应力-应变图，以玻璃为主要例子。与延性材料（如铜）在断裂前经历显著塑性变形不同，脆性材料表现出**非常小或没有塑性变形**——它们在弹性极限处或附近突然断裂。理解该图对于工程应用中避免突然的灾难性失效（如玻璃窗、陶瓷部件）至关重要。本叶节点直接连接到[[Stress-Strain Graphs and Material Behaviour]]，并与[[Stress-Strain Graph for a Ductile Material (Copper)]]和[[Stress-Strain Graph for a Polymeric Material (Rubber)]]形成对比。

# 4. Key Concepts Explained / 关键概念详解

## 4.1 Linear Elastic Behaviour / 线性弹性行为

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** For a brittle material like glass, the stress-strain graph shows a **straight line** from the origin up to the fracture point. This indicates that the material obeys [[Hooke's Law]] ($\sigma = E \epsilon$) throughout its entire loading range. There is **no plastic region** — the material remains perfectly elastic until it suddenly breaks. The gradient of this line equals the [[Young Modulus]] ($E$).

**中文:** 对于像玻璃这样的脆性材料，应力-应变图显示从原点到断裂点的**直线**。这表明材料在整个加载范围内都遵循[[胡克定律]]（$\sigma = E \epsilon$）。**没有塑性区域**——材料保持完全弹性直到突然断裂。该线的梯度等于[[杨氏模量]]（$E$）。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** In brittle materials, the atomic bonds are strong but rigid. When stress is applied, atoms are displaced from their equilibrium positions (elastic strain). However, there are **no mechanisms for plastic deformation** (no dislocation movement, no molecular chain reorientation). Once the stress exceeds the bond strength, the bonds break catastrophically, causing sudden fracture.

**中文:** 在脆性材料中，原子键强但刚性。施加应力时，原子从其平衡位置位移（弹性应变）。然而，**没有塑性变形的机制**（没有位错运动，没有分子链重新取向）。一旦应力超过键强度，键就会灾难性地断裂，导致突然断裂。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- ❌ **"Brittle materials are weak"** — Glass has high compressive strength but low tensile strength.
- ❌ **"Brittle materials can be bent slightly before breaking"** — Glass shows negligible plastic deformation; any bending is purely elastic.
- ❌ **"The fracture point is the same as the ultimate tensile strength"** — For brittle materials, yes, but for ductile materials, UTS occurs before fracture.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- ✅ **English:** Always draw the brittle graph as a straight line ending at a point (no curve). Label "Fracture Point" clearly. State that there is **no plastic region** and **no necking**.
- ✅ **中文:** 始终将脆性材料图绘制为终止于一点的直线（无曲线）。清晰标注"断裂点"。说明**没有塑性区域**和**没有颈缩**。

## 4.2 Absence of Plastic Deformation / 无塑性变形

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** Unlike [[Stress-Strain Graph for a Ductile Material (Copper)]] which shows a yield point, plastic region, and necking, brittle materials exhibit **zero plastic strain**. The elastic limit and fracture point are essentially the same point. This means the material cannot be permanently deformed — it either returns to its original shape (if stress is removed before fracture) or it breaks.

**中文:** 与显示屈服点、塑性区域和颈缩的[[Stress-Strain Graph for a Ductile Material (Copper)]]不同，脆性材料表现出**零塑性应变**。弹性极限和断裂点本质上是同一点。这意味着材料不能永久变形——它要么恢复到原始形状（如果在断裂前移除应力），要么断裂。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** In glass, the atomic structure is amorphous (non-crystalline) with strong covalent bonds. There are no slip planes or dislocations that allow atoms to slide past each other (as in metals). When stress is applied, bonds stretch elastically until they reach their breaking point, then fail simultaneously across a plane.

**中文:** 在玻璃中，原子结构是非晶态（非晶体）的，具有强共价键。没有允许原子相互滑过的滑移面或位错（如金属中）。施加应力时，键弹性拉伸直到达到断裂点，然后在一个平面上同时失效。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- ❌ **"Brittle materials have a yield point"** — No, they have no yield point; they go directly from elastic to fracture.
- ❌ **"Brittle materials can be work-hardened"** — Work hardening requires plastic deformation, which does not occur.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- ✅ **English:** When asked "Why does glass have no plastic region?", answer: "Because the atomic structure has no mechanisms for plastic deformation (no dislocations/slip planes); bonds break suddenly when stress exceeds bond strength."
- ✅ **中文:** 当被问"为什么玻璃没有塑性区域？"时，回答："因为原子结构没有塑性变形的机制（没有位错/滑移面）；当应力超过键强度时，键突然断裂。"

## 4.3 Fracture Mechanism: No Necking / 断裂机制：无颈缩

### Explanation / 解释
**English:** In brittle fracture, cracks propagate rapidly through the material **without any prior necking**. The fracture surface is typically flat and perpendicular to the tensile axis. This is because the material does not undergo plastic deformation that would cause localized thinning (necking) before breaking. The fracture is **catastrophic** — it happens suddenly with no warning.

**中文:** 在脆性断裂中，裂纹**没有任何先前的颈缩**就迅速穿过材料扩展。断裂表面通常是平坦的，垂直于拉伸轴。这是因为材料不会经历会导致局部变薄（颈缩）的塑性变形。断裂是**灾难性的**——突然发生，没有预警。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:** Necking requires plastic flow — atoms sliding past each other to accommodate the strain. Since glass cannot plastically deform, the cross-sectional area remains constant until fracture. When a crack initiates (often at a surface flaw), stress concentrates at the crack tip, causing it to propagate at near the speed of sound through the material.

**中文:** 颈缩需要塑性流动——原子相互滑过以适应应变。由于玻璃不能塑性变形，横截面积在断裂前保持不变。当裂纹萌生时（通常在表面缺陷处），应力集中在裂纹尖端，使其以接近声速的速度穿过材料扩展。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- ❌ **"Brittle materials show necking before fracture"** — Absolutely not; necking is a ductile phenomenon.
- ❌ **"Brittle fracture is slow and gradual"** — It is sudden and catastrophic.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- ✅ **English:** Key phrase: "Brittle materials fracture without necking." Explain that the cross-sectional area remains constant until sudden failure.
- ✅ **中文:** 关键短语："脆性材料断裂时无颈缩。"解释横截面积在突然失效前保持不变。

# 5. Essential Equations / 核心公式

## 5.1 Young Modulus from Brittle Graph / 从脆性材料图计算杨氏模量

$$ E = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon} = \text{gradient of stress-strain graph} $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $E$ | Young Modulus | 杨氏模量 | Pa (or N m$^{-2}$) |
| $\sigma$ | Stress at any point on the linear region | 线性区域任意点的应力 | Pa |
| $\epsilon$ | Strain at the same point | 同一点的应变 | dimensionless (无量纲) |

**Derivation / 推导:**
From Hooke's Law: $\sigma = E \epsilon$, rearranging gives $E = \sigma / \epsilon$. For a linear graph, this is the gradient.

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- **English:** Material must obey Hooke's Law (linear elastic behaviour). Valid only within the elastic region (which for brittle materials is the entire graph up to fracture).
- **中文:** 材料必须遵循胡克定律（线性弹性行为）。仅在弹性区域内有效（对于脆性材料，这是整个图直到断裂点）。

**Limitations / 局限性:**
- **English:** Only applicable for the linear portion. For brittle materials, this is the entire graph, but you must ensure you are reading values from the straight line (not extrapolating beyond fracture).
- **中文:** 仅适用于线性部分。对于脆性材料，这是整个图，但必须确保从直线上读取数值（不要外推到断裂点之外）。

---

## 5.2 Stress at Fracture / 断裂应力

$$ \sigma_{\text{fracture}} = \frac{F_{\text{max}}}{A_0} $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $\sigma_{\text{fracture}}$ | Fracture stress (breaking stress) | 断裂应力（破坏应力） | Pa |
| $F_{\text{max}}$ | Maximum force before fracture | 断裂前的最大力 | N |
| $A_0$ | Original cross-sectional area | 原始横截面积 | m$^2$ |

**Conditions / 适用条件:**
- **English:** For brittle materials, $A_0$ is used (not reduced area) because there is no necking.
- **中文:** 对于脆性材料，使用$A_0$（不是减小后的面积），因为没有颈缩。

## 6.1 Stress-Strain Graph for Glass / 玻璃的应力-应变图

### Axes / 坐标轴
- **Y-axis:** Stress $\sigma$ (Pa or N m$^{-2}$) / 应力 $\sigma$ (帕斯卡或牛每平方米)
- **X-axis:** Strain $\epsilon$ (dimensionless) / 应变 $\epsilon$ (无量纲)

### Shape / 形状
- **English:** A **straight line** from the origin to the fracture point. No curve, no plateau, no yield point. The line ends abruptly at the fracture point.
- **中文:** 从原点到断裂点的**直线**。没有曲线，没有平台，没有屈服点。该线在断裂点突然结束。

### Gradient Meaning / 斜率含义
- **English:** The gradient equals the [[Young Modulus]] ($E$) of the material. A steep gradient means high stiffness (large stress for small strain).
- **中文:** 梯度等于材料的[[杨氏模量]]（$E$）。陡峭的梯度意味着高刚度（小应变产生大应力）。

### Area Meaning / 面积含义
- **English:** The area under the graph up to the fracture point represents the **elastic strain energy per unit volume** stored in the material before fracture. This is the **modulus of resilience**.
- **中文:** 图下直到断裂点的面积表示断裂前储存在材料中的**单位体积弹性应变能**。这是**回弹模量**。

### Exam Interpretation / 考试解读
- **English:** If asked to compare with a ductile material: "The brittle graph is linear throughout, while the ductile graph shows a yield point, plastic region, and necking. The brittle material stores less energy before fracture (smaller area under graph)."
- **中文:** 如果被要求与延性材料比较："脆性材料图全程线性，而延性材料图显示屈服点、塑性区域和颈缩。脆性材料在断裂前储存的能量较少（图下面积较小）。"

# 8. Worked Examples / 典型例题

## Example 1: Calculating Young Modulus from Glass Graph / 从玻璃图计算杨氏模量

### Question / 题目
**English:**
A glass rod of original length 0.500 m and cross-sectional area $2.0 \times 10^{-6}$ m$^2$ is subjected to a tensile force. The force increases until the rod fractures at a force of 140 N. The extension at fracture is $2.0 \times 10^{-3}$ m.

(a) Calculate the stress at fracture.
(b) Calculate the strain at fracture.
(c) Determine the Young modulus of the glass.
(d) Explain why the glass rod shows no necking before fracture.

**中文:**
一根原始长度为0.500米、横截面积为$2.0 \times 10^{-6}$平方米的玻璃棒受到拉伸力。力增加直到棒在140牛的力下断裂。断裂时的伸长为$2.0 \times 10^{-3}$米。

(a) 计算断裂时的应力。
(b) 计算断裂时的应变。
(c) 确定玻璃的杨氏模量。
(d) 解释为什么玻璃棒在断裂前没有显示颈缩。

### Solution / 解答

**(a) Stress at fracture / 断裂应力:**

$$ \sigma = \frac{F}{A} = \frac{140}{2.0 \times 10^{-6}} = 7.0 \times 10^7 \text{ Pa} = 70 \text{ MPa} $$

**(b) Strain at fracture / 断裂应变:**

$$ \epsilon = \frac{\Delta L}{L_0} = \frac{2.0 \times 10^{-3}}{0.500} = 4.0 \times 10^{-3} $$

**(c) Young Modulus / 杨氏模量:**

$$ E = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon} = \frac{7.0 \times 10^7}{4.0 \times 10^{-3}} = 1.75 \times 10^{10} \text{ Pa} = 17.5 \text{ GPa} $$

**(d) Explanation / 解释:**
**English:** Glass is a brittle material. Its atomic structure has no slip planes or dislocations that allow plastic deformation. When stress is applied, the bonds stretch elastically until they reach their breaking point. The material cannot undergo plastic flow, so there is no localized reduction in cross-sectional area (necking). The fracture occurs suddenly across a plane perpendicular to the tensile axis.

**中文:** 玻璃是一种脆性材料。其原子结构没有允许塑性变形的滑移面或位错。施加应力时，键弹性拉伸直到达到断裂点。材料不能发生塑性流动，因此没有横截面积的局部减小（颈缩）。断裂在垂直于拉伸轴的平面上突然发生。

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** (a) 70 MPa, (b) $4.0 \times 10^{-3}$, (c) 17.5 GPa, (d) See explanation above.
**答案：** (a) 70兆帕, (b) $4.0 \times 10^{-3}$, (c) 17.5吉帕, (d) 见上述解释。

### Quick Tip / 提示
- **English:** Always use original cross-sectional area $A_0$ for brittle materials (no necking means area doesn't change).
- **中文:** 对于脆性材料，始终使用原始横截面积$A_0$（无颈缩意味着面积不变）。

## Example 2: Comparing Energy Stored / 比较储存的能量

### Question / 题目
**English:**
A glass rod and a copper rod of identical dimensions are each stretched until they fracture. The stress-strain graph for glass is linear up to fracture at stress 70 MPa and strain $4.0 \times 10^{-3}$. The copper rod has a yield stress of 50 MPa, fractures at stress 200 MPa and strain 0.30.

Calculate the elastic strain energy per unit volume stored in each material just before fracture. Comment on the difference.

**中文:**
一根玻璃棒和一根铜棒尺寸相同，各自拉伸至断裂。玻璃的应力-应变图在断裂前是线性的，断裂应力为70兆帕，应变为$4.0 \times 10^{-3}$。铜棒的屈服应力为50兆帕，断裂应力为200兆帕，应变为0.30。

计算每种材料在断裂前储存的单位体积弹性应变能。评论差异。

### Solution / 解答

**For glass (brittle) / 对于玻璃（脆性材料）:**
Area under graph = area of triangle = $\frac{1}{2} \times \sigma \times \epsilon$

$$ U_{\text{glass}} = \frac{1}{2} \times (70 \times 10^6) \times (4.0 \times 10^{-3}) = 1.4 \times 10^5 \text{ J m}^{-3} $$

**For copper (ductile) / 对于铜（延性材料）:**
Area under graph ≈ area of triangle up to yield + area of rectangle for plastic region (approximate)

$$ U_{\text{copper}} \approx \frac{1}{2} \times (50 \times 10^6) \times (0.002) + (200 \times 10^6) \times (0.30 - 0.002) $$
$$ U_{\text{copper}} \approx 5.0 \times 10^4 + 5.96 \times 10^7 \approx 5.97 \times 10^7 \text{ J m}^{-3} $$

**Comment / 评论:**
**English:** Copper stores approximately 400 times more elastic strain energy per unit volume than glass before fracture. This is because copper undergoes significant plastic deformation, allowing it to absorb much more energy. Glass, being brittle, stores only elastic energy and fractures suddenly with little energy absorption. This explains why glass is dangerous when it breaks — the stored energy is released suddenly.

**中文:** 铜在断裂前储存的单位体积弹性应变能大约是玻璃的400倍。这是因为铜经历了显著的塑性变形，使其能够吸收更多的能量。玻璃是脆性的，只储存弹性能量，吸收的能量很少就突然断裂。这解释了为什么玻璃断裂时很危险——储存的能量突然释放。

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** Glass: $1.4 \times 10^5$ J m$^{-3}$, Copper: $\approx 5.97 \times 10^7$ J m$^{-3}$. Copper stores much more energy due to plastic deformation.
**答案：** 玻璃：$1.4 \times 10^5$ 焦耳每立方米，铜：$\approx 5.97 \times 10^7$ 焦耳每立方米。由于塑性变形，铜储存的能量多得多。

### Quick Tip / 提示
- **English:** For brittle materials, energy stored = $\frac{1}{2} \sigma \epsilon$ (triangle area). For ductile materials, you must include the plastic region area.
- **中文:** 对于脆性材料，储存的能量 = $\frac{1}{2} \sigma \epsilon$（三角形面积）。对于延性材料，必须包括塑性区域面积。

# 10. Practical Skills Connections / 实验技能链接

**English:**
This sub-topic connects to practical work in the following ways:

1. **Tensile Testing of Glass:** In a practical, you would set up a glass rod in a tensile testing machine, apply increasing force, and measure extension using a strain gauge or extensometer. You would plot the stress-strain graph and observe the sudden fracture.

2. **Measurements and Uncertainties:**
   - Measure original length $L_0$ with a ruler (±0.5 mm)
   - Measure diameter with a micrometer (±0.01 mm) to calculate cross-sectional area
   - Measure force with a force sensor (±0.1 N)
   - Measure extension with a strain gauge (±0.001 mm)
   - Calculate uncertainties in stress, strain, and Young modulus

3. **Safety Considerations:** Glass fractures suddenly and catastrophically. Safety goggles must be worn. The fractured pieces are sharp and dangerous.

4. **Graph Plotting:** Plot stress (y-axis) against strain (x-axis). Draw a best-fit straight line through the data points. Calculate gradient to find Young modulus.

5. **Comparison with Ductile Materials:** You could perform the same experiment with a copper wire and compare the graphs. Note the different shapes and the presence/absence of necking.

**中文:**
本子知识点与实验工作有以下联系：

1. **玻璃的拉伸测试：** 在实验中，你将玻璃棒安装在拉伸试验机中，施加逐渐增大的力，并使用应变片或引伸计测量伸长。你将绘制应力-应变图并观察突然断裂。

2. **测量与不确定度：**
   - 用尺子测量原始长度$L_0$（±0.5毫米）
   - 用千分尺测量直径（±0.01毫米）以计算横截面积
   - 用力传感器测量力（±0.1牛）
   - 用应变片测量伸长（±0.001毫米）
   - 计算应力、应变和杨氏模量的不确定度

3. **安全考虑：** 玻璃突然且灾难性地断裂。必须佩戴安全护目镜。断裂的碎片锋利且危险。

4. **图表绘制：** 绘制应力（y轴）对应变（x轴）的图。通过数据点绘制最佳拟合直线。计算梯度以找到杨氏模量。

5. **与延性材料的比较：** 你可以用铜线进行相同的实验并比较图形。注意不同的形状以及颈缩的存在/缺失。

# 12. Quick Revision Sheet / 速查表

| Category / 类别 | Key Points / 要点 |
|----------------|------------------|
| **Definition / 定义** | Brittle material: fractures with little/no plastic deformation. Glass is the classic example. / 脆性材料：几乎没有塑性变形就断裂。玻璃是典型例子。 |
| **Graph Shape / 图形形状** | Straight line from origin to fracture point. No curve, no yield point, no plastic region. / 从原点到断裂点的直线。无曲线，无屈服点，无塑性区域。 |
| **Key Formula / 核心公式** | $E = \sigma/\epsilon$ (gradient), $\sigma = F/A_0$, $U = \frac{1}{2}\sigma\epsilon$ (energy per unit volume) |
| **Key Graph / 核心图表** | Stress-strain graph: linear, ends at fracture point. Label: Elastic Region, Fracture Point, Young Modulus. / 应力-应变图：线性，终止于断裂点。标注：弹性区域、断裂点、杨氏模量。 |
| **No Plastic Region / 无塑性区域** | Atomic structure has no slip planes/dislocations. Bonds break suddenly when stress exceeds bond strength. / 原子结构没有滑移面/位错。当应力超过键强度时，键突然断裂。 |
| **No Necking / 无颈缩** | Cross-sectional area remains constant until fracture. Fracture surface is flat and perpendicular to tensile axis. / 横截面积在断裂前保持不变。断裂表面平坦且垂直于拉伸轴。 |
| **Energy Storage / 能量储存** | Small area under graph → little energy stored before fracture → sudden, catastrophic failure. / 图下面积小 → 断裂前储存的能量少 → 突然的灾难性失效。 |
| **Comparison with Ductile / 与延性材料比较** | Ductile: yield point, plastic region, necking, large energy storage. Brittle: no yield, no plastic region, no necking, small energy storage. / 延性：屈服点、塑性区域、颈缩、大量能量储存。脆性：无屈服、无塑性区域、无颈缩、少量能量储存。 |
| **Exam Tip / 考试提示** | Always use original area $A_0$ for stress calculation. Sketch graph as a straight line only. State "no plastic region" and "no necking" explicitly. / 始终使用原始面积$A_0$计算应力。将图形绘制为直线。明确说明"无塑性区域"和"无颈缩"。 |
| **Common Mistake / 常见错误** | ❌ Drawing a curve or yield point on brittle graph. ❌ Saying brittle materials show necking. ❌ Confusing "brittle" with "weak". / ❌ 在脆性材料图上绘制曲线或屈服点。❌ 说脆性材料显示颈缩。❌ 混淆"脆性"和"弱"。 |
