# Stress-Strain Graph for a Polymeric Material (Rubber) / 聚合物材料（橡胶）的应力-应变图

---

# 1. Overview / 概述

**English:**
This sub-topic focuses on the unique stress-strain behaviour of **polymeric materials**, specifically **rubber**. Unlike ductile metals (e.g., copper) or brittle materials (e.g., glass), rubber exhibits a highly non-linear, elastic response known as **hyperelasticity**. The key feature is the **hysteresis loop** formed during loading and unloading, which demonstrates significant **energy dissipation** (damping). This behaviour is crucial for understanding the material's use in applications like shock absorbers, seals, and tyres. This leaf node builds on the foundational concepts of [[Stress, Strain and Young Modulus]] and contrasts with the behaviour of [[Stress-Strain Graph for a Ductile Material (Copper)]] and [[Stress-Strain Graph for a Brittle Material (Glass)]].

**中文:**
本子知识点专注于**聚合物材料**，特别是**橡胶**独特的应力-应变行为。与延性金属（如铜）或脆性材料（如玻璃）不同，橡胶表现出一种高度非线性的弹性响应，称为**超弹性**。其关键特征是在加载和卸载过程中形成的**滞后回线**，这证明了显著的**能量耗散**（阻尼）。这种行为对于理解该材料在减震器、密封件和轮胎等应用中的用途至关重要。本叶节点建立在[[应力、应变与杨氏模量]]的基础概念之上，并与[[延性材料（铜）的应力-应变图]]和[[脆性材料（玻璃）的应力-应变图]]形成对比。

| **Polymeric Material** / 聚合物材料 | A material composed of long, chain-like molecules (polymers). | 由长链状分子（聚合物）组成的材料。 | Confusing with simple crystalline solids. |

# 4. Key Concepts Explained / 关键概念详解

## 4.1 Elastic Hysteresis and Energy Dissipation / 弹性滞后与能量耗散

### Explanation / 解释
**English:**
When a rubber band is stretched (loaded), the polymer chains uncoil and align. The stress-strain curve is non-linear. When the load is removed (unloaded), the chains do not immediately re-coil to their original random state; they re-coil more slowly. This means the unloading curve lies **below** the loading curve. The area between the two curves is the **hysteresis loop**. The area under the loading curve represents the **energy stored per unit volume** (work done to stretch it). The area under the unloading curve represents the **energy returned per unit volume**. The area of the hysteresis loop represents the **energy dissipated per unit volume** (usually as heat). This is a key concept in [[Material Selection for Engineering Applications]] for damping.

**中文:**
当橡皮筋被拉伸（加载）时，聚合物链会解开并排列。应力-应变曲线是非线性的。当移除负载（卸载）时，链不会立即重新卷曲到其原始的随机状态；它们重新卷曲得更慢。这意味着卸载曲线位于加载曲线**下方**。两条曲线之间的区域是**滞后回线**。加载曲线下的面积代表**每单位体积储存的能量**（拉伸所做的功）。卸载曲线下的面积代表**每单位体积返回的能量**。滞后回线的面积代表**每单位体积耗散的能量**（通常以热量形式）。这是[[工程应用中的材料选择]]中用于阻尼的关键概念。

### Physical Meaning / 物理意义
**English:**
The hysteresis loop is a direct measure of the material's internal friction. The energy dissipated is not lost; it is converted into internal thermal energy, causing the material to heat up. This is why a rubber band feels warm after being repeatedly stretched and released.

**中文:**
滞后回线是材料内部摩擦的直接度量。耗散的能量并未消失；它被转化为内部热能，导致材料升温。这就是为什么橡皮筋在反复拉伸和释放后会感觉温热。

### Common Misconceptions / 常见误区
- **Misconception:** The hysteresis loop means the material has undergone plastic deformation.
  **Correction:** Rubber is still elastic; it returns to its original shape. The loop is due to the time-dependent re-coiling of polymer chains, not permanent atomic rearrangement.
- **Misconception:** The area under the loading curve is the energy dissipated.
  **Correction:** The area under the loading curve is the total energy input. The dissipated energy is the *difference* between the loading and unloading curve areas.

### Exam Tips / 考试提示
- **EN:** Always sketch the loading curve above the unloading curve. Label the axes correctly (Stress/σ on y-axis, Strain/ε on x-axis). Shade the hysteresis loop and label it "Energy dissipated per unit volume".
- **CN:** 务必画出加载曲线在卸载曲线上方。正确标注坐标轴（应力/σ 在 y 轴，应变/ε 在 x 轴）。将滞后回线涂上阴影并标注为“每单位体积耗散的能量”。

# 5. Essential Equations / 核心公式

## 5.1 Energy Stored per Unit Volume / 每单位体积储存的能量

$$ U_{\text{stored}} = \int_{0}^{\epsilon_{\text{max}}} \sigma_{\text{loading}} \, d\epsilon $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $U_{\text{stored}}$ | Energy stored per unit volume | 每单位体积储存的能量 | J m⁻³ |
| $\sigma_{\text{loading}}$ | Stress during loading | 加载过程中的应力 | Pa (N m⁻²) |
| $\epsilon_{\text{max}}$ | Maximum strain | 最大应变 | (dimensionless) |

**Derivation / 推导:** Work done = Force × displacement. For a material, $W = \int F \, dx$. Dividing by volume $V = A \times L$, we get $U = \frac{W}{V} = \int \frac{F}{A} \frac{dx}{L} = \int \sigma \, d\epsilon$.

**Conditions / 适用条件:** Valid for any material under uniaxial stress. The integral is the area under the loading curve.

## 5.2 Energy Dissipated per Unit Volume / 每单位体积耗散的能量

$$ U_{\text{dissipated}} = \oint \sigma \, d\epsilon = \text{Area of Hysteresis Loop} $$

| Symbol (符号) | Meaning (EN) | Meaning (CN) | Unit (单位) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| $U_{\text{dissipated}}$ | Energy dissipated per unit volume per cycle | 每个循环每单位体积耗散的能量 | J m⁻³ |
| $\oint$ | Closed loop integral | 闭合回线积分 | - |

**Derivation / 推导:** The net work done on the material over a complete cycle is the difference between the work done during loading and the work returned during unloading. This is the area enclosed by the loop.

**Conditions / 适用条件:** Only applies to materials exhibiting elastic hysteresis. The loop must be closed (material returns to zero strain).

# 6. Graphs and Relationships / 图表与关系

## 6.1 Stress-Strain Graph for Rubber / 橡胶的应力-应变图

### Axes / 坐标轴 (EN+CN)
- **Y-axis:** Stress (σ) / 应力 (σ)
- **X-axis:** Strain (ε) / 应变 (ε)

### Shape / 形状 (EN+CN)
- **Loading Curve:** Starts with a low gradient (easy to stretch), then becomes steeper (harder to stretch) as chains align. It is a non-linear, upward-curving line.
- **Unloading Curve:** Lies below the loading curve. It is also non-linear, but with a shallower gradient, indicating less stress is required to maintain a given strain during unloading.
- **Hysteresis Loop:** The closed area between the two curves.

### Gradient Meaning / 斜率含义 (EN+CN)
- The gradient of the stress-strain curve at any point is the **tangent modulus** (a measure of instantaneous stiffness). It is not constant (unlike Young's Modulus for linear materials). The gradient increases with strain.

### Area Meaning / 面积含义 (EN+CN)
- **Area under loading curve:** Energy stored per unit volume (work done on the material).
- **Area under unloading curve:** Energy returned per unit volume (work done by the material).
- **Area of hysteresis loop:** Energy dissipated per unit volume (heat generated).

### Exam Interpretation / 考试解读 (EN+CN)
- **EN:** A large hysteresis loop indicates a material that is good at absorbing and dissipating energy (e.g., for shock absorbers). A small loop indicates a more efficient elastic material (e.g., for a bungee cord that returns most of the energy).
- **CN:** 大的滞后回线表明材料善于吸收和耗散能量（例如，用于减震器）。小的回线表明材料是更高效的弹性材料（例如，用于蹦极绳，能返回大部分能量）。

# 8. Worked Examples / 典型例题

## Example 1: Calculating Energy Dissipated / 计算耗散能量

### Question / 题目
**English:**
A rubber band is stretched to a strain of 2.0. The area under the loading curve on its stress-strain graph is 1.5 × 10⁶ J m⁻³. The area under the unloading curve is 0.9 × 10⁶ J m⁻³. The rubber band has a volume of 5.0 × 10⁻⁷ m³.
(a) Calculate the energy stored in the rubber band at maximum strain.
(b) Calculate the energy dissipated as heat during one cycle.
(c) Calculate the efficiency of the rubber band as an energy returner.

**中文:**
一根橡皮筋被拉伸到应变为 2.0。其应力-应变图上加载曲线下的面积为 1.5 × 10⁶ J m⁻³。卸载曲线下的面积为 0.9 × 10⁶ J m⁻³。橡皮筋的体积为 5.0 × 10⁻⁷ m³。
(a) 计算在最大应变下橡皮筋中储存的能量。
(b) 计算在一个循环中以热量形式耗散的能量。
(c) 计算橡皮筋作为能量返回器的效率。

### Solution / 解答
**(a) Energy stored / 储存的能量:**
$$ E_{\text{stored}} = U_{\text{stored}} \times V = (1.5 \times 10^6 \, \text{J m}^{-3}) \times (5.0 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}^3) = 0.75 \, \text{J} $$

**(b) Energy dissipated / 耗散的能量:**
$$ U_{\text{dissipated}} = \text{Area of hysteresis loop} = 1.5 \times 10^6 - 0.9 \times 10^6 = 0.6 \times 10^6 \, \text{J m}^{-3} $$
$$ E_{\text{dissipated}} = U_{\text{dissipated}} \times V = (0.6 \times 10^6) \times (5.0 \times 10^{-7}) = 0.30 \, \text{J} $$

**(c) Efficiency / 效率:**
$$ \text{Efficiency} = \frac{\text{Energy returned}}{\text{Energy input}} \times 100\% = \frac{0.9 \times 10^6}{1.5 \times 10^6} \times 100\% = 60\% $$

### Final Answer / 最终答案
**Answer:** (a) 0.75 J (b) 0.30 J (c) 60% | **答案：** (a) 0.75 焦耳 (b) 0.30 焦耳 (c) 60%

### Quick Tip / 提示
(EN+CN)
- **EN:** Remember to multiply the energy per unit volume by the actual volume of the material to get the total energy.
- **CN:** 记住要将每单位体积的能量乘以材料的实际体积才能得到总能量。

# 10. Practical Skills Connections / 实验技能链接

**English:**
This sub-topic connects to practical work in several ways:
1.  **Experiment: Stretching a Rubber Band:** Students can perform a simple experiment using a rubber band, a clamp stand, masses, and a ruler. By measuring the extension for increasing and then decreasing loads, they can plot a force-extension graph, which is analogous to the stress-strain graph. They can then calculate the energy stored and dissipated.
2.  **Uncertainties:** The main uncertainties come from measuring the extension (ruler parallax) and the force (mass uncertainty). The cross-sectional area of the rubber band changes significantly during stretching, making stress calculation complex. This is a good point to discuss the limitations of the simple experiment.
3.  **Graph Plotting:** Students must be able to plot the loading and unloading curves on the same axes and identify the hysteresis loop. They should be able to estimate the area of the loop using the "counting squares" method.

**中文:**
本子知识点通过以下几种方式与实验工作相关联：
1.  **实验：拉伸橡皮筋：** 学生可以使用橡皮筋、夹架、砝码和尺子进行一个简单的实验。通过测量增加和减少负载时的伸长量，他们可以绘制出力-伸长量图，这类似于应力-应变图。然后他们可以计算储存和耗散的能量。
2.  **不确定度：** 主要的不确定度来自测量伸长量（尺子视差）和力（砝码不确定度）。橡皮筋的横截面积在拉伸过程中会发生显著变化，使得应力计算变得复杂。这是讨论简单实验局限性的一个好点。
3.  **图表绘制：** 学生必须能够在同一坐标轴上绘制加载和卸载曲线，并识别滞后回线。他们应该能够使用“数方格”法估算回线的面积。
